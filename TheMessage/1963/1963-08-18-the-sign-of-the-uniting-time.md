And coming down the road, I turned on a radio minister, Brother Smith, the colored brother, (did you all hear him this morning? Any of you?) up at, I believe it's Ohio. **2** And talking about Brother Neville, and the message the other day, to this little lady, our sister that just went from us; that was, we all know who it was, it's Sister Weaver. And thinking about a man here, being baptized tonight; she... I baptized her in this pool when I ... they had to bring her here in a wheelchair. She said, "I'm not worthy for a minister to be in my house." She said, "I'm a sinner." But she said, "Sir, I don't want to die like this." And so Grace Weber, up here, took me down there. Then, she could hardly raise her hand up, but she wanted to shake hands with everybody. She just ... something happened to her. And she's been a jump ahead of that cancer, all along. If she ... never died with no cancer, she had a heart attack and killed her. They had her under oxygen; she died in a heart attack.
