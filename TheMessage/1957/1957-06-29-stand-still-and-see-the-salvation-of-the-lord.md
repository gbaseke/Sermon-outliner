# Stand still and see the salvation of the Lord



**1** Let us pray just for a moment. Our Heavenly Father, we thank Thee tonight for the blessed Lord Jesus who has made all these great blessings that we are having the privilege of anticipating in, possible by His great vicarious suffering, death, burial, and resurrection, and the coming of the Holy Spirit. And we pray tonight that You'll bless us together as we wait for further word from Thee. We ask in Jesus' name. Amen.

**2** You be seated. [Someone speaks to Bro. Branham.] Such a privilege to be here again tonight in the Philadelphian Church. That's right. I haven't got too much voice, and a little volume goes a long ways here. My little boy, Joseph, two days ago (wife not knowing she was going to get to come), he developed under this great rainy season that we're having, a sore throat. Well, he's Daddy's boy, you know, so I have him up playing and jumping and playing with him, and, well, I find out that I got a little hoarse on my road coming up here. But the Lord was good to him, and we prayed over a certain matter and asked the Lord if it was His will to do a certain thing, that He would stop the fever immediately. He did not. So, I knew it was God then moving for a decision that I had to make right away. So, I was so thankful.

**7** We are so acquainted with these Scriptures. **8** Now, in this particular scripture that we've just read, it was where a great crisis had come. The people were all nervous, upset, because there was a great invading army.
