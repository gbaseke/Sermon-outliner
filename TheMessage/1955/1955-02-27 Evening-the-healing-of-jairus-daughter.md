And now, I'm just a little bit tired, preached twice today, hard, and I'm just a little bit worn out. I was telling some of my brethren a few moments ago, on preaching this afternoon I'm not too much of a preacher, but I hope you got what I meant, that I'm a great believer in the blood.

**2** I think without the shedding of blood there's no remission of sins. And I just can't stress that enough. And my motive this afternoon, in speaking of the Lamb, was the lamb under the old sacrificial law, I thought I'd better kind of straighten it just a little bit ... or, not straighten it, but just bring it down to where you'd see it, and maybe it wouldn't be misunderstood; that under the old law, when a lamb, a burnt offering was made, and the lamb died, the innocent substitute for the guilty sinner, the life that went out of the lamb's blood cell could not return back to the man. So he come out of the church, the holy place, with the same desire to sin that he had when he went in.
