The preacher's raw_notes follow a fixed template with sections: Opening Question, Key Scriptures, Main Theme & Ideas, Types & Shadows, People to Study, Points (rough, often a numbered list of questions), Quotes/Illustrations, Closing Thought, Song Ideas.

Rule: He frequently leaves fields blank (Opening Question, Theme, Closing Thought, Song Ideas) and expects the agent to supply them from the material. The "Points (rough)" list is usually a set of study questions, not finished points — synthesize them into developed sermon points.

**Why:** On the 2026-06-03 "Is it God's plan?" sermon, nearly every prose field was empty and points were raw questions; the agent had to derive the opening question, theme, and hymn.

**How to apply:** Treat blank fields as assignments, not omissions. Also watch for scripture-reference typos — same sermon listed "Jeremiah 19:1-11" in the header but "Jeremiah 29:1-11" in Key Scriptures; the title/theme made clear Jeremiah 29 was intended. When references conflict, reason from the title + theme + study questions, build on the correct one, and flag the discrepancy in Preacher's Reserve for confirmation.
