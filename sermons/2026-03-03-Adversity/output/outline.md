## THIS HOUR — PROPHETIC ANCHOR

The Bride of Christ in this end-time hour is not exempt from storms — she is walking through them. The world is shaking, denominations are crumbling, and every wind that blows seems contrary. But this is not the hour for the Bride to panic; it is the hour for her to keep rowing toward the other side, knowing that He who sent her into the ship is watching from the high place and will come to her in the fourth watch. God is not punishing His Bride with adversity — He is positioning her for the greatest outpouring and the greatest reward this world has ever seen. Ruth gleaned in a hard field and came home with more than she could carry. The disciples fought the storm and ended up exactly where Jesus told them to go. The Bride, standing faithful through her trials, shall be caught up to meet her Kinsman Redeemer in the air. Do not despise the storm — it is your passage to the other shore.

## INTRODUCTION — SETTING THE TYPE

### The Human Scene (Humanistic)

It was an ordinary hillside by the Sea of Galilee — and then Jesus asked twelve ordinary men a question that exposed every human limitation. Five thousand people were hungry. The sun was going down. The disciples looked at what little they had — five biscuits and two small fish — and the arithmetic was all wrong. You could feel it in the pit of your stomach, that sinking sensation when the need is enormous and the resource in your hands is laughably small. The disciples had just finished watching Jesus feed thousands with a little boy's lunch. And now the very same Lord was sending them across the water — into a night wind, into a storm, into the fourth watch. They were obedient. They got in the ship. And the wind came anyway.

Perhaps there is someone here today who got in the ship in obedience. You followed where He said to go. You trusted the Word. And the storm came anyway. This message is for you.

### The Type Established

- **Shadow (OT):** Ruth the Moabite — a Gentile widow, penniless, a foreigner with no inheritance, walking through poverty and grief with loyalty to the God of Israel
- **Substance (NT/End-time):** The Gentile Bride of Christ — called out of the world, separated, pressing through adversity, remaining faithful in the gleaning field of this last age, awaiting her Kinsman Redeemer
- **Branham on this type:**
  > "Naomi, now, the type of the Jewish church. Ruth, the type of the Gentile church. Notice. And now, here she comes back, see. Ruth, being a Gentile, heathen worshipper, she looked back and she said, 'I will go with you. Where you live, I'll live. Where you die, I'll die. Where you are buried, I'll be buried. Let your God be my God.' That's the Gentile church accepting salvation through Jesus Christ our Lord."
  > *— 1954-04-04 · God has a Provided Way · Para. 55*

### Person Profile — Ruth

- **Visible need:** A widow with no inheritance, gleaning behind reapers in a foreign field — poor, exposed, laboring for daily bread
- **Invisible need:** She needed a Kinsman Redeemer. She needed Boaz. She needed someone with the right, the power, and the will to redeem all that was lost
- **Motive:** Love and loyalty to Naomi — she would not let her mother go back to Bethlehem alone
- **Intention:** She came to glean enough to survive — she expected little
- **What God saw:** A Bride. A vessel of total surrender walking through hardship toward her appointed destiny. When the harvest was done and the field was empty, Boaz said: I will take Ruth. The difficult field was never the end — it was the pathway to the threshing floor

## POINT 1 — EVERY STORM HAS A SENDER

**Key Scripture:** "And straightway Jesus constrained his disciples to get into a ship, and to go before him to the other side." — Mark 6:45

### The Human Need (Humanistic)

- No one steps voluntarily into a storm. The disciples did not ask for the wind and the waves — they were following orders
- When trouble arrives, the first question we ask is always: "How did I get here?" And often the answer is: you obeyed. You did what God told you to do. And the storm came anyway
- This is the hardest place to stand — in the middle of a storm you were sent into

### Divine Revelation

**The Word says:**
> "And he saw them toiling in rowing; for the wind was contrary unto them: and about the fourth watch of the night he cometh unto them, walking on the sea." — Mark 6:48

**The type unfolds:**
Ruth did not go to Bethlehem because the journey was easy. She went because loyalty to Naomi and commitment to Naomi's God demanded it. The road from Moab to Bethlehem was dusty, long, and uncertain. She had no guarantee of a warm reception in a foreign land. But the type is plain: the difficult journey was the ordained path to the abundance that awaited her in the field of Boaz. The disciples' storm was not an accident — it was the appointed road to the other shore. The storm did not abort the mission; it was part of it.

**From the Message:**
> "The Christian experience is based solemnly and wholly upon rest... No matter what comes or goes, nothing can touch us without it comes over the Shepherd. He has to permit it. The Bible said, 'All things work together for good to them that love the Lord.' Has to come over the Shepherd first. Said, 'I am the door. No man can come in without Me except...' Let me tell you that when God permits anything, it's working for your good."
> *— 1956-02-12 · Fellowship · Para. 31*

**Sub-points:**
- Jesus sent them into the storm — He was not absent from the plan, He was interceding on the mountain while they rowed (Mark 6:46)
- The storm cannot touch a child of God without passing through the hand of the Shepherd first
- Whatever difficulty you are in today — God permitted it. And if God permitted it, it is working for you, not against you

### THUS SAITH THE LORD — The Vision

The storm you are in right now did not catch God off guard. Before the winds rose, before the oars broke, before the water came over the side of your life — He was already on the mountain praying for you, and He was already beginning to walk across the water to reach you. Your trial has a Sender and it has a Stopper. Hold fast, child of God. The other shore is real and He is coming.

## POINT 2 — TROUBLE IS THE TRAINING GROUND

**Key Scripture:** "And we know that all things work together for good to them that love God, to them who are the called according to his purpose." — Romans 8:28

### The Human Need (Humanistic)

- No athlete builds strength without resistance. No muscle grows without being stressed. No character is formed without pressure
- The disciples were seasoned fishermen — they knew the Sea of Galilee. But pulling on oars against a howling wind all night, from evening until the fourth watch (3 to 6 in the morning), built something in them that calm water never could
- Ruth gleaning behind hired reapers under the hot Palestinian sun, day after day, was not just survival — it was the preparation of a bride. Boaz watched her faithfulness before he ever spoke to her at the threshing floor

### Divine Revelation

**The Word says:**
> "As an eagle stirreth up her nest, fluttereth over her young, spreadeth abroad her wings, taketh them, beareth them on her wings." — Deuteronomy 32:11

**The type unfolds:**
The mother eagle builds the nest with thorns buried beneath the soft feathers and down. When the eaglets are ready to fly but too comfortable to try, she stirs the nest — the thorns come up through the soft lining, and the young eagles are forced out of their comfort and toward the sky. What looks like cruelty is actually the eagle's greatest act of love. God stirs your nest sometimes. The trouble, the pressure, the uncomfortable season — He is not destroying you. He is making you fly.

**From the Message:**
> "Could you imagine Job enjoying being broke out with boils, and all these things gone, losing all of his wealth and his children and everything? It wasn't very easy. But God was proving to Satan that He had somebody He could put confidence in. Maybe He's trying to do the same thing in your case..."
> *— 1963-01-17 · Awakening Jesus · Para. 53*

**Sub-points:**
- Your trial is God's testimony. He is proving something to the enemy: "I have somebody I can put confidence in"
- Grit — the willingness to keep rowing when the oars are heavy and the night is long — is more precious to God than talent that never faced the storm
- Ruth's faithfulness in the hard field made her beautiful to Boaz. Your faithfulness in your hard season is making you beautiful to your Lord

### THUS SAITH THE LORD — The Vision

God is not looking for people who never faced a storm. He is looking for people who kept rowing in one. The Bride of this hour is being developed, deepened, and strengthened for the hour of the Rapture. Every adversity you have pressed through is proof of your election. You are not going under — you are going over. Keep rowing.

## POINT 3 — HE IS WATCHING FROM THE HIGH PLACE

**Key Scripture:** "And he saw them toiling in rowing; for the wind was contrary unto them." — Mark 6:48

### The Human Need (Humanistic)

- One of the deepest pains a person can feel in trouble is the fear that God has forgotten them — that no one sees, no one cares, no one is coming
- The disciples must have felt this. They had left Him on the shore hours ago. They could not see the mountain through the dark and the spray. He seemed far away. The night was long. And no relief came — not the first watch, not the second, not the third
- Have you been rowing since sunset? Has your fourth watch not come yet?

### Divine Revelation

**The Word says:**
> "He careth for you." — 1 Peter 5:7

**The type unfolds:**
The Scripture says He "saw them toiling." He had climbed the highest mountain available so He could watch them all the way across the sea. He had not abandoned them — He had elevated Himself to keep them in view. And at the precise moment of their greatest need, He came. Not a minute early — but never a minute late. The type in Ruth mirrors this exactly: Boaz saw Ruth gleaning in the field. He knew who she was before she knew who he was. He had already told his reapers to leave extra grain for her on purpose. The provision was arranged in advance; she just had not found it yet.

**From the Message:**
> "Jesus had not left them... He climbed up the highest mountain there was, so He could watch them all the way across the ocean. And when He seen them in trouble, He come walking to them on the sea. He hasn't left us. He proves it now, He hasn't left us. He never climbed the highest mountain; He climbed Calvary, and kept on climbing till He come into the presence of God on the throne in glory. He climbed so high He could see the universe from His great throne. His eye is on the sparrow, and I know He watches me. He watches you."
> *— 1960-07-17 · Be not afraid · Para. 69*

**Sub-points:**
- He is not absent — He is elevated. From Calvary to the throne, He sees every stroke of every oar in every storm
- Your name is known by the Lord of the harvest just as Ruth's name was known by Boaz. He has already given orders concerning you
- The fourth watch always comes. God is never late

### THUS SAITH THE LORD — The Vision

Bride of Christ, He sees you. He sees you toiling. He sees the bills, the sickness, the loneliness, the long night. He has climbed so high He sees your situation from the throne of the universe. And He is coming — walking on the very waves that frighten you. Before this service is over, hear Him say to your situation: "Be of good cheer; it is I; be not afraid."

## POINT 4 — SEE THE IMPOSSIBLE AS POSSIBLE — HE IS THE LORD OF CREATION

**Key Scripture:** "And he commanded the multitude to sit down on the grass, and took the five loaves, and the two fishes, and looking up to heaven, he blessed, and brake, and gave the loaves to his disciples, and the disciples to the multitude." — Matthew 14:19

### The Human Need (Humanistic)

- The disciples looked at the crowd and saw an impossible problem: five thousand hungry people and not enough food to feed them
- They brought their arithmetic to Jesus: "We have here but five loaves and two fishes" — and they meant it as a statement of defeat
- This is the posture of unbelief: adding up what you have, comparing it to what you need, and concluding that God cannot work. Jesus had a completely different posture. He looked at those five biscuits and two fish — and He looked up to heaven, not down at the lack

### Divine Revelation

**The Word says:**
> "And they did all eat, and were filled: and they took up of the fragments that remained twelve baskets full." — Matthew 14:20

**The type unfolds:**
The feeding of the five thousand is not merely a miracle — it is a revelation of who Jesus is. He is the Creator God who made bread fall from heaven in the wilderness for forty years. He does not need a pantry — He is the source. What is impossible in your hands becomes abundant in His. The disciples started with barely enough for one boy's lunch. They ended with twelve basketfuls of leftovers — one for each disciple who had doubted. God does not give you just enough to survive. He gives you an abundance beyond what you brought to Him.

**From the Message:**
> "How God can multiply what we put in His hands. It was just enough food in there to feed that one little boy his dinner. But, when it was in the hands of Jesus, it could feed five thousand, and take up several baskets full of pieces."
> *— 1960-07-20 · Be not afraid, it is I · Para. 16*

**Sub-points:**
- The disciples asked Jesus to send the crowd away — their solution was retreat. Jesus said: you feed them. He involves us in the miracle by putting the impossible requirement back in our hands first
- What you have is not the measure of what He can do. Five biscuits and two fish in the hands of the Creator is more than enough
- Not just blessed — an abundance of blessing. Twelve baskets full. God's arithmetic always runs over

### THUS SAITH THE LORD — The Vision

Whatever you have placed in His hands — whatever little seems insufficient — He has taken it, looked up to heaven, blessed it, and broken it. The baskets are coming back to you full. Not the scarcity the enemy told you to expect, but the overflow that the Word has promised. Stop counting what you have. Start looking at who has your offering in His hands.

## POINT 5 — IN THE DARKEST HOUR, THEN JESUS COMES

**Key Scripture:** "And in the fourth watch of the night Jesus went unto them, walking on the sea. And when the disciples saw him walking on the sea, they were troubled, saying, It is a spirit; and they cried out for fear. But straightway Jesus spake unto them, saying, Be of good cheer; it is I; be not afraid." — Matthew 14:25-27

### The Human Need (Humanistic)

- The fourth watch of the night is three to six in the morning — the darkest, coldest, most exhausting part of the night
- The disciples had been rowing since the evening. Nine hours of fighting the wind. They were at their limit. The oars were broken. The ship was water-logged. The sails were gone. They were holding each other
- And then they saw something coming — and they were afraid of the very thing that was coming to save them

### Divine Revelation

**The Word says:**
> "And they all ate, and were filled: and they took up of the fragments that remained twelve baskets full." — Matthew 14:20
> "And immediately the wind ceased: and they were sore amazed in themselves beyond measure." — Mark 6:51

**The type unfolds:**
Ruth came to the threshing floor at night. Naomi's instruction was careful and specific: wait until the work is done, until Boaz has eaten and drunk, until he has laid down — then go, and uncover his feet, and lie down. The blessing came at midnight. Boaz woke at midnight and found his Bride. The fullness of redemption came in the dark, quiet hour when all the day's labor was done. The Bride of Christ is living in the fourth watch. The night has been long. But the Lord of the harvest is about to rise, and when He does, He will say: I know who you are.

**From the Message:**
> "Right at the last moment, the darkest hour, Jesus comes along. And He come walking on the water... What looks scary... their boat was sinking, their oars was gone, and they was at the mercy of the devil on the sea, and a storm twisting them. And the only hope they could have was coming to them."
> *— 1959-08-11 · It is I, be not afraid · Para. 27*

**Sub-points:**
- The disciples were afraid of the only thing that could save them — this is exactly what happens today. The world is afraid of the supernatural move of the Holy Spirit because it looks unusual, like something spooky
- He came to them walking ON the storm — not around it. He used the very thing the enemy intended to drown them as the road He walked to reach them
- "Be of good cheer." The Greek word means: take courage, be bold. He was not scolding their fear — He was replacing it with His presence

### THUS SAITH THE LORD — The Vision

The very thing the enemy sent to stop you, God is using as His highway to get to you. The storm that was meant to drown the Bride will be the stage on which the supernatural power of this end-time Message walks in and says, "It is I — be not afraid." The fourth watch is almost over. He is already coming.

## POINT 6 — NOT JUST BLESSED, BUT AN ABUNDANCE OF BLESSING

**Key Scripture:** "Now unto him that is able to do exceeding abundantly above all that we ask or think, according to the power that worketh in us." — Ephesians 3:20

### The Human Need (Humanistic)

- After a season of adversity, the tendency is to lower our expectations. We have been knocked down so often that we begin to think small: "Lord, just let me survive this." "Lord, just give me enough." "Lord, I am not asking for much."
- But survival is not the destination — the other shore is. The disciples were not fighting the storm to survive; they were fighting it to get to the other side, where a ministry waited, where a demoniac would be set free
- Ruth was not gleaning to barely survive; she was on the road to the threshing floor, to the full redemption of all that Naomi lost, to becoming the great-grandmother of King David

### Divine Revelation

**The Word says:**
> "And he said unto them, Come ye yourselves apart into a desert place, and rest a while." — Mark 6:31
> "And they took up twelve baskets full of the fragments, and of the fishes." — Mark 6:43

**The type unfolds:**
When Boaz redeemed the estate and took Ruth as his bride, he did not merely restore what Naomi had lost — he added to it. Ruth came back from gleaning with more than she expected every single evening. Boaz had commanded his reapers: "Let fall also some of the handfuls of purpose for her, and leave them, that she may glean them." The abundance was pre-arranged by the Lord of the harvest on behalf of the Bride. The fragments after the five thousand were twelve baskets full — one for every doubting disciple. God does not give you what you deserve; He gives you what His grace has pre-arranged.

**From the Message:**
> "All things work together for good. You just can't lose when you become a Christian, just can't lose. Just trust in Him, and whatever the way goes, just keep your sails set to His Spirit. He'll guide you into the harbor. It'll all be all right."
> *— 1955-02-23 · God's Servant Job · Para. 10*

**Sub-points:**
- The disciples made it to the other shore — the storm did not prevent the destination
- The Bride of Christ is heading to a destination the storm cannot stop: the Rapture, the Wedding Supper, the Millennium, the New Jerusalem
- The fragments of your obedience — the little you brought and placed in His hands — He has blessed. You will gather more than you brought. You will come home from the hard field full

### THUS SAITH THE LORD — The Vision

The same God who kept the manna coming for forty years in the wilderness, who made the widow's oil not run out, who made five biscuits feed five thousand and still had twelve baskets left over — that God is your God. You came into this season with barely enough. You are going out with an abundance. Not just blessed — abundantly above all you could ask or think.

## CONCLUSION

### The Epiphany — The Question Answered

> Do all things truly work together for good to those who love the Lord — even the storms, the hunger, the impossible situations?
>
> Yes. The storm was sent by the same Lord who walked on it. The hunger exposed the miracle that was waiting. The hard field was the road to the threshing floor of redemption. Every adversity you have faced, God permitted. Every adversity He permitted, He has been working. And what He works together — always comes out for good.

### Summary

- **Point 1 — Every Storm Has a Sender:** Nothing touches the child of God without passing through the Shepherd's hand first; the trial was permitted by the one who loves you most.
- **Point 2 — Trouble Is the Training Ground:** God stirs the nest to make you fly; Job's boils, Ruth's hard field, and the disciples' broken oars were all developing a Bride.
- **Point 3 — He Is Watching from the High Place:** He climbed Calvary and ascended the throne so He could see you in every storm; His eye has never left you.
- **Point 4 — See the Impossible as Possible:** Five biscuits and two fish in the Creator's hands becomes twelve baskets full; what you give Him, He multiplies beyond your need.
- **Point 5 — In the Darkest Hour, Then Jesus Comes:** The fourth watch is the hour of His arrival; He walks on the very storm He permitted, and His word to you is "Be of good cheer — it is I."
- **Point 6 — Not Just Blessed, but an Abundance:** God's purpose is not survival — it is the other shore, the harvest field, the threshing floor, and the wedding; you are headed somewhere storms cannot stop.

> **The heart of this message:** God is not working against you in your storm — He has been working all things together for your good all along, and the other shore is closer than you think.

### A Closing Hymn

**Suggested hymn:** "His Eye Is on the Sparrow"
**Why:** This hymn captures the central pastoral truth of the sermon — that no matter how dark the night or violent the storm, the Lord sees every disciple toiling in rowing, every gleaner bent low in the hard field, and His eye has never left His Bride.
