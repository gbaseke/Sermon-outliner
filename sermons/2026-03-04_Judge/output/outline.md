## THIS HOUR -- PROPHETIC ANCHOR

We are living in the hour when Jesus Christ has stepped forth from the mercy seat. The wig of white upon the head of the Son of Man in the Revelation reveals Him not as Lamb, not as Priest, but as the Supreme Judge of all the earth. The Bride of Christ in this hour is not a people waiting for judgement -- she has already been judged in Him at Calvary. She stands free, clothed in His righteousness, while the world rushes headlong toward the great white throne. This message is both a comfort and a warning: comfort to the Bride who rests in Christ, and a warning to every soul that has heard the Word and not yet yielded to it. The countdown has begun.

---

## INTRODUCTION -- SETTING THE TYPE

**Tone:** Warm and pastoral -- open the heart before opening the Word.

### The Human Scene (Humanistic)

Every human being carries an awareness of judgement. Even the smallest child, caught in wrongdoing, feels that moment of exposure -- that dread of being found out. We carry it all our lives. Something inside us knows: there is a reckoning. The world tries to drown it out with entertainment, with philosophy, with religion -- but the question presses in: "What happens when I stand before God?" It is the question behind every restless night, every nagging conscience, every unanswered prayer. Beloved, you are not here tonight by accident. There is something in your heart that needs to know -- not just that judgement is coming, but *who* is doing the judging, and where you stand before Him.

### The Type Established

- **Shadow (OT):** The three offices of Christ in Israel -- Prophet, Priest, and King. In the Old Testament, these were always separate: Moses the prophet, Aaron the priest, David the king. No man held all three at once.
- **Substance (NT/End-time):** Jesus Christ fulfills all three offices. On earth He was the Prophet; now in glory He is the Priest (our Intercessor); and when He returns He is the King and Judge. In this present hour He has moved from the mercy seat to the judgement seat -- the white wig of Supreme Deity is upon Him.
- **Branham on this type:**
  > "On earth He was a prophet; in glory He's a priest; coming, He's a king. Oh, I like that! Prophet, faithful witness of the Word; priest, with his own blood before God; king, king of saints."
  > *-- 1960-12-04 -- Church Ages, part 1: Revelation Chapter 1 -- p77*

### Person Profile: Jesus Christ -- The Just Judge

- **Visible need:** The world needs someone to set all things right -- a judge who cannot be bribed, cannot be fooled, cannot be partial.
- **Invisible need:** Man needs more than justice -- he needs mercy *before* the gavel falls.
- **Motive:** "For God sent not his Son into the world to condemn the world; but that the world through him might be saved" (John 3:17).
- **Intention:** To offer every soul a way out *before* the judgement seat is set.
- **What God saw:** A world lost in sin, and a Lamb slain before the foundation of the world -- judgement and mercy held in the same nail-scarred hand.

## POINT 1 -- ALL JUDGEMENT IS COMMITTED UNTO THE SON

**Key Scripture:** "For the Father judgeth no man, but hath committed all judgment unto the Son." -- John 5:22

### The Human Need (Humanistic)

- People fear a faceless, unknowable judge -- some cosmic force that cares nothing for their suffering.
- Many carry a distorted image of God: angry, distant, waiting to destroy them for the smallest failure.
- The human heart cries out: "Who is judging me? Does He know what I have been through?"

### Divine Revelation

**The Word says:**
> "For the Father judgeth no man, but hath committed all judgment unto the Son: That all men should honour the Son, even as they honour the Father." -- John 5:22-23

**The type unfolds:**
In the Old Testament, the judges of Israel were raised up by God to deliver His people. But every earthly judge was limited -- they could not see the heart. When Israel asked for a king to judge them "like all the nations" (1 Samuel 8:5), they were rejecting the perfect judgement of God for the imperfect judgement of man. But in Christ, God gave the world a Judge who is both God and man -- One who has walked in our shoes, felt our pain, been tempted in all points like as we are, yet without sin. He is qualified to judge because He has *lived* among us.

**From the Message:**
> "You believe He's a judge? Let's read St. John 5:22 right quick, and we'll see whether He's a judge or not. St. John 5 and 22: For the Father judges no man, but has committed all judgment unto the Son: That right? He is the judge, the supreme judge. And John shows that He wasn't in the day of his prophecy as a prophet; neither was He plumb out into the days of his kingship; but He was in the day of the Lord as a judge."
> *-- 1960-12-04 -- Church Ages, part 2: The Patmos Vision -- p89*

**Sub-points:**
- A. Jesus is not a distant judge -- He is the One who bled on Calvary for you. The same hand that holds the gavel is the hand that was pierced for your sins.
- B. The Father did this so that "all men should honour the Son." Judgement is meant to point you to Jesus, not away from Him.
- C. God is not partial -- He is no respecter of persons (Acts 10:34). Every soul will be measured by the same standard: the Word of God.

### THUS SAITH THE LORD -- The Vision

Bride of Christ, hear me tonight. Your Judge is not a stranger. He is the Bridegroom. He is the One who laid down His life for you. In this hour, when the world trembles at what is coming, you can look into the face of the Judge and see the face of the One who loves you. He has wigged Himself in supreme authority -- not to condemn His Bride, but to vindicate her. Every judgement He carries out is righteous, and every mercy He extends is just. The countdown is running, and the Judge is at the door.

## POINT 2 -- THE BELIEVER HAS ALREADY BEEN JUDGED

**Key Scripture:** "Verily, verily, I say unto you, He that heareth my word, and believeth on him that sent me, hath everlasting life, and shall not come into condemnation; but is passed from death unto life." -- John 5:24

### The Human Need (Humanistic)

- Christians still wrestle with fear: "Will God show all my faults to everyone? Will He embarrass me before the world?"
- Many believers live under a cloud of shame, as though the blood of Jesus were not enough.
- The devil whispers, "You are not good enough to escape judgement." And the flesh agrees.

### Divine Revelation

**The Word says:**
> "There is therefore now no condemnation to them which are in Christ Jesus, who walk not after the flesh, but after the Spirit." -- Romans 8:1

**The type unfolds:**
In Exodus 12, the blood of the Passover lamb was placed on the doorpost -- and when the death angel passed through Egypt, he could not touch those who were under the blood. The blood was not for God to see whether you were worthy; it was for God to see whether you were *covered*. The lamb had already died in your place. The judgement had already fallen -- on the lamb. So it is with the believer in Christ: the Lamb of God has already taken your judgement. God cannot justly judge you again, for your judgement was settled at Calvary.

**From the Message:**
> "Then he's already judged. You don't have to worry about judgment. As I turn and look at that little crucifix, I realize that that represents His body. And now that body has already been judged. God can't justly judge it again, for it's already judged. God struck the judgments of death upon that body. And as long as I can find a way to get hid in that body! His judgment was struck for me and for you. We are free!"
> *-- 1957-04-19 -- Perfection -- p25*

**Sub-points:**
- A. "Shall not come into condemnation" -- this is not a hope, it is a settled fact. Jesus said "Verily, verily" -- Amen, amen -- it is doubly certain.
- B. The question is not "Is Jesus trying to shame people?" No! He bore our shame on the cross so that we would never have to carry it again. "He that believeth on him is not condemned" (John 3:18).
- C. How do you get "into Christ"? "For by one Spirit are we all baptized into one body" (1 Corinthians 12:13). Not by joining a church, not by shaking a hand, but by the baptism of the Holy Ghost -- placed into the body that has already been judged.

**From the Message:**
> "No condemnation to them which has joined church. No condemnation to them which has shook hands. No condemnation to them which has been baptized, unh-uh. 'No condemnation to them which are in Christ Jesus.' How do you get into it? 'By one Spirit we're all baptized into one body.'"
> *-- 1953-11-22 -- The resurrection of Lazarus -- p18*

### THUS SAITH THE LORD -- The Vision

Saints of God, lift up your heads. Jesus is not trying to shame you. He is not compiling a list of your failures to parade before the universe. Your sins were nailed to the cross and buried in the sea of His forgetfulness. You have *passed* -- past tense -- from death unto life. The countdown to judgement is not a countdown to your destruction; it is a countdown to your vindication. When the world stands trembling, the Bride will stand free, because her judgement was settled two thousand years ago at Calvary.

## POINT 3 -- THE CATEGORIES OF JUDGEMENT: NOT ALL STAND IN THE SAME COURT

**Key Scripture:** "For we must all appear before the judgment seat of Christ; that every one may receive the things done in his body, according to that he hath done, whether it be good or bad." -- 2 Corinthians 5:10

### The Human Need (Humanistic)

- People want to know: "Is this judgement the same for everybody? What about good people who never heard the gospel? What about the foolish virgins?"
- There is genuine confusion about the different judgements in scripture -- the judgement seat of Christ, the sheep-and-goat separation, the great white throne.
- Some feel paralyzed: "If everyone is judged, what is the point of trying?"

### Divine Revelation

**The Word says:**
> "Blessed and holy is he that hath part in the first resurrection: on such the second death hath no power, but they shall be priests of God and of Christ, and shall reign with him a thousand years." -- Revelation 20:6

**The type unfolds:**
In the earthly court system, there are different courts for different cases. A citizen in good standing does not appear in criminal court. The Bride of Christ, being in the first resurrection, does not appear at the great white throne -- she is *with* Christ at that judgement, not *before* Him. The foolish virgins -- those who had the oil in their lamps but not enough, who were good and moral but missed the fullness of the Spirit -- they are the sheep at His right hand (Matthew 25:33-34). And the wicked, those who rejected the Word entirely, are the goats on the left. Three groups, three outcomes.

**From the Message:**
> "The sleeping virgin will be saved, of course. She'll be saved at the judgment. She'll never be the bride, but she is a saved group of people that will come in the judgment, that will not be included in the bride. But as long as they are virgins, they are before God, see. They are to be saved. He separates... They'll be the sheep on His right side, and the unsaved will be the goats on His left at the great white throne judgment."
> *-- 1961-10-15 -- Questions and Answers -- p91*

**Sub-points:**
- A. The Bride: Raptured, caught up, already judged in Christ. She does not face the great white throne -- she sits *with* the Judge. "Know ye not that the saints shall judge the earth?" (1 Corinthians 6:2).
- B. The foolish virgins and the righteous nations: Good people, moral people, church-going people -- but they missed the filling of the Holy Ghost. They are saved through the tribulation period and raised in the general resurrection.
- C. The wicked: Those who heard the Word and rejected it. Those whose names were never in the Lamb's Book of Life, or whose names were blotted out by deliberate unbelief.

**From the Message:**
> "He leaves the throne to be an intercessor as a slain Lamb to be a Lion, King, to bring the world to judgment who has rejected his message. He's not a mediator... Then He become not a lamb, but a lion. King! And He calls for his queen to come stand by his side. 'Know ye not the saints shall judge the earth?'"
> *-- 1963-03-17 -- Seals 2: Breach between the Church Ages and the Seals -- p132*

### THUS SAITH THE LORD -- The Vision

Where do you stand tonight? Not every person will face the same court. The Bride has a different appointment -- not before the throne, but beside the throne. In this hour God is calling out a people, sealing them by His Spirit, placing them into the Body. The countdown is not just to judgement -- it is to position. Where you end up on that Day depends on what you do with Jesus Christ *right now*, in this present hour.

## POINT 4 -- FOUR SOULS AT THE CROSSROADS: WHAT DETERMINED THEIR JUDGEMENT

**Key Scripture:** "He that rejecteth me, and receiveth not my words, hath one that judgeth him: the word that I have spoken, the same shall judge him in the last day." -- John 12:48

### The Human Need (Humanistic)

- We all know people who were so close to the truth and turned away. We know others who seemed beyond hope and were saved at the last moment.
- The question burns: "What makes the difference? Is God choosing favorites?"
- Four lives from scripture answer this question with devastating clarity.

### Divine Revelation

**The Word says:**
> "He that believeth on him is not condemned: but he that believeth not is condemned already, because he hath not believed in the name of the only begotten Son of God." -- John 3:18

**The type unfolds:**
Consider four souls who stood at the crossroads of judgement:

**a) Judas Iscariot** -- He walked with Jesus for three and a half years. He cast out devils. His name was written in the Book of Life. But he sold the Word for thirty pieces of silver. He had light, he had opportunity, he had calling -- and he traded it all for the world.

- **Visible need:** He seemed to need nothing -- he was in the inner circle.
- **Invisible need:** His heart was never surrendered. He followed Jesus for what he could get, not for who Jesus was.
- **Motive:** Self-interest. He kept the money bag (John 12:6).
- **Intention:** To profit from the ministry without being changed by the Word.
- **What God saw:** A son of perdition, foreknown, whose betrayal would fulfill prophecy -- but whose choice was entirely his own.

**From the Message:**
> "And Judas Iscariot professed to be a believer, and he had his name written among them on the book of life. Is that right? He claimed to have eternal life. And his name was put on the book of life. So he has to... Why, he has to be judged then to see why did he condemn the Word of life then, sell out for thirty pieces of silver. Some sells out to be a Methodist, Baptist or whatmore."
> *-- 1964-08-30 -- Questions and Answers, Part 3 -- p156*

**b) The rich young ruler** -- He came running to Jesus. He had kept the commandments from his youth. But when Jesus said, "Sell all that thou hast, and follow me" -- he turned away grieved, for he had great possessions (Mark 10:21-22).

- **Visible need:** None apparent -- he was wealthy, moral, and religious.
- **Invisible need:** His heart was chained to his possessions. He wanted eternal life without total surrender.
- **Motive:** Sincerity -- he genuinely wanted to know the way.
- **Intention:** To add eternal life to what he already had, without giving anything up.
- **What God saw:** A man who loved his comfort more than his Christ.

**From the Message:**
> "Now compare this church age with that rich young ruler. Compare them. Now, didn't Jesus say the Laodicea church was rich, increased in goods, had need for nothing? Isn't that the condition of the church today? And yet Christ has unfolded Himself..."
> *-- 1963-08-03 -- Investments -- p47*

**c) The thief on the cross** -- He had nothing to offer. No good works, no religious standing, no moral record. But in his final hour, he turned to Jesus and said, "Lord, remember me when thou comest into thy kingdom" (Luke 23:42). And Jesus answered, "To day shalt thou be with me in paradise."

- **Visible need:** He was dying -- condemned by the law of man.
- **Invisible need:** His soul was crying out for mercy, and he recognized in Christ something that the priests and Pharisees had missed.
- **Motive:** Desperation mixed with genuine faith -- he had nothing left to lose and nothing left to offer.
- **Intention:** Simply to be remembered.
- **What God saw:** A predestinated seed that blossomed at the last possible moment. Faith -- however small, however late -- that took God at His Word.

**d) The rich man and Lazarus** (Luke 16:19-31) -- The rich man lived in luxury every day. Lazarus lay at his gate, covered in sores. Both died. Lazarus was carried to Abraham's bosom; the rich man lifted up his eyes in hell.

- **Visible need:** The rich man had no visible need -- he had everything the world could offer.
- **Invisible need:** He had religion (he called Abraham "Father") but he had no investment in Christ.
- **Motive:** Self-satisfaction. His whole life was lived for himself.
- **What God saw:** A man who sat under the light and did nothing with it.

**From the Message:**
> "And remember, he still wanted to hold his profession in hell. He seen Lazarus, in the bosoms of Abraham, and he said, 'Father Abraham, send Lazarus down here.' Still calling him 'father.' See? He took his knowledge, and went to an intellectual church. When the Light struck him, he turned It down."
> *-- 1963-06-30 -- Is your life worthy of the Gospel? -- p120*

**Sub-points:**
- A. Judas had light, opportunity, and calling -- and he rejected all three. His judgement will be according to what he was given.
- B. The rich young ruler had sincerity but no surrender. Close to the Kingdom is not in the Kingdom.
- C. The thief had nothing but faith -- and faith was all that was required.
- D. The rich man had religion without reality. He called Abraham "Father" but he never knew the God of Abraham.

### THUS SAITH THE LORD -- The Vision

Beloved, look at these four souls and see yourself honestly before God tonight. The question is never "How much did you have?" but "What did you do with what you were given?" God is measuring three things: How much light were you exposed to? How much opportunity did He place before you -- gifts, talents, resources, calling? And what did you do with it? The thief had one moment of light and he seized it. Judas had three and a half years of light and he sold it. The countdown to judgement is not about fairness -- it is about faithfulness.

## POINT 5 -- THE STANDARD OF JUDGEMENT: THE WORD AND NOTHING BUT THE WORD

**Key Scripture:** "He that rejecteth me, and receiveth not my words, hath one that judgeth him: the word that I have spoken, the same shall judge him in the last day." -- John 12:48

### The Human Need (Humanistic)

- People wonder, "Which standard? Which church? Which denomination has it right?"
- In a world of a thousand conflicting voices, how can anyone know what God will hold them to?
- The fear is real: "What if I have been following the wrong thing my whole life?"

### Divine Revelation

**The Word says:**
> "In the beginning was the Word, and the Word was with God, and the Word was God." -- John 1:1

**The type unfolds:**
In the Old Testament, God wrote the Law on tablets of stone -- an unchangeable standard. No king could alter it, no priest could amend it. When Josiah found the Book of the Law in the Temple (2 Kings 22), the nation trembled -- because the standard had been there all along, gathering dust, while the people lived by their own ideas. So it is today. The standard of judgement is not the Catholic church, not the Baptist church, not any denomination. It is the Word -- the eternal, unchangeable, God-breathed Word. Jesus Christ and the Word are One.

**From the Message:**
> "Now, God and his Word is the self-same being. Think of it! I believe that God will judge the world some day. Not by the act of Congress, not by the act of a church, but by his Word He'll judge the world. If we had to take church -- organization church -- which one of them is right?"
> *-- 1962-03-19 -- The End-time Sign Seed -- p7*

**From the Message:**
> "A thinking man's filter. A man that wants to stand in the presence of God... What was God's filter? His Word. 'The day you eat thereof, that day you die.' I don't care what's happening out here; don't you pass that Word. Washed by the waters of the Word -- a thinking man's filter. Not a denomination, not a creed, not a church, not a cathedral, but a thinking man's filter! For you're going to be judged by the Word."
> *-- 1965-08-22 -- A thinking man's filter -- p59*

**Sub-points:**
- A. Jesus said, "The word that I have spoken, the same shall judge him in the last day" (John 12:48). Not a creed -- the Word.
- B. If God judged the world by the Catholic church, the Protestants are lost. If by the Methodists, the Baptists are lost. But He judges by His Word, and that settles every argument.
- C. This is why the Message of the hour matters. The Word has been restored in its fullness through the ministry of a prophet -- and that Word is the standard by which this generation will be judged.

### THUS SAITH THE LORD -- The Vision

Church, listen to me. The countdown to judgement is a countdown to the Word. Every sermon you have heard, every scripture that has been placed before you, every revelation that has been vindicated in this hour -- it will all stand as a witness. You will not be judged by what your church taught you; you will be judged by what the Word said. And the Word is Christ. Line up with it. Walk in it. Let it wash you and keep you. The thinking man's filter is not a denomination -- it is the Word of the Living God.

## POINT 6 -- THE SAINTS SHALL JUDGE THE WORLD

**Key Scripture:** "Do ye not know that the saints shall judge the earth? and if the earth shall be judged by you, are ye unworthy to judge the smallest matters?" -- 1 Corinthians 6:2

### The Human Need (Humanistic)

- Many believers feel small, insignificant, overlooked by the world. They wonder if their faithfulness even matters.
- The world laughs at the church. The world calls the Bride "a little group of nobodies."
- But God has a different plan for this little group.

### Divine Revelation

**The Word says:**
> "Do ye not know that the saints shall judge the earth?" -- 1 Corinthians 6:2a

**The type unfolds:**
In the book of Daniel, the prophet saw the Ancient of Days take His seat, and the books were opened. But notice who was with Him: "the saints of the most High shall take the kingdom, and possess the kingdom for ever, even for ever and ever" (Daniel 7:18). The Bride is not merely acquitted at the judgement -- she is *seated* with the Judge. Just as Esther stood with the King when Haman was judged, just as Joseph sat on the throne when his brothers bowed before him, so the Bride of Christ will stand at His side when the nations are brought to account.

**From the Message:**
> "Here He comes back for the judgment upon the nations. Paul said, 'Dare any of you all go to court that has a matter against one another? can't take care of these little things? Know ye not that the saints shall judge the earth?' Here's the judgment that has been given over to Christ, and His... They're the attorneys in the box, and there He sits at the throne. Here's the bride, the attorneys."
> *-- 1960-12-11 -- Church Ages, part 9: The ten virgins, and the 144,000 -- p107*

**Sub-points:**
- A. The Bride is not before the judgement seat -- she is at the Judge's side. She has already been judged in Christ and found "not guilty."
- B. The saints judge the earth by the testimony they have lived. Your life of faithfulness in this hour becomes a standard by which others are measured.
- C. This is the destiny of the Bride: not just to escape judgement, but to reign and rule with Christ forever.

**From the Message:**
> "Don't Paul say, 'Dare you [talking to the bride], having any matter or grievance against each other, that you go to the unjust law. Don't you know the saints shall judge the earth?' There you are. The Saints is going to judge the earth and take it over. That's right!"
> *-- 1963-03-18 -- Seals 3: The First Seal -- p54*

### THUS SAITH THE LORD -- The Vision

Little Bride, do not despise the day of small things. The world may look at you and see nothing -- a small congregation, a humble people, no great cathedrals or television programs. But God sees something else. He sees His queen, His co-heir, His partner in judgement. You will not always be the judged -- one day you will be the judges. And the testimony of your life, your faithfulness to the Word in this dark hour, will be the very standard by which nations rise or fall. The countdown to judgement is also a countdown to your coronation.

## CONCLUSION

### The Epiphany -- The Question Answered

> What is judgement, who is the Judge, and how does receiving Jesus Christ change everything about where you stand on that great and terrible Day?
>
> "Judgement is the Word of God applied in righteousness by the only qualified Judge -- Jesus Christ, the Son of the Living God. And for the one who has heard His Word, believed on Him, and been baptized by one Spirit into His Body, there is no condemnation, no countdown to fear. Your judgement fell on Calvary. You have passed from death unto life. And when He sits as Judge, you will not stand before Him -- you will stand beside Him."

### Summary

- **Point 1:** All judgement has been committed unto the Son -- Jesus Christ is the only righteous and qualified Judge.
- **Point 2:** The believer who is in Christ has already been judged at Calvary and shall not come into condemnation.
- **Point 3:** There are distinct categories of judgement -- the Bride, the foolish virgins, and the wicked each face a different court.
- **Point 4:** Four souls at the crossroads -- Judas, the rich young ruler, the thief, and the rich man -- show that judgement is measured by light received, opportunity given, and what you did with it.
- **Point 5:** The standard of judgement is not a church or a creed -- it is the Word of God, which is Christ Himself.
- **Point 6:** The saints shall judge the world -- the Bride's destiny is not merely to escape judgement but to reign with the Judge.

> **The heart of this message:** The countdown to judgement is not a reason for terror but for decision -- receive Christ, be hid in His body, and the judgement that shakes the world will be the very thing that crowns the Bride.

### A Closing Hymn

**Suggested hymn:** "My Hope Is Built On Nothing Less"
**Why:** This hymn captures the entire arc of the sermon -- that our confidence before the judgement seat rests not on our own goodness, but on the blood and righteousness of Jesus Christ. "When He shall come with trumpet sound, oh may I then in Him be found, dressed in His righteousness alone, faultless to stand before the throne."
