## 🏁 Session 1 — **The Furious Ride: When Zeal Replaces Surrender**

![Image](https://d3d00swyhr67nd.cloudfront.net/w1200h1200/collection/LSW/GADM/LSW_GADM_1997_096-001.jpg)

![Image](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/3/39/Jehu_on_the_Black_Obelisk_of_Shalmaneser_III.jpg)

![Image](https://media.bible.art/e53c1e21-d78c-4864-a3ac-d5f59c154232-compressed.jpg)

![Image](https://media.bible.art/ea447e21-3a06-4800-b40e-043851be1706-thumbnail.jpg)

**Primary Text:** 2 Kings 9:14–20
**Key Verse:** “He driveth furiously.”

### Big Idea:

**Energy is not the same as obedience.**
God desires surrendered hearts, not just passionate instruments.

### Points:

1. **The Anointing Does Not Equal Transformation**

   * Jehu was divinely commissioned (1 Kings 19:16).
   * Only northern king formally anointed.
   * Yet no evidence of prayer, surrender, or intimacy with God.

2. **Furious Movement Can Mask Inner Emptiness**

   * His speed defined him.
   * Sometimes movement replaces meditation.
   * Busyness can silence conviction.

3. **Zeal That Announces Itself**

   * “Come see *my* zeal for the LORD.” (2 Kings 10:16)
   * A surrendered man says, “Come see what God has done.”
   * Hidden need: validation and public glory.

4. **Application**

   * Are we doing God’s work… or building our name?
   * Is our ministry fueled by devotion or ambition?

### Prophetic Question:

**Are you driving furiously — but never bowing quietly?**

## 🔥 Session 2 — **Jezebel’s Judgment: When God Uses Imperfect Instruments**

![Image](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/0/03/John_Liston_Byam_Shaw_003.jpg)

![Image](https://www.goodsalt.com/media/catalog/product/cache/72eeda554d65337da0ae7df201fead63/j/e/jezebel-being-thrown-from-a-window-1-GoodSalt-pppas0408.jpg)

![Image](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/d/d1/New_pictorial_Bible_%281%29_-_Jezebel_eaten_by_dogs.png)

![Image](https://victorianweb.org/art/illustration/blackburn/7.jpg)

**Primary Text:** 2 Kings 9:30–37

### Big Idea:

God fulfills His Word — even through flawed people.

### Points:

1. **Prophecy Fulfilled Precisely**

   * Elijah’s word executed decades later.
   * God never forgets injustice.

2. **Jezebel as a System**

   * Seduction
   * Manipulation
   * Religious corruption
   * Worldliness

3. **Jehu as Judgment**

   * Uncompromising against visible wickedness.
   * Could not be seduced.
   * Spiritual toughness.

4. **The Danger**

   * Destroying external Jezebel while harboring internal idols.
   * Reform without renewal.

### Application:

* It is possible to fight corruption publicly while tolerating compromise privately.

## 🧠 Session 3 — **Knowledge That Does Not Transform**

**Primary Text:** 2 Kings 9:25–26; 10:31

### Big Idea:

You can quote prophecy and still lack surrender.

### Points:

1. **Jehu Knew the Word**

   * Quoted Elijah.
   * Connected Naboth’s field to prophecy.
   * Understood divine justice.

2. **But He Took No Heed**

   * “Jehu took no heed to walk in the law… with all his heart.” (2 Kings 10:31)
   * Intellectual faith vs transformative faith.

3. **The Difference**

   * Intellectual faith: Uses Scripture.
   * Perfect faith: Submits to Scripture.

4. **No Recorded Prayer**

   * He acted.
   * He declared.
   * He displayed.
   * He never sought.

### Application:

* Do we study Scripture to obey it — or to deploy it?

### Prophetic Question:

**Has the Word informed you — but never transformed you?**

## ⚖️ Session 4 — **Partial Obedience: The Sin That Looks Like Success**

![Image](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/f/f0/The_golden_calf_at_Bethel.jpg)

![Image](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/2/2f/Worshiping_the_golden_calf.jpg)

![Image](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/3/30/The_Black_Obelisk_of_Shalmaneser_III%2C_9th_century_BC%2C_from_Nimrud%2C_Iraq._The_British_Museum.jpg)

![Image](https://library.biblicalarchaeology.org/sites/default/files/bsba170105900l.jpg)

**Primary Text:** 2 Kings 10:28–32; Hosea 1:4

### Big Idea:

Partial obedience is refined rebellion.

### Points:

1. **He Destroyed Baal — But Kept the Calves**

   * Eliminated foreign idolatry.
   * Preserved politically useful religion.
   * Institutional loyalty over devotion.

2. **Convenient Compromise**

   * Jeroboam’s calves protected national identity.
   * He chose throne stability over spiritual purity.

3. **God’s Mixed Verdict**

   * Commended (2 Kings 10:30).
   * Judged later (Hosea 1:4).
   * The act was right — the heart was wrong.

4. **Territorial Loss**

   * Hazael reduced Israel’s borders.
   * External erosion mirrors internal compromise.

### Application:

* What idols remain because they serve our comfort?
* What systems do we preserve because they secure our position?

---

## 👑 Session 5 — **The Man Who Was Used by God but Never Walked With God**

**Primary Text:** 2 Kings 10:15–16

### Big Idea:

You can be God’s instrument without being God’s friend.

### Points:

1. **“Is Thine Heart Right?”**

   * Jehu asked Jehonadab.
   * Never asked himself.

2. **Visible Needs**

   * Legitimacy
   * Security
   * Recognition

3. **Hidden Needs**

   * Validation
   * Public endorsement
   * Fear beneath fury

4. **The Tragedy**

   * Longest-reigning northern king (28 years).
   * Four-generation dynasty.
   * Yet spiritually hollow.

### Final Application:

* Do we want God — or God’s platform?
* Do we want obedience — or outcomes?

# 🔥 Closing Challenge for the Series

Jehu destroyed Jezebel.
Jehu fulfilled prophecy.
Jehu reformed a nation.
Jehu received commendation.

But Scripture’s final verdict is devastating:

> “He took no heed to walk in the law of the LORD with all his heart.”

**The question Jehu asked still stands in every generation:**

> **“Is thine heart right?”**
