# Vials in Revelation 15–17 (KJV)

**Date:** 2026-05-04  
**Title:** The Seven Golden Vials in Revelation 15–17  
**Scripture(s):** Revelation 15; Revelation 16; Revelation 17  
**Theme / Central thought:** In the KJV text, the vials are presented as golden vessels full of the wrath of God. Revelation 15 introduces them, Revelation 16 shows them poured out in sequence, and Revelation 17 connects one of the vial angels to the unveiling of Babylon's judgment.

---

## Overview

The word **vials** appears in this section as part of the final outpouring of divine judgment.

- **Revelation 15** introduces the seven angels and the **seven golden vials full of the wrath of God**.
- **Revelation 16** records the pouring out of all seven vials upon the earth, sea, rivers, sun, seat of the beast, Euphrates, and air.
- **Revelation 17** begins with **one of the seven angels which had the seven vials** showing John the judgment of the great whore, Mystery Babylon.

This creates a clear flow in the KJV:

1. **Preparation of judgment** — Revelation 15  
2. **Execution of judgment** — Revelation 16  
3. **Explanation of judgment** — Revelation 17

---

## Key KJV References

### Revelation 15:7

> "And one of the four beasts gave unto the seven angels seven golden vials full of the wrath of God, who liveth for ever and ever."

### Revelation 16:1

### Revelation 17:1

## Chapter-by-Chapter Research

## Revelation 15 — The Vials Are Given

This chapter does not yet describe the vials being poured out. It prepares the reader for that moment.

### Main observations

- John sees **"seven angels having the seven last plagues"** (Rev. 15:1).
- These plagues are called the final completion of God's wrath: **"for in them is filled up the wrath of God"**.
- Before judgment falls, John sees a victorious company standing on the **sea of glass mingled with fire** (Rev. 15:2).
- They sing **the song of Moses** and **the song of the Lamb**, which places judgment in a worship context and shows God as righteous in all His ways (Rev. 15:3-4).
- The heavenly temple is opened, and the seven angels come out clothed in purity and priestly dignity (Rev. 15:5-6).
- The vials are specifically called **golden vials** and are **full of the wrath of God** (Rev. 15:7).
- The temple is filled with smoke from God's glory so that **no man was able to enter into the temple, till the seven plagues ... were fulfilled** (Rev. 15:8).

### Key emphasis

The KJV emphasizes that the vials are not random disasters. They proceed from the opened heavenly temple, from the glory of God, and from His settled righteousness.

---

## Revelation 16 — The Seven Vials Are Poured Out

This chapter is the central vial chapter. Each vial is poured out in order, and the response of man repeatedly shows refusal to repent.

### The seven vials in order

1. **First vial — upon the earth** (Rev. 16:2)  
   - A **noisome and grievous sore** falls upon those who have the mark of the beast and worship his image.

2. **Second vial — upon the sea** (Rev. 16:3)  
   - The sea becomes **as the blood of a dead man**, and **every living soul died in the sea**.

3. **Third vial — upon the rivers and fountains of waters** (Rev. 16:4-7)  
   - The waters become blood.
   - The angel declares God's judgment righteous because they **shed the blood of saints and prophets**, and now are given **blood to drink**.

4. **Fourth vial — upon the sun** (Rev. 16:8-9)  
   - Men are scorched with great heat.
   - The KJV notes a solemn moral response: **"they repented not to give him glory."**

5. **Fifth vial — upon the seat of the beast** (Rev. 16:10-11)  
   - His kingdom is filled with darkness.
   - Men gnaw their tongues for pain and still **repented not of their deeds**.

6. **Sixth vial — upon the great river Euphrates** (Rev. 16:12-16)  
   - The river is dried up to prepare the way of the kings of the east.
   - Three unclean spirits like frogs go out to gather the kings of the earth to the battle of the great day of God Almighty.
   - Armageddon is named in verse 16.

7. **Seventh vial — into the air** (Rev. 16:17-21)  
   - A great voice from the temple says, **"It is done."**
   - Voices, thunders, lightnings, and the greatest earthquake follow.
   - Great Babylon comes into remembrance before God.
   - Great hail falls, yet men still blaspheme God.

### Repeated pattern in Revelation 16

A major thread in the KJV text is not only the judgments themselves, but man's response to them.

- **They blasphemed the name of God** (Rev. 16:9)
- **They repented not to give him glory** (Rev. 16:9)
- **They repented not of their deeds** (Rev. 16:11)
- **Men blasphemed God because of the plague of the hail** (Rev. 16:21)

The chapter therefore presents the vials as exposing the hardness of those under judgment.

## Symbols Grouped by Vial

This section gathers the main images attached to each vial and offers brief notes on what they may symbolize. Some meanings are explicit in the KJV text, while others are reasonable interpretive observations.

### Setup symbols before the pouring begins

- **Golden vials** (Rev. 15:7)
   - These suggest heavenly order, sacred authority, and measured judgment.
   - The wrath is not accidental; it is held, assigned, and poured out by divine command.

- **Temple opened in heaven** (Rev. 15:5)
   - This symbolizes judgment proceeding from God's own holy court.

- **Temple filled with smoke from the glory of God** (Rev. 15:8)
   - This suggests overwhelming divine presence, majesty, and the solemn finality of what is about to happen.

### First vial — earth

- **Earth** (Rev. 16:2)
   - May symbolize the settled realm of human life and the visible social order.

- **Noisome and grievous sore**
   - May symbolize corruption breaking out openly in those aligned with the beast.
   - At minimum, it marks a painful judgment falling directly on beast-worshippers.

- **Mark of the beast**
   - Explicitly identifies the target group as those in conscious allegiance to the beast system.

### Second vial — sea

- **Sea** (Rev. 16:3)
   - In apocalyptic language, the sea often suggests the mass of restless nations or the broader world of human movement and commerce.

- **Blood of a dead man**
   - Suggests death, uncleanness, and total corruption.
   - The sea is not merely stained; it becomes a realm of death.

- **Every living soul died in the sea**
   - Points to sweeping judgment, not a minor disturbance.

### Third vial — rivers and fountains of waters

- **Rivers and fountains** (Rev. 16:4)
   - May symbolize the sources of life, supply, refreshment, and civilization.

- **Waters became blood**
   - Explicitly connects judgment to bloodguilt when read with verses 5-6.

- **Angel of the waters** (Rev. 16:5)
   - Highlights divine oversight over creation and affirms that the judgment is righteous.

- **Blood to drink** (Rev. 16:6)
   - Symbolizes fitting recompense: those who shed blood receive blood in judgment.

### Fourth vial — sun

- **Sun** (Rev. 16:8)
   - May symbolize the supreme ruling light or the greatest natural power over daily life.

- **Scorched with fire / great heat**
   - Suggests intense exposure, torment, and consuming pressure.
   - What usually gives light and order now becomes an instrument of affliction.

- **Blasphemy instead of repentance** (Rev. 16:9)
   - Symbolizes that judgment by itself does not soften the rebellious heart.

### Fifth vial — seat of the beast

- **Seat of the beast** (Rev. 16:10)
   - Symbolizes the throne, center, or governing authority of the beast kingdom.

- **Darkness**
   - Commonly symbolizes confusion, misery, spiritual blindness, or collapse of dominion.
   - Here it falls specifically on the beast's realm.

- **Gnawing tongues for pain**
   - Suggests unbearable anguish and inward torment.

### Sixth vial — Euphrates, frogs, kings, Armageddon

- **Great river Euphrates** (Rev. 16:12)
   - May symbolize a boundary, restraint, or strategic barrier being removed.

- **Water dried up**
   - Suggests preparation for invasion, movement, or the removal of an obstacle.

- **Kings of the east**
   - Symbolize approaching geopolitical powers being positioned for the final conflict.

- **Three unclean spirits like frogs** (Rev. 16:13)
   - Frogs suggest uncleanness, croaking propaganda, or spiritually foul influences.
   - The text explicitly says these are **spirits of devils, working miracles**.

- **Dragon, beast, false prophet**
   - Symbolize a counterfeit trinity of satanic power, political force, and religious deception.

- **Gathering of the kings of the earth** (Rev. 16:14)
   - Suggests global consolidation of rebellion.

- **Armageddon** (Rev. 16:16)
   - Symbolizes the climactic gathering point of organized opposition to God.

### Seventh vial — air, voice, earthquake, hail, Babylon

- **Air** (Rev. 16:17)
   - May symbolize the broadest sphere over the whole inhabited world, as though judgment now reaches the total environment of human rule.

- **Great voice from the temple, from the throne: "It is done."**
   - Symbolizes finality, completion, and irreversible divine decree.

- **Voices, thunders, lightnings** (Rev. 16:18)
   - Often symbolize divine majesty, courtroom authority, and the manifestation of God's direct intervention.

- **Great earthquake**
   - May symbolize the shaking of all established structures, political and social as well as physical.

- **Great city divided / cities of the nations fell** (Rev. 16:19)
   - Symbolizes fragmentation and collapse of organized human civilization in rebellion against God.

- **Babylon remembered before God**
   - Symbolizes that accumulated corruption is now fully called into judgment.

- **Cup of the wine of the fierceness of his wrath**
   - Suggests that Babylon, which made nations drink her fornication, must now drink God's wrath.

- **Islands fled / mountains not found** (Rev. 16:20)
   - May symbolize the removal of stability, refuge, and what seemed permanent.

- **Great hail** (Rev. 16:21)
   - Suggests crushing judgment falling directly from heaven.

### Revelation 17 symbols that explain the vial outcome

- **One of the seven vial angels** (Rev. 17:1)
   - Shows continuity between the vial judgments and Babylon's exposure.

- **Great whore upon many waters**
   - The text later explains the waters as **peoples, and multitudes, and nations, and tongues** (Rev. 17:15).
   - She symbolizes an unfaithful, corrupt, ruling religious-political system.

- **Golden cup in her hand** (Rev. 17:4)
   - A counterfeit to what appears precious; outward splendor containing inward abomination.

- **Woman drunken with blood** (Rev. 17:6)
   - Symbolizes persecution, cruelty, and delight in violence against saints.

### Summary observation

The symbols move from **localized judgment** to **cosmic and civilizational collapse**:

- earth
- sea
- rivers
- sun
- beast's throne
- Euphrates and world gathering
- air, Babylon, and the shaking of the whole order

Taken together, the vial symbols portray God's judgment touching body, environment, power structures, spiritual deception, world politics, and finally the whole organized system of rebellious human civilization.

## Revelation 17 — One Vial Angel Explains Babylon

This chapter does not continue the sequence by adding another vial. Instead, it introduces **one of the seven angels which had the seven vials** as John's guide into the mystery of Babylon.

### Main observations

- The angel says, **"I will shew unto thee the judgment of the great whore that sitteth upon many waters"** (Rev. 17:1).
- Babylon is shown as seductive, wealthy, corrupt, and spiritually intoxicating (Rev. 17:2-4).
- She is named **"MYSTERY, BABYLON THE GREAT, THE MOTHER OF HARLOTS AND ABOMINATIONS OF THE EARTH"** (Rev. 17:5).
- She is drunk with the blood of saints and martyrs (Rev. 17:6).
- The chapter then explains the beast, the heads, the horns, and the eventual destruction of the woman (Rev. 17:7-18).

### Connection to the vials

Revelation 17 shows that the vial judgments are not merely natural catastrophes; they are tied to the exposure and overthrow of a corrupt world system opposed to God.

Especially important is the bridge between chapters 16 and 17:

- In **Revelation 16:19**, **great Babylon came in remembrance before God**.
- In **Revelation 17:1**, one of the vial angels begins to show John that judgment in detail.

So chapter 17 expands what chapter 16 announced.

## The Angels — What the KJV Text Says Specifically

### How many angels are involved

The KJV text identifies **seven angels** as the primary agents of the vial judgments, plus two other voices that speak in response to them.

- **Seven vial angels** — introduced in Rev. 15:1 and active through Rev. 16 and 17.
- **Angel of the waters** — a distinct speaking angel in Rev. 16:5-6.
- **Voice out of the altar** — a second confirmatory voice in Rev. 16:7 (possibly angelic; the KJV does not name this speaker).

---

### The appearance of the seven angels (Revelation 15:6)

> "And the seven angels came out of the temple, having the seven plagues, **clothed in pure and white linen**, and having their **breasts girded with golden girdles**."

These are the only physical details the KJV gives about the vial angels. Several things are worth noting:

- **Pure and white linen** — this is priestly attire. The same material is associated with righteousness (Rev. 19:8) and with the Lamb's army (Rev. 19:14). The angels carry wrath, but they are clothed in purity, indicating the judgments are not morally defiled but righteous.
- **Golden girdles on the breasts** — this parallels the description of the glorified Christ in Revelation 1:13, where he appears with a golden girdle. It places these angels in the category of holy, authoritative, and heavenly servants acting under divine commission.

---

### Where the angels come from (Revelation 15:5-6)

> "And after that I looked, and, behold, the **temple of the tabernacle of the testimony in heaven was opened**: And the seven angels came out of the temple…"

- The angels emerge from **the opened heavenly temple**, specifically identified as the tabernacle of the testimony — the same heavenly original behind the Mosaic tabernacle.
- This connects the vial judgments directly to God's covenantal faithfulness and holiness, not simply power.

---

### Who gives them the vials (Revelation 15:7)

> "And **one of the four beasts** gave unto the seven angels seven golden vials full of the wrath of God, who liveth for ever and ever."

- The four beasts (living creatures) who surround the throne are the ones who hand the vials to the angels.
- This means the chain of authority is: God → four living creatures → seven angels → earth.
- The vials are not self-initiated by the angels; they act entirely as commissioned servants.

---

### How the angels are commanded to act (Revelation 16:1)

> "And I heard **a great voice out of the temple** saying to the seven angels, **Go your ways, and pour out the vials of the wrath of God upon the earth.**"

- The command comes from the temple itself, reinforcing that this judgment proceeds from the holy dwelling of God.
- The KJV does not identify whose voice this is. It may be God's voice directly.
- The phrase **"Go your ways"** indicates orderly dispatch, not chaos.

---

### The angel of the waters (Revelation 16:5-6)

> "And I heard **the angel of the waters** say, Thou art righteous, O Lord, which art, and wast, and shalt be, because thou hast judged thus. For they have shed the blood of saints and prophets, and thou hast given them blood to drink; for they are worthy."

- This angel appears to have a specific domain or responsibility over the waters of the earth.
- His speech is not a command or action but a **declaration of God's righteousness** in judgment.
- He interprets the water-turned-blood as just recompense for bloodshed against saints and prophets.
- This is one of the few moments in Revelation 16 where an angel offers theology, not just action.

---

### The voice out of the altar (Revelation 16:7)

> "And I heard **another out of the altar** say, Even so, Lord God Almighty, true and righteous are thy judgments."

- This voice echoes the angel of the waters and agrees with the verdict.
- The KJV says it comes "out of the altar," which may point to the souls under the altar of Revelation 6:9-10 who cried for God to avenge their blood — now, in a sense, answered.

---

### The seventh angel and the declaration "It is done" (Revelation 16:17)

> "And the seventh angel poured out his vial **into the air**; and there came **a great voice out of the temple of heaven, from the throne**, saying, **It is done.**"

- The seventh vial is poured into the air, the widest possible sphere.
- The declaration **"It is done"** mirrors Christ's cry from the cross ("It is finished," John 19:30), though in a different context — here it is the completion of wrath, not redemption.
- The voice comes from the throne itself, the highest authority.

---

### The vial angel who guides John in Revelation 17 (Revelation 17:1)

> "And there came **one of the seven angels which had the seven vials**, and talked with me, saying unto me, **Come hither; I will shew unto thee the judgment of the great whore…**"

- The text does not name which of the seven vial angels this is.
- His role shifts from executing judgment to **explaining and interpreting** it.
- This is significant: the same angels who pour out God's wrath are also assigned the work of revealing God's purposes to John.

---

### Summary of what makes the vial angels distinctive

| Feature | KJV Detail |
|---|---|
| Number | Seven |
| Source | Come out of the opened heavenly temple |
| Clothing | Pure white linen, golden girdles on the breast |
| Who assigns them | One of the four living creatures |
| Who commands them | A great voice from the temple |
| Role in Rev. 16 | Pour out each vial in sequence |
| Angel of the waters | Interprets the third vial as just recompense |
| Voice from the altar | Confirms God's judgments are righteous |
| Seventh angel | Final vial; "It is done" follows |
| One in Rev. 17 | Shifts from judgment-executor to revelation-guide |

## Main Themes in the KJV Text

- **The vials are full** — God's wrath has reached a completed measure.
- **The vials are golden** — even in judgment, the scene is heavenly and ordered, not chaotic.
- **The vials are final** — Revelation 15:1 calls them the **seven last plagues**.
- **The judgments are righteous** — heaven openly declares God's ways **just and true**.
- **The wicked do not repent** — the KJV repeatedly stresses blasphemy and refusal.
- **Babylon is remembered and judged** — the vials move history toward the downfall of the great corrupting power.

## Study Notes for Preaching or Further Research

- The scene begins in worship before it moves into wrath.
- The song of Moses and the song of the Lamb frame God's judgments as holy and true.
- The judgments fall especially on the beast's domain and worshippers.
- The vials reveal both God's justice and man's impenitence.
- The final vials move directly toward Babylon's remembrance, exposure, and destruction.
- Revelation 17 should be read as an enlargement of the Babylon judgment announced under the seventh vial.

## Interpolated Timeline — Vials vs. Human History

If we try to place the vials on the larger timeline of human existence, the safest reading is that the vials belong **near the end of human rule under sin**, not near the beginning or middle of history.

### What the KJV text makes clear

- The vials are called the **seven last plagues** (Rev. 15:1).
- They are poured out on a world where the **beast**, his **mark**, and his worship system are already active (Rev. 16:2).
- They culminate in the gathering to **Armageddon** and the remembrance of **Babylon** before God (Rev. 16:16, 19).
- Revelation 17 then expands the judgment of that Babylonian world order.

This means the vials are not early-history judgments like the flood, Babel, Egypt, or the exile. In the KJV sequence they are **end-stage judgments** falling on a fully matured rebellious world system.

### Broad biblical timeline

Below is a simple interpolation of where the vials appear to fit in relation to human history:

1. **Creation and fall**  
   - Human existence begins in Genesis.
   - Sin, death, and rebellion enter the race.

2. **Old Testament world history**  
   - Flood, Babel, patriarchs, Israel, kingdoms, prophets.
   - These contain many judgments, but they are not the seven vials.

3. **First coming of Christ**  
   - Redemption is accomplished in history through Christ's death, burial, and resurrection.

4. **Church age / gospel age**  
   - The gospel goes to the nations.
   - Human history continues under mercy, patience, and mixed judgments, but Revelation 15-16 has not yet reached its terminal language.

5. **Final beast-centered world rebellion**  
   - The mark of the beast, false worship, and concentrated anti-God rule are in place.
   - This is the setting required by Revelation 16:2, 10, and 13.

6. **The seven vials**  
   - God's wrath is filled up in the seven last plagues.
   - These appear to fall during the closing phase of human rebellion.

7. **Babylon exposed and judged**  
   - The seventh vial brings Babylon into remembrance before God.
   - Revelation 17 explains her nature and doom in more detail.

8. **Transition out of the present evil order**  
   - Armageddon, collapse of the rebellious order, and the end of man's proud system as presently arranged.

### Simple timeline statement

If human existence is viewed as a long line from **Genesis to the final judgment of evil**, the vials belong very near the **far right end** of that line.

In simple terms:

- **Most of human history** happens **before** the vials.
- **The beast system is already mature** when the vials begin.
- **Babylon's fall is already in motion** by the later vials.
- The vials mark the **closing judgments of the present world order**.

### Rough visual sketch

Creation -> Fall -> Nations -> Israel -> Christ -> Church age -> Beast system fully manifest -> Seven vials -> Babylon judged -> Present order collapses

### Important caution

The KJV text clearly gives the **sequence of the vial judgments**, but it does not give a dated calendar in modern historical terms. So any timeline comparison to "human existence" should be stated as an **interpolation**, not as an exact chronological chart.

The strongest conclusion is this:

- The vials belong to the **late, final phase** of man's rebellious civilization.
- They are **not** spread across ordinary history.
- They come when evil has reached a ripened and organized form under the beast and Babylon.

### Preaching takeaway

The vials are not simply about disaster; they show that human history does not continue endlessly. According to Revelation 15-17, history moves toward a moment when God answers accumulated rebellion, remembers Babylon, and closes the age with final judgments.

## Possible Sermon Angles

- **Golden vials, terrible contents** — outward heavenly order, inward fullness of wrath.
- **When heaven says, "It is done"** — the seventh vial and the finishing of judgment.
- **Judgment without repentance** — how Revelation 16 shows the hardness of fallen man.
- **Babylon remembered before God** — what men celebrate on earth, God judges from heaven.
- **The God who judges is the God heaven still worships** — linking Revelation 15:3-4 with chapter 16.

---

## Closing Thought

In the KJV text, the vials are not isolated symbols. They are the final, measured, God-sent judgments that proceed from heaven's temple, vindicate the blood of saints and prophets, expose the rebellion of the beast's kingdom, and open the way for Babylon's judgment to be fully revealed.
