# Third Vial — Rivers and Fountains of Waters Become Blood
## Revelation 16:4-7

---

### Scripture (KJV)
> **Revelation 16:4** — And the third angel poured out his vial upon the rivers and fountains of waters; and they became blood.
>
> **Revelation 16:5** — And I heard the angel of the waters say, Thou art righteous, O Lord, which art, and wast, and shalt be, because thou hast judged thus.
>
> **Revelation 16:6** — For they have shed the blood of saints and prophets, and thou hast given them blood to drink; for they are worthy.
>
> **Revelation 16:7** — And I heard another out of the altar say, Even so, Lord God Almighty, true and righteous are thy judgments.

### Cross-Reference — Egypt (KJV)
> **Exodus 7:20-21** — And he lifted up the rod, and smote the waters that were in the river… and all the waters that were in the river were turned to blood.

### Cross-Reference — Third Trumpet (KJV)
> **Revelation 8:10-11** — And the third angel sounded, and there fell a great star from heaven, burning as it were a lamp, and it fell upon the third part of the rivers, and upon the fountains of waters; And the name of the star is called Wormwood: and the third part of the waters became wormwood; and many men died of the waters, because they were made bitter.

---

## 1. From Sea to Springs — Judgment Goes Deeper

The second vial struck the sea — the vast saltwater oceans, the multitudes of nations and peoples. The third vial strikes **rivers and fountains of waters** — freshwater, the sources and springs of life. Nothing is exempt. From ocean to inland waterway to mountain spring, every source of water becomes blood.

If the second vial cuts off the apostate masses, the third vial cuts off the very **sources of spiritual refreshing** that the false church claimed to offer. Their rivers of doctrine, their fountains of tradition, their springs of denominational teaching — all of it becomes the blood of judgment.

---

## 2. The Divine Rationale — Blood for Blood

The most striking feature of the third vial is the **explicit divine commentary** provided. An angel (the angel of the waters) and a voice from the altar both speak, declaring this judgment righteous and just. God does not pour out this vial in anger alone — He pours it out in perfect, measured **recompense**.

### The Crime: Shedding the Blood of Saints and Prophets

> **Revelation 16:6** — For they have shed the blood of saints and prophets, and thou hast given them blood to drink; for they are worthy.

The apostate religious system — primarily the Roman Catholic Church and its aligned powers — has throughout history **shed the blood of saints and prophets**. Branham taught this repeatedly:

> "He hath judged the great whore, which did corrupt the earth with her fornication, and has avenged the blood of His servants at her hand."
> — *Revelation 19:2 (KJV), cited in 1958-09-27, Why Are We Not a Denomination?, ¶75*

The Inquisition, the burning of reformers, the martyrdom of the Waldenses, the slaughter of Huguenots, the persecution of all who dared preach the pure Word of God outside her walls — all of this is remembered before God. The altar in Revelation 6:9-10 already cried out: "How long, O Lord, holy and true, dost thou not judge and avenge our blood?" The third vial is the answer.

> "For they have shed the blood of saints and prophets, and thou hast given them blood to drink; for they are worthy."

**Poetic justice** is the principle — they gave blood to drink to the saints; now blood is given to them to drink. The punishment precisely mirrors the crime. The rivers that once flowed red with martyrs' blood now flow red against the murderers.

---

## 3. The Angel of the Waters

The phrase **"the angel of the waters"** is significant. Revelation presents angels as stewards of various aspects of creation — the four angels of the winds (Rev 7:1), the angel of fire (Rev 14:18), and here, the angel presiding over the waters. When this angel speaks, he **vindicates God's justice**:

> "Thou art righteous, O Lord, which art, and wast, and shalt be, because thou hast judged thus."
> — *Revelation 16:5 (KJV)*

The eternal nature of God ("which art, and wast, and shalt be") is invoked — this judgment is not a surprise reaction but the expression of the eternal, unchanging righteousness of God. He is the same God who judged Egypt, who judged Babylon, who judged Rome's persecutions — and the third vial is the final execution of that consistent character.

The **voice from the altar** (Rev 16:7) echoes in agreement:

> "Even so, Lord God Almighty, true and righteous are thy judgments."

This is the altar from which the souls of the martyrs cried (Rev 6:9-10). Their cry is now answered. The altar that absorbed the prayers of the persecuted saints now witnesses the vindication of those prayers.

---

## 4. Parallel — Egypt's First Plague

| Egypt (Type) | Vial (Antitype) |
|---|---|
| River Nile turned to blood (Ex 7:20) | All rivers and fountains become blood (Rev 16:4) |
| Fish died; water undrinkable | Every water source becomes blood |
| Egypt dependent on Nile for life | Beast's system dependent on its waters of doctrine |
| God-vindicated through Moses | God vindicated by the angel of the waters |

The plague on Egypt's waters was the opening salvo of judgment upon Pharaoh's kingdom. In the same way, the third vial strikes the very sources of life in the beast's domain.

---

## 5. Contrast With Third Trumpet (Wormwood)

The **Third Trumpet** (Rev 8:10-11) — "Wormwood" — fell upon a third of the rivers and fountains, making the water bitter (not blood). Many died, but it was a partial judgment.

- **Wormwood** = bitterness, spiritual poison — false doctrine corrupting the waters of the Word
- **Third Vial** = blood — full and final judgment upon those who poisoned the spiritual waters

The progression: the trumpet warned with bitterness; the vial executes with blood. The false teachers who made the waters of doctrine bitter unto death will themselves drink blood.

---

## 6. Application in the Message

Branham saw the false religious system of his day already in the process of polluting the spiritual waters — the Word of God being corrupted by denominational creeds, higher criticism, and organized religion. The third vial is the ultimate consequence of that spiritual pollution.

The true springs of living water come from Christ alone:

> "If any man thirst, let him come unto me, and drink. He that believeth on me, as the scripture hath said, out of his belly shall flow rivers of living water."
> — *John 7:37-38 (KJV)*

Those who rejected these springs of living water and instead gave the people the polluted water of tradition, creed, and false doctrine are given blood to drink in the third vial — a just and righteous judgment, confirmed by heaven itself.

---

## 7. Timing

Like all the vials, the third is poured during the great tribulation after the rapture. The blood-shedding of saints that this vial avenges spans the entire history of the church ages — from the first martyrs through the last. When the seventh seal is opened and probation is closed, the accumulated account of martyrs' blood is finally settled.

> "God doesn't call man to judgment without warning."
> — *1963-07-24, God Doesn't Call Man to Judgement Without Warning, ¶56*
