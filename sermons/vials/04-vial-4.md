# Fourth Vial — The Sun Scorches Men With Fire
## Revelation 16:8-9

---

### Scripture (KJV)
> **Revelation 16:8** — And the fourth angel poured out his vial upon the sun; and power was given unto him to scorch men with fire.
>
> **Revelation 16:9** — And men were scorched with great heat, and blasphemed the name of God, which hath power over these plagues: and they repented not to give him glory.

### Cross-Reference — Fourth Trumpet (Contrast, KJV)
> **Revelation 8:12** — And the fourth angel sounded, and the third part of the sun was smitten, and the third part of the moon, and the third part of the stars; so as the third part of them was darkened, and the day shone not for a third part of it, and the night likewise.

### Cross-Reference — The Sun of Righteousness (KJV)
> **Malachi 4:1-2** — For, behold, the day cometh, that shall burn as an oven; and all the proud, yea, and all that do wickedly, shall be stubble: and the day that cometh shall burn them up, saith the LORD of hosts, that it shall leave them neither root nor branch. But unto you that fear my name shall the Sun of righteousness arise with healing in his wings.

---

## 1. The Target — The Sun

The fourth vial is poured upon **the sun itself** — the great luminary that God set in the heavens to give light and sustain all life on earth. Power is given to the sun to **scorch men with fire**, intensifying its heat to a scorching, tormenting degree.

This stands in direct contrast to the **Fourth Trumpet** (Rev 8:12), where the sun was **darkened** by one third — a withdrawal of light. In the trumpet, God reduced the sun's light as a warning, a partial judgment. In the vial, the opposite happens: the sun's intensity is **increased** to a scorching degree.

The movement from trumpet to vial shows the character of God's escalating judgment:
- **Trumpet 4** = Light taken away (warning through darkness)
- **Vial 4** = Light intensified to burning (execution through scorching heat)

---

## 2. The Antichrist Trinity of Sun-Worship

Branham's wider teaching connects sun-worship to the entire Babylonian religious system that flows through Rome. The Catholic church adopted many of the feast days, symbols, and practices of Babylonian sun-worship — including the halo (sun-disc), December 25th (Saturnalia/Natalis Solis Invicti), and the wafer (a sun-disc emblem).

> "Now, Babylon first appears in the beginning of the Bible. Then it appears in the middle of the Bible. And then it appears in the last of the Bible."
> — *1958-06-18, Handwriting on the Wall, ¶16*

The fourth vial may be understood as a judgment specifically aimed at sun-worship — those who worshipped the creation rather than the Creator. The very object of their religious veneration becomes the instrument of their torment.

---

## 3. Malachi 4 — The Day That Shall Burn as an Oven

Malachi 4:1-2 provides the prophetic background for the fourth vial. God declared that a day would come that would **burn as an oven** — consuming the proud and the wicked as stubble. This same passage promised that unto those who fear God's name, the **Sun of righteousness** would arise with healing in His wings.

Branham understood the "Sun of righteousness" as Christ Himself — the One who appeared to Branham in a pillar of fire (Light). The true Sun of righteousness brings healing to those under His wings. But those who rejected the Sun of righteousness — who preferred the Babylonian counterfeit sun — will be scorched by the literal sun under the fourth vial.

This is the terrible reversal of the end time:
- To those who accepted Christ (the Sun of righteousness): healing, glory, resurrection, rapture
- To those who rejected Him and worshipped the beast's sun: scorching, torment, and no repentance

---

## 4. "They Repented Not to Give Him Glory"

The defining statement of the fourth vial — and indeed of all the vials — is **"and they repented not."** This phrase appears at vials 4, 5, and 7, showing that the judgments do not soften the heart of the beast-worshipping world. Instead, the hardening is complete.

> "And men were scorched with great heat, and blasphemed the name of God, which hath power over these plagues: and they repented not to give him glory."
> — *Revelation 16:9 (KJV)*

The response is **blasphemy** — not repentance. They know that God has power over these plagues (they are not ignorant), but rather than calling upon Him, they curse Him. This is the fullest expression of what it means to have received the mark of the beast — the absolute, permanent hardening of the soul against God.

Branham observed the progressive hardening in his own generation:

> "Let the righteous wrath of God rain down, through old-fashioned, hellfire and brimstone preaching. Too bad we ever got away from it, and sissified it."
> — *1956-07-28, Making the Valley Full of Ditches, ¶27*

If men will not repent under preaching, under conviction, under the witness of the Spirit, under the seals, under the trumpets — they will not repent under the scorching of the sun.

---

## 5. Literal and Spiritual Dimensions

The fourth vial operates on both levels:

**Literally**: Intensified solar radiation and heat upon the earth during the tribulation period — scorching, tormenting heat that cannot be escaped.

**Spiritually**: The Light of God's truth, long rejected, becomes the instrument of judgment. Men who lived in spiritual darkness, who snuffed out the light of the gospel — are now exposed to a blazing, scorching light they cannot bear and cannot escape.

Branham used the imagery of scorching as a metaphor for the discomfort of God's searching Word:

> "I know that's scorching, but I'd rather be scorched than then burnt forever there."
> — *1957-09-15, Hebrews Chapter 7 Part 1, ¶89*

> "They may believe for a few days. After while, by and by, the hot weather and the scorching… Scorch away…"
> — *1957-08-10, Jehovah-Jireh, ¶69*

The scorching of the fourth vial is the final, irreversible version of what the Word of God has always done — separated the sincere from the superficial.

---

## 6. Timing and Position in the Sequence

The first three vials struck the earth's surface — land, sea, and fresh waters. The fourth vial moves to the **heavens above** — the sun itself. This escalation signals that no realm is beyond the reach of God's judgment. The beast's worshippers cannot hide underground (the earth is struck) or at sea (the sea becomes blood), drink from fresh water (turned to blood), or find relief in shade from an intensified sun.

The progression of judgment is comprehensive — from ground level to the sky — hemming in the unrepentant on every side.
