# The Seven Vials of God's Wrath — Overview
## Revelation 15, 16, and 17 in The Message of the Hour

---

### Scripture — Revelation 15:1 (KJV)
> And I saw another sign in heaven, great and marvellous, seven angels having the seven last plagues; for in them is filled up the wrath of God.

---

## 1. Introduction

The seven vials (also called the seven last plagues, or seven bowls of wrath) are poured out upon the earth in Revelation 16, introduced and prepared in Revelation 15, and directly tied to the judgment of Mystery Babylon in Revelation 17. They represent the final, undiluted outpouring of God's wrath upon an apostate, Christ-rejecting world — poured out after the Bride of Christ has been caught away to glory.

William Branham consistently taught that the vials are not for the Bride. They fall upon those who bore the mark of the beast, upon the apostate religious system (Rome and her Protestant daughters), and upon an unrepentant world during the great tribulation period.

## 2. Revelation 15 — The Sea of Glass Mingled With Fire

### The Scripture (KJV)
> **Rev 15:2** — And I saw as it were a sea of glass mingled with fire: and them that had gotten the victory over the beast, and over his image, and over his mark, and over the number of his name, stood on the sea of glass, having the harps of God.
>
> **Rev 15:3** — And they sing the song of Moses the servant of God, and the song of the Lamb, saying, Great and marvellous are thy works, Lord God Almighty; just and true are thy ways, thou King of saints.
>
> **Rev 15:7** — And one of the four beasts gave unto the seven angels seven golden vials full of the wrath of God, who liveth for ever and ever.
>
> **Rev 15:8** — And the temple was filled with smoke from the glory of God, and from his power; and no man was able to enter into the temple, till the seven plagues of the seven angels were fulfilled.

### Branham's Teaching

The sea of glass is a central image in Revelation 15. Branham identifies it as the heavenly antitype of the brazen laver in the tabernacle of Moses — the place of washing and preparation. In Revelation 4 it appears as clear as crystal, representing the redeemed church already standing before God, washed by the water of the Word.

> "Now we're at the laver. And notice, this laver was empty and was as clear as crystal. Why? The church had already been redeemed! Now, now we notice a little later on, though, when the tribulation saints comes up, we find it again full of fire."
> — *1961-01-08, Revelation Chapter 4c: Throne of Mercy and Judgement, ¶82*

> "And I saw as it were a sea of glass mingled with fire: and them that had gotten the victory over the beast, and over his image, and over his mark, and over the number of his name, stood on the sea of glass, having harps of God. Now, see, they had never entered in, but they had heard the Word."
> — *1961-01-08, Revelation Chapter 4c: Throne of Mercy and Judgement, ¶86*

In Revelation 15, the sea is no longer clear but is **mingled with fire** — judgment has come. The tribulation saints — those who came out of the great tribulation having refused the mark of the beast — stand upon it victorious, singing the Song of Moses and the Song of the Lamb.

> "And if the Lord will, I would like to take a text from that to call it a 'V-Day,' 'Victory Day,' as we symbolize that a complete victory. These people that in the chapter that we're… have just read, had just gotten the victory."
> — *1963-04-21, Victory Day, ¶15*

**The song of Moses** connects the scene to the great deliverance at the Red Sea — a type of the final triumph of God's people over the beast system, just as Israel triumphed over Pharaoh.

> "Over in Revelation, those who've got perfect redemption of the body, stood on the sea of glass and sung the song of Moses again, over in the book of Revelation. Talk about a Holy Ghost meeting!"
> — *1954-03-30, Redemption in Completeness in Joy, ¶32*

The temple being filled with smoke so that **no man could enter** until the seven plagues were fulfilled indicates that God's mercy has been exhausted toward the beast system. The day of grace is over. Intercession is finished. Nothing but wrath remains.

## 3. The Parallel Structure — Seals, Trumpets, Vials, Church Ages

One of Branham's foundational teachings about the book of Revelation is that the **seven seals, seven trumpets, seven vials, and seven church ages all run parallel** — they are different facets of the same period of history, all sealed up in one book, all unfolding simultaneously from different perspectives.

> "Now you see the seven vials. You know these sevens as we're going through. They all happened right at the same time, following the plagues, following the church ages just the same, because it is all sealed up into that one book. Everything happens just in rotation, and one goes right into the other."
> — *1963-03-19, Seals 4: The Second Seal, ¶118*

The seals reveal the spiritual history of the church ages from heaven's standpoint. The trumpets deal with God's judgment upon Israel. The vials are God's wrath poured upon the Gentile apostate world during the tribulation period.

Furthermore, the seventh seal contains within it all the final events:

> "Right in that seventh seal there pours out vials, and everything else are poured out. What they are, I don't know yet."
> — *1963-03-21, Seals 6: The Fourth Seal, ¶47*

## 4. Timing — When Are the Vials Poured?

The vials are poured out **during the great tribulation**, after the Bride of Christ has been raptured. They are God's undiluted, unmixed wrath upon an unrepentant world.

> "The seven seals, seven trumpets, seven vials, three woes, woman in the sun, casting out of the devil or the red dragon, comes between these times, during the great tribulation period. Don't forget it."
> — *1961-07-30, Daniel 9: The Sixfold Purpose of Gabriel's Visitation, ¶82*

The church — the true Bride — does not go through the tribulation:

> "These tribulation-period saints that come up, had went through great tribulation. The church will not go through the tribulation! Do you see? They was already in glory?"
> — *1961-01-08, Revelation Chapter 4c: Throne of Mercy and Judgement, ¶85*

When asked directly whether the vials concern the Bride, Branham answered with notable humility:

> "The Holy Spirit has told you that the trumpets have nothing to do with the bride. Do the seven vials have anything at all to do with us? I'll wait and see if the Holy Spirit reveals it in that manner. I don't know yet."
> — *1964-08-30, Questions and Answers Part 4, ¶47*

The hideous nature of the vials is not yet fully unfolded, but their coming is certain:

> "Those vials, rather, pouring out of the vials, and the seven thunders… And those hideous sights that's coming upon the earth… Men now and people today are in such a neurotic condition, the whole world…"
> — *1965-08-22, Christ is Revealed in His Own Word, ¶15*

> "When we open up those, the Lord willing, on those seven vials and show those hideous things… Men will be so insane after while, till they'll imagine they're seeing ants the size of a mountain."
> — *1965-08-22, Christ is Revealed in His Own Word, ¶18*

## 5. Revelation 17 — The Vial Angel and Mystery Babylon

Revelation 17 opens with one of the seven vial-angels showing John the judgment of the great whore. This is significant — the same angels who pour wrath are the ones who reveal the identity of the target of that wrath.

### Scripture
> **Rev 17:1** — And there came one of the seven angels which had the seven vials, and talked with me, saying unto me, Come hither; I will shew unto thee the judgment of the great whore that sitteth upon many waters.
>
> **Rev 17:5** — And upon her forehead was a name written, MYSTERY, BABYLON THE GREAT, THE MOTHER OF HARLOTS AND ABOMINATIONS OF THE EARTH.

### Branham's Identification

Branham consistently and emphatically identifies Mystery Babylon as **the Roman Catholic Church**, and her daughters — the Protestant denominations — as the harlots born from her.

> "And there came to me one of the seven angels which had the seven vials, (that's the last vials, that's poured out), and talked with me, saying… Come hither; and I will show… thee the judgment of the great whore that sitteth upon many waters."
> — *1954-05-13, The Mark of the Beast, ¶62*

> "Remember, she was called 'Mystery, Babylon' (we know that's the Catholic church), but notice, she's the mother of harlots. What's a harlot? The same thing that she is, the whore."
> — *1960-09-11, Five Definite Identifications of the True Church, ¶36*

> "For true and righteous are thy judgment; for He hath judged the great whore, which did corrupt the earth with her fornication, and has avenged the blood of His servants at her hand. [That's the ending of the Catholic church.]"
> — *1958-09-27, Why Are We Not a Denomination?, ¶75*

The "seat of the beast" (Rev 16:10, Fifth Vial) is also identified as Rome:

> "That's where it began right there, in Rome, and that's where the seat of the beast is, and from there come the old mother whore."
> — *1964-08-23, Questions and Answers Part 1, ¶162*

The Protestant daughters are not exempt:

> "There's their mother, that's what they are to begin with. Then you say, 'That's the antichrist.' That's true. Then if that's antichrist, then what about our organizations? Just as harlot and whore is the same thing."
> — *1960-09-11, Five Definite Identifications of the True Church, ¶36*

## 6. The Vials as God's Wrath Without Mixture

The vials represent **God's untempered, unmixed wrath** — the cup of His indignation poured out without mercy. The day of grace and intercession is over for those who have received the mark of the beast. The same angel who declared in Revelation 14:9-10 the doom of those who take the mark is now executing that sentence.

> "Grieve not the Holy Spirit of God… Be sealed… for the mark of the beast and the seal of God is the last thing."
> — *1959-04-15, God's Provided Way, ¶66*

The vials parallel the plagues of Egypt in purpose — as Pharaoh's heart was hardened despite plague after plague, so the beast's followers blaspheme God through every vial rather than repent (see Rev 16:9, 11, 21). God's wrath is not arbitrary; it is judicial, precise, and righteous.
