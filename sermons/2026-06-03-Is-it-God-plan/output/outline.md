> When you find yourself in a captivity you did not choose — far from the place of blessing, surrounded by voices promising you a quicker way out — how do you know whether the path before you is God's plan, or a deception sent to turn you from it?

## THIS HOUR — PROPHETIC ANCHOR

**The Bride in a Babylon She Was Called Out Of**

- The Bride is a captive people, scattered through Babylon.
- Called out of confusion, yet still hearing its noise.
- She holds a sure promise — rapture and restoration.
- The appointed time has not yet come.
- Three frog spirits croak now, hurrying the waiting.
- The cry: settle on the Word, wait, seek.

## INTRODUCTION — SETTING THE TYPE

**Tone:** Warm and pastoral — open the heart before opening the Word.

### The Human Scene (Humanistic)

**By the Rivers of Babylon**

- Picture Judah by the rivers of Babylon.
- City broken, temple gone, harps on willows.
- Covenant people in chains among strange gods.
- The natural heart screams one question: how long?
- A letter arrives from a far-off prophet.
- Nearer, louder voices promise: home in two years.
- Every captive had to decide which voice carried God.

### The Type Established

- **Shadow (OT):** Judah carried into Babylon seventy years, holding a written promise of return.
- **Substance (NT/End-time):** The Bride called out of Babylon (confusion/organized religion), holding the promise of the rapture and restored apostolic faith.
- **The two cities:** Jerusalem types the dwelling-place of God's vindicated Word and worship — Pentecost, the true Bride; Babylon types confusion, the man-built religious system that holds God's people captive.
- **Branham on this type:**
  > "Now, in this time, the end time, how that this carnal comparison, impersonations of Christianity would all mingle up into one big Babylon just like it did back there. And they were carried away into Babylon. Is that right? And the Bible said, \"The angel of the Lord called and said, 'Come out of Babylon, my people!'\" Confusion! This, \"I'm a Baptist\"; \"I'm a Methodist\"; \"I'm a Presbyterian\"; all that's just confusion. There's no salvation in it at all. Now, don't say that I'm saying the Baptist people hasn't got salvation---the Methodist people. I'm not talking about that. I'm talking about the church in its political power, it's politics. And politically speaking, because you're Methodist, or Baptist, or Presbyterian, or Catholic, has nothing to do with it. God don't even recognize it, and it's in Babylon today. And God's calling out a church out of all of them, to pull out the pure in heart, making His church. How beautiful!"
  > *— 1954-01-03 · Questions and Answers, part 1 · ¶33*

### Person Profile — Jeremiah

- **Visible need:** A discouraged people demanding immediate rescue.
- **Invisible need:** Courage to preach an unpopular Word.
- **Motive:** Love for God's people, loyalty to the Word.
- **Intention:** Anchor the captives in God's actual plan.
- **What God saw:** A true prophet who stays with the Word.

## POINT 1 — GOD ALWAYS HAS A PLAN (THE EXPECTED END)

**Key Scripture:** "For I know the thoughts that I think toward you, saith the LORD, thoughts of peace, and not of evil, to give you an expected end." — Jeremiah 29:11

### The Human Need (Humanistic)

**When the Heart Says God Forgot**

- The captives felt forgotten, driven, scattered, punished.
- Trouble's first lie: God has no plan now.
- Every believer in hardship needs this assurance.
- The captivity itself was foreknown and bounded.

### Divine Revelation

**The Word says:**
> "For thus saith the LORD, That after seventy years be accomplished at Babylon I will visit you, and perform my good word toward you, in causing you to return to this place." — Jeremiah 29:10

**The type unfolds:**

- God set the captivity and God set the return.
- Same hand that allowed Babylon limited Babylon.
- Why captive? For breaking the covenant.
- When? In stages under Nebuchadnezzar, climaxing under Zedekiah.
- When home? When seventy years were "accomplished," exactly foretold.
- A plan: beginning, measured middle, promised end.
- The Church's journey: falling away, calling out, restoration.

**From the Message:**
> "And He promised that He would return and bring the people, from scattered all over the earth after those seven years, back into Jerusalem where they left from; and He did it just that way. That's right. ``` Jeremiah 29:10 For thus saith the LORD, That after seventy years be accomplished at Babylon I will visit you, and perform my good word toward you, in causing you to return to this place. Jeremiah 29:11 For I know the thoughts that I think toward you, saith the LORD, thoughts of peace, and not of evil, to give you an expected end. Jeremiah 29:12 Then shall ye call upon me, and ye shall go and pray unto me, and I will hearken unto you. Jeremiah 29:13 And ye shall seek me, and find me, when ye shall search for me with all your heart. Jeremiah 29:14 And I will be found of you, saith the LORD: and I will turn away your captivity, and I will gather you from all the nations, and from all the places whither I have driven you, saith the LORD; and I will bring you again into the place whence I caused you to be carried away captive. ```"
> *— 1963-09-01 · Desperations · ¶32*

**Sub-points:**
- His thoughts toward them are "peace, and not of evil."
- "Expected end" means the outcome is already settled.
- The plan includes the hard middle, not only the ending.

### THUS SAITH THE LORD — The Vision

**Your Captivity Has a Numbered End**

- Bride, your captivity is not an accident.
- The God who numbered seventy years numbered your days.
- He has an expected end — rapture and homecoming.
- His thoughts toward you this hour are peace.
- The plan stands; the promise will be performed.

## POINT 2 — THERE IS ALWAYS A VOICE SENT TO SEDUCE YOU FROM THE PLAN

**Key Scripture:** "Beloved, believe not every spirit, but try the spirits whether they are of God: because many false prophets are gone out into the world." — 1 John 4:1

### The Human Need (Humanistic)

**The Sweeter, Quicker Promise**

- False prophets told the captives: it will be short.
- "Rebel, resist, you'll be home soon."
- The seducing voice is always easier and faster.
- A pressured believer is vulnerable to flattered impatience.

### Divine Revelation

**The Word says:**
> "And I saw three unclean spirits like frogs come out of the mouth of the dragon, and out of the mouth of the beast, and out of the mouth of the false prophet." — Revelation 16:13

**The type unfolds:**

- The dangerous lie comes dressed as a prophet.
- In 1 Kings 13 a young prophet held God's command.
- Eat no bread, drink no water, return another way.
- An old prophet said, "An angel spake" — and lied.
- He traded God's voice for a man's fresher claim.
- A prophet lying to a prophet — the seducing spirit.
- Enough true Word to make the false believable.
- The frog always looks backward, to tradition not promise.

**From the Message:**
> "False prophets bring false words. What do they do? Have false sensations. False prophets keep enough of the true Word to be false. They used to didn't do it, but the Bible said... You want to put down scripture on that, II Timothy 3, see. The false prophets has enough of the true Word to make it false, because the Bible said that they'd have a form of godliness. In order to have a form of godliness, they have to have some of it as truth. \"...a form of godliness, but deny the power thereof.\" The power of what? To make you straighten up, be different, serve God, not go to dances and things of the world the Bible condemns not cut your hair. Like, false prophets say that don't make any difference."
> *— 1961-12-31 · You Must Be Born Again · ¶88*

**Sub-points:**
- The seducing spirit is religious — quotes angels, has a form of godliness.
- It offers a shortcut around the cross-shaped plan.
- "Try the spirits" — the test is the already-revealed Word.

### THUS SAITH THE LORD — The Vision

**The Wolf Wears Sheep's Wool**

- Three frog spirits croak over the earth now.
- They gather the world to a counterfeit unity.
- The wolf comes in sheep's wool, with an open Bible.
- He brings a sweeter, quicker promise.
- Do not give heed to seducing spirits.
- God gave you the Word to try every spirit.

## POINT 3 — WHILE YOU WAIT: DO THE ORDINARY THINGS OF FAITH

**Key Scripture:** "Build ye houses, and dwell in them; and plant gardens, and eat the fruit of them... and seek the peace of the city whither I have caused you to be carried away captive." — Jeremiah 29:5-7

### The Human Need (Humanistic)

**We Despise the Ordinary**

- The captives wanted drama: rebellion, escape, instant miracle.
- God said build, plant, marry, raise children, pray.
- We want the spectacular while we wait.
- Most waiting is lived in daily obedience.

### Divine Revelation

**The Word says:**
> "And seek the peace of the city whither I have caused you to be carried away captive, and pray unto the LORD for it." — Jeremiah 29:7

**The type unfolds:**

- Why homely instructions — build, dwell, plant, marry, pray?
- God's plan is performed through faithful ordinary life.
- False prophets offered excitement and an early exit.
- God prescribed patience, productivity, and prayer.
- The builders were still standing when seventy years ended.
- Waiting is not idleness — it is working, praying faith.

**From the Message:**
> "And all the time these things are going on, the Spirit of the living God is striving with flesh, trying to get man away from his own achievement, trying to get man away from his own selfish motives and objectives, to a plan which God has laid down. And as long as man works in his own plan, he will be a failure. And a man can only achieve right when he comes back with the right objective and the right motive, and that is, according to God's plan for him. Any other foundation is on sinking sands."
> *— 1958-12-21 · The unity of one God in the one Church · ¶14*

**Sub-points:**
- Build and plant: keep living by faith where you are.
- Pray for the city: intercede instead of cursing it.
- Work God's plan, not your own — your own fails.

### THUS SAITH THE LORD — The Vision

**Be Found Working When He Comes**

- Bride, do not sit idle weeping until the rapture.
- Build, plant, raise a godly seed, pray, serve.
- Keep the Token burning in your house.
- The waiting is part of the plan.
- Be found working when the Master comes.

## POINT 4 — HOW YOU KNOW SOMEONE IS SENT FROM GOD

**Key Scripture:** "Beloved, believe not every spirit, but try the spirits whether they are of God." — 1 John 4:1

### The Human Need (Humanistic)

**Two Prophets, Opposite Messages**

- Two prophets, both claiming "Thus saith the Lord."
- The captives had to discern which was true.
- Sincerity is not proof; signs are not proof.
- The devil can impersonate the gifts.

### Divine Revelation

**The Word says:**
> "And ye shall seek me, and find me, when ye shall search for me with all your heart." — Jeremiah 29:13

**The type unfolds:**

- Jeremiah's mark: his word came to pass.
- His word stayed with the covenant Word.
- Babylon's prophets flattered the flesh, proven liars by time.
- Discernment is not suspicion of everyone.
- It is measuring every voice against the Word.
- Satan impersonates the gift but cannot be the Spirit.

**From the Message:**
> "Neither would any servant of God take glory. Neither would any servant of God take a gift of God and try to glorify himself, or anything else, but his right achievement is to do something to the glory of God. That's why you see the same thing today. We must have discernment of spirit, to see a person what they're trying to do. Are they trying to glorify God? Are they trying to glorify themselves? ``` 1 John 4:1 Beloved, believe not every spirit, but try the spirits whether they are of God: because many false prophets are gone out into the world. ```"
> *— 1960-03-08 · Discernment of Spirit · ¶24*

**Sub-points:**
- Test the motive: self-glory unmasks the false.
- Test by vindication: does it come to pass and agree with Scripture?
- A true messenger drives you to the Word, not himself.

### THUS SAITH THE LORD — The Vision

**The Elect Cannot Be Finally Fooled**

- This hour will almost deceive the very elect.
- But the elect hold the Word and cannot finally fall.
- Bride, you know His voice — it is vindicated Word.
- Try every spirit by the revealed Message.
- Rest in the voice that points you to God.

## POINT 5 — SEEK HIM WITH ALL YOUR HEART AND FIND THE EXPECTED END

**Key Scripture:** "And I will be found of you, saith the LORD: and I will turn away your captivity." — Jeremiah 29:14

### The Human Need (Humanistic)

**The Half-Hearted Are Easy Prey**

- A half-hearted seeker is the easiest prey.
- The promise has a condition: all your heart.
- It takes desperation, not casual church-going.
- Only the all-out seeker lays hold.

### Divine Revelation

**The Word says:**
> "Then shall ye call upon me, and ye shall go and pray unto me, and I will hearken unto you." — Jeremiah 29:12

**The type unfolds:**

- God bound the return to a whole-hearted search.
- Complainers stayed captive in spirit.
- Those who sought Him with all their heart found Him.
- Branham preached this very text as "Desperations."
- It takes desperation to break through the false voices.
- The expected end belongs to the all-out seeker.

**From the Message:**
> "Desperation. \"When thou seekest Me with all thine heart, then I'll hear you,\" see. The kingdom... The law and the prophets were until John. Since then, the kingdom of heaven's been preached, and men press into it. You don't just stand around and say, \"Pick me up by the collar, Lord, push me in!\" You press into it. You get desperate, between life and death. ``` Jeremiah 29:13 And ye shall seek me, and find me, when ye shall search for me with all your heart. Luke 16:16 The law and the prophets were until John: since that time the kingdom of God is preached, and every man presseth into it. ```"
> *— 1963-09-01 · Desperations · ¶102*

**Sub-points:**
- A heart all-in on God has no room for a counterfeit.
- "Call... pray... seek... search" — an escalating desperation God answers.
- The end is guaranteed: "I will turn away your captivity."

### THUS SAITH THE LORD — The Vision

**The Rapture Is for the Desperate**

- Bride, the rapture is not for the casual.
- It is for those who press in with all their heart.
- Surrounded by seducing voices, seek Him desperately.
- The God who turned Judah's captivity will turn yours.
- Your expected end is sure, and it is glorious.

## CONCLUSION

### The Epiphany — The Question Answered

> When you find yourself in a captivity you did not choose — far from the place of blessing, surrounded by voices promising you a quicker way out — how do you know whether the path before you is God's plan, or a deception sent to turn you from it?

**You Know by the Word**

- You know by the Word — written and vindicated.
- God's plan is bounded by His own promise.
- The seduction offers a shorter, easier way around it.
- Stay with the revealed Word; do the work of faith.
- Try every spirit; seek Him with all your heart.
- His thoughts toward you are peace — the expected end.

### Summary

- **Point 1:** God always has a plan — a foreknown captivity with a promised, expected end.
- **Point 2:** There is always a seducing voice — even a prophet lying to a prophet — offering a shortcut.
- **Point 3:** While you wait, do the ordinary things of faith — build, plant, raise a seed, pray, press in.
- **Point 4:** Know the true messenger by trying the spirit against the vindicated Word, not signs or sincerity.
- **Point 5:** Seek Him with all your heart, and the God of the expected end will turn your captivity.

> **The heart of this message:** God's plan is settled and good; stay with His Word, refuse every seducing voice, and seek Him with all your heart until the expected end comes to pass.

### A Closing Hymn

**Suggested hymn:** "Standing on the Promises"
**Why:** It captures the captive Bride resting wholly on God's spoken Word against every wavering, seducing voice — standing on the promises until the expected end arrives.
