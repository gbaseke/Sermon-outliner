## THIS HOUR — PROPHETIC ANCHOR

The Bride of Christ today is a people in a kind of captivity — scattered through Babylon, the great system of confusion, called out yet still surrounded by the noise of organized religion. Like Judah in Jeremiah's day, she has been given a sure promise of an "expected end," a rapture and a restoration, but the appointed time has not yet come. In exactly this waiting hour the enemy releases his three frog spirits (Rev 16:13) — the dragon, the beast, and the false prophet — to whisper that the captivity will be short, that the Word can be hurried, that another voice may be trusted. The cry of this Message to the Bride is the same cry Jeremiah sent to the captives: settle down on the revealed Word, do the ordinary things of faith, do not give heed to the seducing spirits, and wait with all your heart for the God who keeps His thoughts of peace toward you.

## INTRODUCTION — SETTING THE TYPE

**Tone:** Warm and pastoral — open the heart before opening the Word.

### The Human Scene (Humanistic)
Picture Judah by the rivers of Babylon. Their city is broken, the temple gone, their harps hung on the willows. They are God's covenant people — yet they are in chains, in a foreign land, among strange gods. The natural heart screams one question over and over: *How long?* And into that ache walks a letter from a prophet far away (Jeremiah 29), and a chorus of nearer, louder voices saying, "Not long now — you'll be home in two years." Everyone in that camp had to decide which voice carried the thoughts of God.

### The Type Established
- **Shadow (OT):** Judah carried captive into Babylon for seventy years, holding a written promise of return.
- **Substance (NT/End-time):** The Bride of Christ called out of Babylon (confusion / organized religion), holding the promise of the rapture and the restoration of the true apostolic faith.
- **Branham on this type:**
  > "Babylon appeared first in Genesis. Now, we have to watch Babylon. It appears over here in the Bible again about the middle of the Old Testament, and then comes on over into Revelations for the last days, Babylon. It begin first, was the Gates of God, it was called. Then it was called Babylon, confusion."
  > *— 1953-11-10 · I Will Restore · ¶9*

### Person Profile — Jeremiah
- **Visible need:** A discouraged people demanding immediate deliverance; a prophet weeping over a nation that would not listen.
- **Invisible need:** Faith to deliver an unpopular Word — seventy years, not seventy days — against the comfortable lies of the false prophets.
- **Motive:** Love for God's people and absolute loyalty to the Word that came to him.
- **Intention:** To anchor the captives in God's actual plan so they would not waste their captivity chasing a counterfeit hope.
- **What God saw:** A true prophet who would stay with the Word no matter the trend of the day — the very mark God honors.

> "A true prophet stays with the Word. Whatever the Word says, they stay right with it. No matter what the trend of the day, or what the modern church says... the true prophet stays right with the Word."
> *— 1961-10-01 · It Becometh Us To Fulfil All Righteousness · ¶57*

## POINT 1 — GOD ALWAYS HAS A PLAN (THE EXPECTED END)

**Key Scripture:** "For I know the thoughts that I think toward you, saith the LORD, thoughts of peace, and not of evil, to give you an expected end." — Jeremiah 29:11

### The Human Need (Humanistic)
- The captives felt forgotten — driven, scattered, punished. (Jer 29:14, "all the places whither I have driven you")
- When trouble comes, the heart's first lie is, *God has no plan for me anymore.*
- Application: every believer in a hard season needs to hear that the captivity itself was foreknown, measured, and bounded by God.

### Divine Revelation
**The Word says:**
> "For thus saith the LORD, That after seventy years be accomplished at Babylon I will visit you, and perform my good word toward you, in causing you to return to this place." — Jeremiah 29:10

**The type unfolds:**
Notice — God set the captivity (seventy years), and God set the return. The same hand that allowed Babylon set the limit of Babylon. Why was Israel carried captive? For breaking the covenant. When? In stages under Nebuchadnezzar, climaxing in Zedekiah's day. When did they return? When the seventy years were "accomplished," exactly as the prophet said. The plan had a beginning, a measured middle, and a promised end. So does the Church's journey out of Babylon: a falling away, a calling out, and a sure restoration.

**From the Message:**
> "And He promised that He would return and bring the people, from scattered all over the earth after those seven years, back into Jerusalem where they left from; and He did it just that way. That's right."
> *— 1963-09-01 · Desperations · ¶32*

**Sub-points:**
- God's thoughts toward His people are "peace, and not of evil" — even in the captivity.
- An "expected end" means the outcome is already settled in the mind of God; you are waiting on a promise, not a possibility.
- The plan includes the hard middle, not only the happy ending — Babylon was in the plan.

### THUS SAITH THE LORD — The Vision
Bride of Christ, your captivity is not an accident and it is not the end of the story. The God who numbered Judah's seventy years has numbered your days in Babylon, and He has an expected end for you — a rapture, a glorified body, a homecoming to the very Pentecost you were driven from. His thoughts toward you this hour are thoughts of peace. The plan stands.

## POINT 2 — THERE IS ALWAYS A VOICE SENT TO SEDUCE YOU FROM THE PLAN

**Key Scripture:** "Beloved, believe not every spirit, but try the spirits whether they are of God: because many false prophets are gone out into the world." — 1 John 4:1

### The Human Need (Humanistic)
- In the very chapter (Jer 29), false prophets in Babylon were telling the captives, "It'll be short — rebel, resist, you'll be home soon."
- The seducing voice is always *easier and faster* than the revealed Word.
- Application: a believer under pressure is most vulnerable to the lie that flatters his impatience.

### Divine Revelation
**The Word says:**
> "And I saw three unclean spirits like frogs come out of the mouth of the dragon, and out of the mouth of the beast, and out of the mouth of the false prophet." — Revelation 16:13

**The type unfolds:**
The most dangerous lie does not come from the world; it comes dressed as a prophet. In 1 Kings 13 a young prophet of God carried a clear command — eat no bread, drink no water, return another way. He held the plan perfectly... until an *old prophet* met him and said, "An angel spake unto me" — and lied. The young man traded the voice he had heard from God for the voice of a man claiming a fresher revelation, and it cost him his life. A prophet lying to a prophet — that is the precise picture of the last-day seducing spirit: enough of the true Word to make the false believable.

**From the Message:**
> "False prophets bring false words. What do they do?... False prophets keep enough of the true Word to be false... The false prophets has enough of the true Word to make it false, because the Bible said that they'd have a form of godliness."
> *— 1961-12-31 · You Must Be Born Again · ¶88*

**Sub-points:**
- The seducing spirit is *religious* — it quotes angels, claims revelation, has a form of godliness (2 Tim 3:5).
- It always offers a shortcut around the cross-shaped plan of God (1 King 13: "come home, eat bread" instead of "go another way").
- "Try the spirits" — the test is never sincerity or signs, but whether the voice agrees with the already-revealed Word.

### THUS SAITH THE LORD — The Vision
The three frog spirits are croaking over the whole earth right now, gathering the world to a counterfeit unity. Bride, the wolf does not come in wolf's clothing — he comes in sheep's wool, with an open Bible and a sweeter, quicker promise. Do not give heed to seducing spirits. The same God who gave you the Message gave you the discernment to try every spirit by it.

## POINT 3 — WHILE YOU WAIT: DO THE ORDINARY THINGS OF FAITH

**Key Scripture:** "Build ye houses, and dwell in them; and plant gardens, and eat the fruit of them; Take ye wives, and beget sons and daughters... and seek the peace of the city whither I have caused you to be carried away captive." — Jeremiah 29:5-7 (read in full)

### The Human Need (Humanistic)
- The captives wanted drama: rebellion, escape, an instant miracle. God told them to *build, plant, marry, raise children, pray for peace.*
- We despise the ordinary. We want the spectacular while we wait.
- Application: most of the waiting season is lived not in visions but in faithful daily obedience.

### Divine Revelation
**The Word says:**
> "And seek the peace of the city whither I have caused you to be carried away captive, and pray unto the LORD for it." — Jeremiah 29:7

**The type unfolds:**
Why such homely instructions — build, dwell, plant, harvest, marry, raise a generation? Because God's plan is performed *through* faithful ordinary life, not around it. The false prophets offered excitement and an early exit; God prescribed patience, productivity, and prayer. The captives who built houses were the ones still standing, still believing, still pressing in (Luke 16:16) when the seventy years were accomplished. Waiting on the Lord is not idleness — it is settled, working, praying faith.

**From the Message:**
> "Wait on the Lord. He will provide for you. What do you hear? Can you hear the voice?... [The bear] don't grow any snowshoes... God makes a way for him. He listens."
> *— 1959-06-09 · What Hearest Thou, Elijah? · ¶38*

**Sub-points:**
- Build and plant: keep living by faith — raise your family, do your work, serve your church — captivity is no excuse to quit.
- Pray for the peace of the city: intercede where God has placed you instead of cursing it.
- "Every man presseth into it" (Luke 16:16) — the kingdom is pressed into, not drifted into.

### THUS SAITH THE LORD — The Vision
Bride, you are not called to sit idle on the riverbank weeping until the rapture; you are called to build, to plant, to raise a godly seed, to pray, to keep the fires of the Token burning in your house while you wait. The waiting is part of the plan. Be found working when the Master comes.

## POINT 4 — HOW YOU KNOW SOMEONE IS SENT FROM GOD

**Key Scripture:** "And ye shall seek me, and find me, when ye shall search for me with all your heart." — Jeremiah 29:13

### The Human Need (Humanistic)
- Two prophets, opposite messages — both claiming "Thus saith the Lord." The captives had to discern.
- Sincerity is not proof; signs are not proof; the devil can impersonate the gifts.
- Application: in an hour of many voices, the believer's safety is the ability to test the messenger by the Word.

### Divine Revelation
**The Word says:**
> "Beloved, believe not every spirit, but try the spirits whether they are of God." — 1 John 4:1

**The type unfolds:**
Jeremiah's mark was simple: his word came to pass and his word stayed with the covenant. The false prophets of Babylon flattered the flesh and were proven liars by the seventy years. Discernment in the last day is not suspicion of everyone — it is measuring every voice against the revealed Word. The devil can impersonate the gift, but he cannot be the Holy Ghost, and he cannot stay perfectly with the whole Word.

**From the Message:**
> "We must have discernment of spirit, to see a person what they're trying to do. Are they trying to glorify God? Are they trying to glorify themselves?... Believe not every spirit, but try the spirits whether they are of God."
> *— 1960-03-08 · Discernment Of Spirit · ¶24*

**Sub-points:**
- Test the fruit and the motive: self-glory unmasks the false; God-glory marks the true.
- Test by vindication of the Word: does it come to pass, and does it agree with the whole Scripture?
- A true messenger drives you *to* the Word and *to* seeking God with all your heart — not to himself.

### THUS SAITH THE LORD — The Vision
This is the hour Jesus warned would almost deceive the very elect. But the elect cannot be finally fooled, because the elect hold the Word. Bride, you know His voice because His voice is the Word vindicated. Try every spirit — and rest in the one that points you back to seeking God with all your heart.

## POINT 5 — SEEK HIM WITH ALL YOUR HEART AND FIND THE EXPECTED END

**Key Scripture:** "And I will be found of you, saith the LORD: and I will turn away your captivity." — Jeremiah 29:14

### The Human Need (Humanistic)
- A half-hearted seeker is the easiest prey for the seducing voice.
- The promise has a condition: "when ye shall search for me with all your heart."
- Application: it takes desperation, not casual church-going, to lay hold of the plan of God.

### Divine Revelation
**The Word says:**
> "Then shall ye call upon me, and ye shall go and pray unto me, and I will hearken unto you." — Jeremiah 29:12

**The type unfolds:**
God bound His promise of return to a whole-hearted search. The captives who merely complained stayed captive in spirit; those who sought Him with all their heart found Him and were gathered home. Branham preached this very text under the title "Desperations" — for it takes desperation to break through the noise of the false prophets and lay hold of the real plan. The expected end belongs to the all-out seeker.

**From the Message:**
> "Now, 'When you seek me with all your heart, then I'll be found.'... since that time the kingdom of God is preached, and every man presses into it... Not just simply walks into it easily, but it's got to be pressed into."
> *— 1963-09-01 · Desperations · ¶31*

**Sub-points:**
- The condition is the cure for seduction: a heart all-in on God has no room for a counterfeit.
- "Call... pray... seek... search" — an escalating desperation that God promises to answer.
- The end is guaranteed for the seeker: "I will turn away your captivity."

### THUS SAITH THE LORD — The Vision
Bride, the rapture is not for the casual; it is for the desperate — those who, surrounded by every seducing voice, press into God with all their heart. Seek Him like that, and the same God who turned Judah's captivity will turn yours. Your expected end is sure, and it is glorious.

## CONCLUSION

### The Epiphany — The Question Answered

> When you find yourself in a captivity you did not choose — far from the place of blessing, surrounded by voices promising you a quicker way out — how do you know whether the path before you is God's plan, or a deception sent to turn you from it?
>
> "You know by the Word. God's plan is always written, vindicated, and bounded by His own promise; the seduction always offers a shorter, easier way around it. Stay with the revealed Word, do the ordinary work of faith, try every spirit, and seek Him with all your heart — and the God whose thoughts toward you are peace will bring you to your expected end."

### Summary

- **Point 1:** God always has a plan — a foreknown, measured captivity with a promised, expected end.
- **Point 2:** There is always a seducing voice — even a prophet lying to a prophet — offering a shortcut around the plan.
- **Point 3:** While you wait, do the ordinary things of faith — build, plant, raise a seed, pray, press in.
- **Point 4:** Know the true messenger by trying the spirit against the vindicated Word, not by signs or sincerity.
- **Point 5:** Seek Him with all your heart, and the God of the expected end will turn away your captivity.

> **The heart of this message:** God's plan is settled and good; stay with His Word, refuse every seducing voice, and seek Him with all your heart until the expected end comes to pass.

## 1 King 13
1 Kings 13 — Summary, Lessons & Applications

  The Story

  A unnamed man of God from Judah is sent by God to Bethel to prophesy against Jeroboam's false altar. He delivers the
  word boldly, even when Jeroboam stretches out his hand to arrest him and it withers. God vindicates him with a sign.
  Jeroboam invites him home to eat and be rewarded — the man of God refuses, saying God specifically commanded him: eat
  no bread, drink no water, return not by the same way.

  An old prophet in Bethel hears what happened. He rides after the man of God and says: "I am a prophet also... an angel
  told me to bring you back to eat." It was a lie. The man of God believed him, turned back, ate and drank. On his way
  home a lion killed him — yet left his body untouched, standing over it as a sign.

  The old prophet buried him and mourned, saying: "It is the man of God, who was disobedient unto the word of the LORD."

  ---
  Life Lessons

  1. Your assignment comes with specific instructions — honour them exactly.
  God told the man of God not just what to do but how to leave. Partial obedience is disobedience. The route home
  matters as much as the mission itself.

  2. A fresh "word" that contradicts your last word from God is not from God.
  The man of God had a clear, direct word. The old prophet brought a competing word dressed in prophetic authority. The
  rule: a new voice cannot override a word God already gave you — especially when it's convenient and flattering.

  3. The devil uses prophets, not just obvious sinners, to pull you off course.
  The seducing voice came from inside the faith community — from someone who knew the language, had a track record, and
  looked legitimate. This is the wolf in sheep's clothing dynamic your notes flag.

  4. The sincerity of the messenger does not validate the message.
  We don't know the old prophet's motive — possibly jealousy, curiosity, or self-justification. But his sincerity or
  prophetic office didn't make his lie true. Try the spirits, not the credentials.

  5. Disobedience has consequences even when you were deceived.
  The man of God was lied to, yet still paid the price. This is sobering: God holds us responsible to test every voice
  against His already-revealed Word. Deception is not an automatic excuse — it is a warning to be more anchored, not
  less.

  6. The old prophet mourned what he caused.
  After the man of God died, the old prophet wept and honoured him — "he cried yet for his sons after him, Lay my bones
  beside his bones." Even the deceiver recognized the man of God's true calling. What a tragic irony: the man who pulled
  him off the Word ended up honouring his bones.

  ---
  Applications for Your Sermon

  This type fits perfectly into Point 2 of your outline — the seducing voice always comes dressed in something familiar
  and spiritual. The false prophets in Jeremiah's day were not pagans; they were claiming to speak for Jehovah. The
  three frog spirits (Rev 16:13) come out of the mouth — they come as words, prophecies, "new light." The test is always
  the same: does this agree with the Word God already gave you?

  The takeaway for the Bride: settle your word before you leave home — so when a voice meets you on the road, you
  already know the answer.
