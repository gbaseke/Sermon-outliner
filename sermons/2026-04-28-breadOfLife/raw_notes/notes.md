# Sermon Notes — The Bread of Life

## Theme
The Bread of Life — Christ Himself, the True Bread come down from heaven, given for the life of the world.

## Primary Scripture
**John 6:26-71 (KJV)** — The full discourse following the feeding of the five thousand:
- The crowd seeking Jesus for loaves, not for the sign
- "Labour not for the meat which perisheth, but for that meat which endureth unto everlasting life"
- The demand for a sign — "What sign shewest thou?"
- The reference to manna in the wilderness — "He gave them bread from heaven to eat"
- Jesus' declaration: "I am the bread of life"
- The murmuring of the Jews — "Is not this Jesus, the son of Joseph, whose father and mother we know?"
- "Except ye eat the flesh of the Son of man, and drink his blood, ye have no life in you"
- The offence — "This is an hard saying; who can hear it?"
- The falling away — "From that time many of his disciples went back, and walked no more with him"
- The remnant — "Then said Jesus unto the twelve, Will ye also go away?"
- Peter's confession — "Lord, to whom shall we go? thou hast the words of eternal life"
- The betrayer foreshadowed — "Have not I chosen you twelve, and one of you is a devil?"

## Central Question
What kind of bread are we hungry for? — the bread that perishes, or the Bread of Life?

## Types and Shadows to Develop
- **Manna in the wilderness** (Exodus 16) — type of Christ, the hidden manna, fresh every morning, gathered before the sun rose hot
- **The corn of wheat** (John 12:24) — must fall into the ground and die
- **Melchizedek bringing forth bread and wine to Abraham** (Genesis 14) — the priesthood that ministers the Bread
- **Bethlehem — the "house of bread"** — birthplace of David, birthplace of Christ
- **Boaz feeding Ruth at harvest** (Ruth 2) — the Kinsman Redeemer giving bread to the Gentile bride

## Key Themes / Threads
- The difference between seeking the loaves and seeking the Lord
- The sovereignty of God: "No man can come to me, except the Father which hath sent me draw him"
- The sifting — the Word separates the disciples from the multitude
- The predestinated seed hears the hard saying and stays
- The flesh profiteth nothing — it is the Spirit that quickeneth — the Word made flesh, eaten by faith
- Eating His flesh and drinking His blood = receiving the revealed Word into the inner man
- This hour: the Bride feeds on the unsealed Word, the hidden manna of the seventh seal

## People to Examine
- **The multitude** — visible need: hunger; invisible need: to know the Sender of the Bread
- **The disciples who went back** — offended at the Word; chose easier bread
- **Peter and the twelve** — having no other place to go; tasted the words of eternal life
- **Judas** — ate the bread but was never quickened by it; a devil among the chosen

## Tone
Warm. Pastoral. A shepherd setting the table for hungry sheep. End with an altar call to eat — to receive the Word for this hour.
