## THIS HOUR — PROPHETIC ANCHOR

We are living in the closing moments of the Laodicean age — an hour when the world is feeding on every kind of bread except the Bread that came down from heaven. There is bread of education, bread of programs, bread of denominational creed, bread of the latest religious sensation. But the Bride is feeding on something else — the unsealed Word, the hidden manna of the seventh seal, fresh from heaven for this hour. The Father is drawing His own to the Word, and as in John 6, the Word itself is the great sifter. The multitude has gone back. The Bride remains at the table.

---

## INTRODUCTION — SETTING THE TYPE

**Tone:** Warm and pastoral — open the heart before opening the Word. Sit down with the people the way Jesus sat down on that hillside.

### The Human Scene (Humanistic)

Picture the morning after the miracle. Five thousand had gone to sleep with full stomachs. They had eaten loaves they did not bake, fish they did not catch. And when they woke up, the first thing they did was hunt for the Man who had multiplied the bread. They got into little boats. They crossed over the lake. They asked, "Rabbi, when camest thou hither?"

But Jesus knew them. He looked through the crowd and said, "Verily, verily, I say unto you, Ye seek me, not because ye saw the miracles, but because ye did eat of the loaves, and were filled" (John 6:26). They came hungry — but for the wrong bread. They wanted another meal, not another miracle. They wanted full bellies, not full hearts. And the Master, who feeds the hungry, refuses to fill stomachs that have closed their souls. He sets aside the loaves and lifts up the True Bread: "I am the bread of life."

### The Type Established
- **Shadow (OT):** Bethlehem — the "house of bread"; the manna falling fresh every morning in the wilderness; Melchizedek bringing forth bread and wine to Abraham after the battle; Boaz dropping handfuls on purpose for Ruth in the barley field.
- **Substance (NT/End-time):** Christ Himself — the Word made flesh — born in the house of bread, broken on Calvary, served fresh every morning to His Bride, and revealed in this hour as the unsealed Word for the predestinated seed.

**Branham on this type:**
> "And the name Bethlehem means 'the house of God's bread.' Now, how fitting that is to Jesus, the Bread of Eternal Life. Christ is the Bread of Life. We all believe that, don't we? How fitting Bethlehem there, the bread center of the world, was the bread center of Eternal Life. That's why the King had to be born there. He said, in St. John 6:35, 'I am the Bread of Life that come from God out of heaven. Your fathers did eat manna in the wilderness, and all dead. But this Bread, if a man eats this Bread, he shall never die.'"
> *— 1963-12-14 · Why little Bethlehem? · ¶51*

### Person Profile — The Multitude
- **Visible need:** Empty stomachs. They were hungry; they were poor; they wanted another miracle meal.
- **Invisible need:** They needed to know the Sender of the bread, not just the bread of the Sender. They needed eyes to see the sign, not just the supper.
- **Motive:** Self-preservation. "If we follow this Man, we'll never have to work for bread again."
- **Intention:** To make Him a king after the flesh — a baker-king, a welfare-king.
- **What God saw:** A crowd that wanted the gift but not the Giver; a crowd that would forsake Him before sunset when He stopped passing out loaves and started passing out the Word.

## POINT 1 — TWO HUNGERS, TWO BREADS

**Key Scripture:** "Labour not for the meat which perisheth, but for that meat which endureth unto everlasting life, which the Son of man shall give unto you: for him hath God the Father sealed." — John 6:27

### The Human Need (Humanistic)
- Every human being is born hungry. We come into the world crying, wanting milk before we want anything else.
- But there are two hungers in every man: a hunger of the body that demands bread that perishes, and a hunger of the soul that nothing perishable can ever fill.
- A person can be a millionaire and still go to bed empty. A person can have three meals a day and starve to death on the inside.
- We chase loaves all our lives — promotions, possessions, popularity — and wake up still hungry.

### Divine Revelation
**The Word says:**
> "Jesus answered them and said, Verily, verily, I say unto you, Ye seek me, not because ye saw the miracles, but because ye did eat of the loaves, and were filled." — John 6:26

> "Man shall not live by bread alone, but by every word that proceedeth out of the mouth of God." — Matthew 4:4

**The type unfolds:**
The multitude is following Jesus, but they are following the loaves. They love what Jesus does, not what Jesus is. And the Lord, who knows the heart, refuses to feed the wrong appetite. He will not multiply loaves for a soul that needs to be born again. He shifts the menu — from natural bread to the Bread of Life — and the crowd thins out.

**From the Message:**
> "When the church is hungry, God wants His children fed with the bread of life, not with some theory, but with the bread of life. Who---or what---is this bread of life? Jesus said, 'I am the bread of life that came from God out of heaven.' Said, 'Your fathers ate manna in the wilderness... and they're every one dead. But I am the bread of life that come from God out of heaven. If any man eats this bread, he shall never die.'"
> *— 1956-03-11 · The true and false vines · ¶20*

**Sub-points:**
- Bread that perishes feeds a body that perishes; Bread of Life feeds a soul that lives forever.
- The world has a thousand bakeries; God has only one Bread.
- A church seeking loaves is no longer seeking the Lord.

### THUS SAITH THE LORD — The Vision
Bride of Christ, hear it this morning: God is sifting His people in this hour by the kind of bread they are hungry for. He is taking the loaves off the table, and laying down the unsealed Word. Those who came for the loaves are walking back to Egypt. But those who came for Him — they are pressing in closer than ever. Stop labouring for the meat which perisheth. Hunger and thirst after righteousness, and the Father shall fill you.

## POINT 2 — THE TRUE BREAD OUT OF HEAVEN — THE TYPE OF MANNA

**Key Scripture:** "Moses gave you not that bread from heaven; but my Father giveth you the true bread from heaven. For the bread of God is he which cometh down from heaven, and giveth life unto the world." — John 6:32-33

### The Human Need (Humanistic)
- Israel wandered in a wilderness with no fields, no harvest, no bakeries.
- Every morning, before the sun rose hot, they had to gather fresh manna — what they gathered yesterday turned wormy by today.
- That is the human condition exactly: yesterday's experience cannot feed today's hunger. Yesterday's revival cannot pay today's bills with God.

### Divine Revelation
**The Word says:**
> "And when the dew that lay was gone up, behold, upon the face of the wilderness there lay a small round thing, as small as the hoar frost on the ground." — Exodus 16:14

> "I am that bread of life. Your fathers did eat manna in the wilderness, and are dead. This is the bread which cometh down from heaven, that a man may eat thereof, and not die." — John 6:48-50

**The type unfolds:**
The manna in the wilderness was a perfect shadow of Christ. Small. Round. White. Sweet as honey. It came down at night and lay on the ground in the morning, and every man had to bend down and pick it up for himself. No one could gather it for another. And it had to be fresh — yesterday's manna turned to worms. So Christ comes down out of heaven every morning as a fresh revelation to His Bride. The Word is not stale. It is alive. It is for now.

**From the Message:**
> "Oh, notice, Israel received a new fresh bread every night, coming down from heaven for their journey. Christ is our Life, Bread of Life, and every day we receive a freshness from Christ, from heaven, the Holy Spirit coming down upon the believer every day. Fresh! Yesterday's experience, many of us live on yesterday's experience. We mustn't do that. It's today's experience! That's what's the matter with our denominations, they're living on the experience of John Wesley, upon the experience of Dwight Moody..."
> *— 1963-12-14 · Why little Bethlehem? · ¶52*

**Sub-points:**
- Manna fell every morning — the Bride must feed every morning.
- Manna was gathered before the sun got hot — the Bride must hunger early, before the cares of the world melt the Word away.
- The omer was hidden in the ark — the **hidden manna** is the special revelation kept for the overcomers in the last age.

### THUS SAITH THE LORD — The Vision
The hidden manna of Revelation 2:17 is being served at the King's table in this hour. It is not the manna of the multitude — it is the manna of the overcomer, the priest's bread, the special revelation of who Christ is for this age. The Bride is eating something the world has never tasted, something the denominations have no recipe for. Beloved, this is your hour. Eat the hidden manna. Eat it fresh.

> "You're feasting at a fountain that the world knows nothing about. You're drinking that somebody knows ... the world knows nothing about. You're eating hidden manna that the world never even knowed nothing of it, for you're an eagle. You're quickened..."
> *— 1965-04-18 · It is the Rising of the Sun · ¶80*

## POINT 3 — THE HOUSE OF BREAD AND THE CORN OF WHEAT

**Key Scripture:** "And Jesus said unto them, I am the bread of life: he that cometh to me shall never hunger; and he that believeth on me shall never thirst." — John 6:35

### The Human Need (Humanistic)
- A baby in Bethlehem. A peasant girl, a stable, a manger. The world saw nothing.
- We are people who measure greatness by stages and platforms. God measured His greatness by a feeding-trough.
- Why does the heart respond to a baby in a manger more than to a king on a throne? Because deep down, every soul knows the Bread had to be broken before it could feed the world.

### Divine Revelation
**The Word says:**
> "But thou, Bethlehem Ephratah, though thou be little among the thousands of Judah, yet out of thee shall he come forth unto me that is to be ruler in Israel; whose goings forth have been from of old, from everlasting." — Micah 5:2

> "Verily, verily, I say unto you, Except a corn of wheat fall into the ground and die, it abideth alone: but if it die, it bringeth forth much fruit." — John 12:24

**The type unfolds:**
Bethlehem — *Beth-Elohim-lechem* — the house of God's bread. The Bread of Life had to be born in the bread basket. Every road leading to Bethlehem was a road of wheat fields. And the King who was born there was Himself the corn of wheat, who must fall into the ground and die. He was kneaded in the womb of a virgin, baked in the oven of suffering, broken at Calvary, and now He is served fresh every morning to a hungry Bride.

**From the Message:**
> "We must have something fresh from Christ, His Word today for this hour, see. He is our freshness, our Bread that falls every day from heaven upon the believer. He is our Bethlehem, God's house of Eternal Life Bread. Christ was born in Bethlehem, and became God's house of Eternal Life Bread. He is the Bread of Life. He is our Bethlehem. Christ is our Bethlehem."
> *— 1963-12-14 · Why little Bethlehem? · ¶53*

> "Then all true believing sons of God are born in Bethlehem with Him. If Christ had to become the Bread of Life, to be born in Bethlehem, which is Life's Bread House, then every one of true believers in Christ is born in Christ, they are born in God's Bethlehem."
> *— 1963-12-14 · Why little Bethlehem? · ¶55*

**Sub-points:**
- Bethlehem is not just a place; it is a position. Every born-again believer is born in the house of bread.
- The corn of wheat had to fall — the Bread had to be broken — before any of us could eat.
- Where the Bread is, the Bride is born.

### THUS SAITH THE LORD — The Vision
We who believe are born in Bethlehem. We are born where the Bread is broken. The world is starving in Jerusalem of denomination, but the Bride is feasting in Bethlehem of revelation. The corn of wheat fell — and now a great harvest of mature sons and daughters is rising in this last hour to be made one with the Word that fell into the ground for them.

## POINT 4 — MELCHIZEDEK'S BREAD AND BOAZ'S HANDFULS — THE PRIESTLY AND THE KINSMAN BREAD

**Key Scripture:** "And Melchizedek king of Salem brought forth bread and wine: and he was the priest of the most high God." — Genesis 14:18

### The Human Need (Humanistic)
- After every battle, a soldier is hungry.
- After every spiritual war — every test, every trial, every fight against the flesh — the saint comes back tired and famished.
- And in that hour we need more than victory. We need somebody to set a table and serve us bread.

### Divine Revelation
**The Word says:**
> "And Melchizedek king of Salem brought forth bread and wine: and he was the priest of the most high God. And he blessed him..." — Genesis 14:18-19

> "And Boaz said unto her, At mealtime come thou hither, and eat of the bread, and dip thy morsel in the vinegar... And let fall also some of the handfuls of purpose for her, and leave them, that she may glean them..." — Ruth 2:14, 16

**The type unfolds:**
Two beautiful pictures of Christ feeding His own. Melchizedek — King of Salem, priest of the Most High, without father, without mother, without descent — came out to meet Abraham *after the battle was over* and served him bread and wine. That is communion. That is the same Christ, the eternal High Priest, ministering bread to the seed of Abraham after every battle. And then Boaz — the kinsman redeemer — saw Ruth the Gentile gleaning in his field, and *commanded his reapers to drop handfuls on purpose* for her. The Bride is fed by the Kinsman's deliberate generosity. He drops the Word on purpose where the Bride can find it.

**From the Message — Melchizedek:**
> "And notice what a type we find here. After the battle is over---after the real believer has fought the enemy, broke down the lines, and tried to capture his brother---the real seed of Abraham bringing back that wayward brother, Melchizedek come out and served wine and bread---communion---giving communion after the battle was over. Served the communion to him after the battle. A very beautiful type of when the battle is over here on earth. The victories have been won. Jesus said they would eat it new with us in the Father's kingdom..."
> *— 1964-04-02 · Jehovah-Jireh, part 1 · ¶37*

**From the Message — Boaz and Ruth:**
> "Naomi and Ruth come in just at barley season. Barley season was bread season!---the season when fresh bread was being served. And the church in this last days, through two thousand years of pagan teaching..."
> *— 1960-10-02 · The Kinsman Redeemer · ¶52*

**Sub-points:**
- Melchizedek's bread = the eternal priestly ministry of Christ to the spiritual seed of Abraham.
- Boaz's handfuls of purpose = the Word dropped deliberately by the Kinsman Redeemer for the Gentile Bride to glean.
- The Bride does not gather scraps by accident — every revelation is dropped *on purpose* for her.

### THUS SAITH THE LORD — The Vision
The Kinsman Redeemer is in the field this morning. He is dropping handfuls on purpose. Every quote, every Word, every revelation that finds your heart — that wasn't an accident. That was Boaz watching Ruth from the corner of His eye and saying, "Drop another handful right where she'll find it." The Bride is being fed deliberately by the Lord of the harvest, and the season is barley season — bread season — for this hour.

## POINT 5 — THE HARD SAYING AND THE GREAT SIFTING

**Key Scripture:** "Many therefore of his disciples, when they had heard this, said, This is an hard saying; who can hear it? ... From that time many of his disciples went back, and walked no more with him." — John 6:60, 66

### The Human Need (Humanistic)
- Everybody loves a sermon on God's love until God's Word starts cutting.
- The same crowd that ate the loaves on the hillside is offended on the synagogue floor.
- Human beings will follow a Christ who feeds them. They will not follow a Christ who feeds *on* them — who demands their flesh, their blood, their will, their everything.

### Divine Revelation
**The Word says:**
> "Except ye eat the flesh of the Son of man, and drink his blood, ye have no life in you." — John 6:53

> "It is the spirit that quickeneth; the flesh profiteth nothing: the words that I speak unto you, they are spirit, and they are life." — John 6:63

> "From that time many of his disciples went back, and walked no more with him." — John 6:66

**The type unfolds:**
This is the hour the Word becomes the great divider. The same Word that draws the elect offends the multitude. Jesus did not run after the seventy who left. He did not soften His message. He did not chase the crowd. He turned to the twelve and said, "Will ye also go away?" The Word itself is the sifter. It separates the predestinated seed from the religious crowd. Eating His flesh and drinking His blood means receiving the revealed Word into the inner man — and only those drawn by the Father can stomach it.

**From the Message:**
> "And here's these disciples standing there (them seventy) and watching Jesus do the things that He did and knowed the Scripture. Him telling them that that was the age that this was to take place. And then when He said something (the Son of man) ... What would you say when He begin to tell them about breaking the bread, and so forth, and went ahead telling them about great spiritual things? And they said, 'Oh, this is a hard saying.'"
> *— 1963-11-24 · Three kinds of believers · ¶59*

> "He said, 'What is it? the flesh that you're talking about profit nothing; it's the Spirit that quickens.' There you are. The Spirit quickens the Word. It's a Spirit, not the creed; the Spirit of ... the Holy Ghost quickens the Word to you and becomes alive, and there you are. You see it. By faith you see it."
> *— 1963-11-24 · Three kinds of believers · ¶48*

**Sub-points:**
- The Word always thins the crowd.
- The flesh profiteth nothing — natural reasoning will never digest spiritual revelation.
- "No man can come unto me, except it were given unto him of my Father" (John 6:65) — the Father draws; the Word sifts.

### Person Profile — The Disciples Who Went Back
- **Visible need:** A teacher, a leader, a meal ticket, a movement.
- **Invisible need:** A new birth — but they didn't want one.
- **Motive:** They wanted to be associated with the prophet of Galilee, but only on their terms.
- **Intention:** To follow Him until the cost got too high.
- **What God saw:** Seed that had no depth of earth; ground that was never tilled; hearts that came for loaves and could not stay for the Word.

### THUS SAITH THE LORD — The Vision
The same hard saying is being preached this morning. The Word for this hour is offending the multitude. Whole churches are walking back. Whole denominations are saying, "This is too hard." But the Bride — the predestinated seed, the elected, the called-out — she hears the hard saying and she eats it. She drinks it. She says with Peter, "Lord, where else can we go? Thou hast the words of eternal life."

## POINT 6 — THE PREDESTINATED SEED, THE BETRAYER, AND THE BELIEVER

**Key Scripture:** "Then said Jesus unto the twelve, Will ye also go away? Then Simon Peter answered him, Lord, to whom shall we go? thou hast the words of eternal life. And we believe and are sure that thou art that Christ, the Son of the living God." — John 6:67-69

### The Human Need (Humanistic)
- When the crowd thins out, the heart gets tested.
- It is one thing to believe with five thousand others. It is another thing to believe with eleven.
- And here is where every soul must answer: when the seventy walk back, when your friends turn their face, when the world calls the Word a hard saying — will you also go away?

### Divine Revelation
**The Word says:**
> "All that the Father giveth me shall come to me; and him that cometh to me I will in no wise cast out." — John 6:37

> "No man can come to me, except the Father which hath sent me draw him: and I will raise him up at the last day." — John 6:44

> "Have not I chosen you twelve, and one of you is a devil?" — John 6:70

**The type unfolds:**
Three kinds of people stood before Jesus that day:
- **The multitude** — came for loaves and went home empty.
- **The disciples who went back** — came for advancement and walked away offended.
- **The twelve** — drawn by the Father, holding on to nothing but His Word.

But even among the twelve, Judas stood. He ate the bread but was never quickened by it. He heard the Word but it never became flesh in him. He is the eternal warning — that proximity to the Bread is not the same as life from the Bread.

The pregnated person with the seed of God, the Word in there manifesting itself..."

### Person Profile — Peter
- **Visible need:** A leader, a future, a place in the kingdom.
- **Invisible need:** A revelation of who Jesus really was — and he had it. "Thou art the Christ, the Son of the living God."
- **Motive:** Pure attachment. He had nowhere else to go because nothing else satisfied.
- **Intention:** To stay — even when he didn't understand.
- **What God saw:** A predestinated seed. A man who would deny Him three times and yet still feed His sheep.

### Person Profile — Judas
- **Visible need:** Money, position, importance among the twelve.
- **Invisible need:** A heart that would never be quickened, no matter how close he sat to the Bread.
- **Motive:** Self-promotion masked as ministry. He carried the bag.
- **Intention:** To use Jesus, never to be used by Him.
- **What God saw:** "One of you is a devil." A son of perdition. A bread-eater who never became bread, a manna-handler who was never manna-fed.

### THUS SAITH THE LORD — The Vision
Bride of Christ, hear it: the same scene is happening in this hour. The multitude is walking. The seventy is offended. And the elect — the predestinated, the Father-drawn — are pressing in closer. There are still Judases at the table. There are still Peters at the table. The Word will divide between them before this hour closes. Hold fast. He that hath begun a good work in you will perform it until the day of Jesus Christ.

## CONCLUSION

### The Epiphany — The Question Answered

> **What kind of bread are you hungry for this morning — the bread that perishes, or the Bread of Life?**
>
> The Bread of Life is a Person. He is Christ — born in Bethlehem, the house of bread; broken as the corn of wheat at Calvary; ministered by Melchizedek's priestly hand after every battle; dropped on purpose by Boaz the Kinsman in the field where Ruth gleans; eaten fresh every morning as the hidden manna of this hour. And the Father is drawing every predestinated seed to this Bread — and only this Bread — that they may eat and never die. Hunger for Him. Eat Him. Live forever.

### Summary

- **Point 1 — Two Hungers, Two Breads:** The multitude came for loaves; the Bride must come for the Lord.
- **Point 2 — The True Bread Out of Heaven:** Christ is the manna fallen fresh every morning, and the hidden manna is the special revelation for the overcomers in this last age.
- **Point 3 — The House of Bread and the Corn of Wheat:** The Bread had to be born in Bethlehem and broken at Calvary so that every believer could be born in the house of bread with Him.
- **Point 4 — Melchizedek's Bread and Boaz's Handfuls:** The eternal Priest serves bread after every battle, and the Kinsman Redeemer drops handfuls on purpose for His Gentile Bride.
- **Point 5 — The Hard Saying and the Great Sifting:** The Word itself is the sifter; the multitude is offended, but the Bride eats the hard saying.
- **Point 6 — The Predestinated Seed, the Betrayer, and the Believer:** Three crowds stood before Jesus — only the drawn ones stayed; only the quickened ones lived.

> **The heart of this message:** Christ Himself is the Bread, the Bride is being fed fresh from heaven in this hour, and the Word itself is dividing between those who came for loaves and those who came for the Lord.

### A Closing Hymn

**Suggested hymn:** "Break Thou the Bread of Life"
**Why:** Every line of this old hymn is a prayer over John 6 — "Break Thou the bread of life, dear Lord, to me, As Thou didst break the loaves beside the sea..." It carries the exact mood of the sermon: the hungry soul, the broken Bread, the seeking heart that says with Peter, "Lord, to whom shall we go? Thou hast the words of eternal life."

- **The crossbreeding warning** — "Death comes by crossbreeding... To cross the Word with denomination is death." (1962-03-18 · The Spoken Word is the original seed · ¶205) — pairs with Point 5 if more weight is needed against denominational bread.

- **John 6:37 and 6:44 together** — "All that the Father giveth me shall come to me... No man can come to me, except the Father which hath sent me draw him..." Branham's repeated emphasis on the divine drawing should be held in reserve for the altar call: every hand that goes up was drawn by the Father.

- **Scripture for the altar call** — Revelation 3:20: "Behold, I stand at the door, and knock: if any man hear my voice, and open the door, I will come in to him, and will sup with him, and he with me." The Bread is at the door. He is asking to come and eat with you.
