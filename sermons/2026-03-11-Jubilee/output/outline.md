## THIS HOUR -- PROPHETIC ANCHOR

The trumpet of Jubilee is not a future event for the Bride of Christ -- it is sounding right now. Seven church ages have run their course, like seven Sabbaths of years, and we stand at the threshold of the fiftieth -- the Jubilee. The Seventh Seal has been opened, the mysteries have been revealed, and the Message of the hour is the Gospel trumpet calling every predestinated seed back to their original possession. What Adam lost in the Garden, the Kinsman Redeemer has purchased back, and the trumpet is announcing: "Return, every man, to your possession." The question is not whether the trumpet is sounding; the question is whether you have ears to hear it.

---

## INTRODUCTION -- SETTING THE TYPE

**Tone:** Warm and pastoral -- open the heart before opening the Word.

### The Human Scene (Humanistic)

Imagine an Israelite slave. He has been sold into bondage -- perhaps by his own poor choices, perhaps by circumstances beyond his control. He has forgotten the taste of freedom. His children were born in the master's house. Every morning he picks up the hoe, and every evening he sets it down. The whip cracks behind him, and he has come to believe that this is simply his life. Then, one day, a sound breaks through the hot air of the field -- the clear, unmistakable blast of a silver trumpet. The jubilee priest is riding through the land. And this man has a decision to make: Will he drop the hoe and go home, or will he stay?

That is the picture of so many of God's people tonight. They have heard the trumpet, but they are still hoeing. They have been told they are free, but they live as though they are bound. The chains are not on their wrists -- they are on their minds.

### The Type Established
- **Shadow (OT):** The Year of Jubilee (Leviticus 25) -- every fiftieth year, all slaves went free, all debts were cancelled, every man returned to his original possession.
- **Substance (NT/End-time):** Jesus Christ IS the Jubilee. He announced it in the synagogue at Nazareth (Luke 4:18-19). And the Message of this hour is the final Jubilee trumpet sounding to the Bride, calling her back to her original possession -- the full Word of God, the power of Pentecost, the fellowship with the Father that Adam walked in.
- **Branham on this type:**
  > "And Christ Jesus came to the earth to bring a jubilee to the human race, that through the preaching of the Gospel, hearing by faith, might set every slave to sin free."
  > *-- 1955-03-11 -- The Seal of Antichrist -- p37*

## POINT 1 -- THE MOUNTAIN WHERE GOD PROVIDES (MORIAH)

**Key Scripture:** "And he said, Take now thy son, thine only son Isaac, whom thou lovest, and get thee into the land of Moriah; and offer him there for a burnt offering upon one of the mountains which I will tell thee of." -- Genesis 22:2

### The Human Need (Humanistic)
- Every person carries something they cannot pay for -- a debt too large, a bondage too deep, a need too great for human resources.
- Abraham loved his son. That was not the problem. The problem was that God asked him to place the dearest thing in his life on the altar, and Abraham did not know how God would work it out.
- We can relate to that tonight. We do not always see the end from the beginning. We hold things precious that God is asking us to surrender.

### Divine Revelation
**The Word says:**
> "And Abraham called the name of that place Jehovahjireh: as it is said to this day, In the mount of the LORD it shall be seen." -- Genesis 22:14

**The type unfolds:**

Mount Moriah is one of the most sacred pieces of ground on the earth, because it is where God's provision has been displayed across the ages. On Moriah, Abraham offered Isaac, and God provided the ram -- a type of Christ, the Lamb of God. On that same ridge, Solomon built the Temple -- the dwelling place of God among men. And on Moriah's extension -- Calvary -- Jesus Christ, God's only Son, was offered as the final atonement. Moriah means "chosen by God" or "seen of God." It is the mountain where God looks down and sees the need, and provides. The Jubilee trumpet sounds from this very place, because without the atonement there is no Jubilee.

**From the Message:**
> "Behind it now, we see the picture He was painting: God giving His only Son."
> *-- 1954-04-04 -- God has a Provided Way -- p35*

**Sub-points:**
- Abraham on Moriah: God provides the sacrifice (the ram -- a type of Christ)
- Solomon on Moriah: God provides the dwelling place (the Temple -- a type of the Body of Christ)
- Christ on Moriah (Calvary): God provides the atonement (the Lamb -- the substance of all the types)
- The Jubilee trumpet sounded on the Day of Atonement -- it is rooted in what God did on this mountain

### THUS SAITH THE LORD -- The Vision

Beloved, the God who provided on Moriah is the same God who has provided for you tonight. Every shadow that was cast on that mountain pointed to one thing: the Lamb of God that taketh away the sin of the world. And if the atonement has been made -- and it has -- then the trumpet has a right to sound. You have a right to go free. Not because of what you have done, but because of what He has provided on the mountain. The Bride of Christ does not approach this hour empty-handed. She comes with the blood of the Lamb and the Word of testimony. She comes back to Moriah -- back to the place of provision -- and there she finds that everything she ever needed was already waiting.

## POINT 2 -- THE HOUSE OF MERCY (BETHESDA)

**Key Scripture:** "Now there is at Jerusalem by the sheep market a pool, which is called in the Hebrew tongue Bethesda, having five porches. In these lay a great multitude of impotent folk, of blind, halt, withered, waiting for the moving of the water." -- John 5:2-3

### The Human Need (Humanistic)
- The name "Bethesda" means "House of Mercy" or "House of Grace." Think of it -- a building dedicated to mercy, and yet it was full of suffering. Five porches, representing the five books of Moses (the Law), and under those porches lay multitudes who could not be healed by the Law.
- These were not casual visitors. They were desperate. Blind, halt, withered -- waiting. Some had been there for years. They were close to the water but could not get in. They had a form of religion (they were at the right place), but they had no power to be made whole.
- There are people sitting in churches tonight in the same condition: close to something, waiting for something, but unable to lay hold of it. They are in the house of mercy, but mercy has not yet touched them.

### Divine Revelation
**The Word says:**
> "When Jesus saw him lie, and knew that he had been now a long time in that case, he saith unto him, Wilt thou be made whole?" -- John 5:6

**The type unfolds:**

Five porches -- the five books of the Law. The Law could show you your condition but could not heal you. You could lie under its shadow all your life and never be made whole. What was needed was not another stirring of the water, but the Word Himself walking through. Jesus came to Bethesda and did not heal the multitudes. He found one man -- the man the Father showed Him -- and spoke one word to him. That is the sovereignty of God. The Jubilee trumpet does not save the world; it calls out those who will hear. Jesus bypassed the multitudes and went straight to the one with faith. That is a type of this hour: the Message does not convert the masses; it calls out a Bride.

**From the Message:**
> "Could you imagine a person full of mercy and compassion, walk by the twisted, and lame, and blind, and withered, trying to get into the pool; and just walked on by them and went to a man that He knowed where he was at? Healed him and left that whole multitude?"
> *-- 1953-08-29 -- The testimony of Jesus Christ -- p45*

**Sub-points:**
- Bethesda (House of Mercy) with five porches = the Law, which could not heal
- The multitudes waited for the moving of the water = religious form, waiting for a move of God they could not enter
- Jesus walked past the multitudes to one man = sovereignty, election, the Father's will
- "Wilt thou be made whole?" = the personal call of Jubilee -- God does not broadcast generically; He speaks to the one He has chosen

### THUS SAITH THE LORD -- The Vision

Church, we are not waiting for the moving of the water. The Word Himself has walked into the house. He is not asking the multitudes a question tonight. He is asking you -- you who have been lying in your condition for years, you who have been close to the truth but never quite touched it -- "Wilt thou be made whole?" That is the Jubilee question. Not "Can you get yourself in the water?" Not "Are you strong enough?" But "Do you want to be free?" The Bride does not need an angel to trouble the water. She has the Word made manifest, walking in her midst, calling her by name. Rise up. Take up your bed. Walk. This is Jubilee.

## POINT 3 -- THE MOUNT OF OLIVES: WHERE THE KING WAS REJECTED AND WHERE HE SHALL RETURN

**Key Scripture:** "And his feet shall stand in that day upon the mount of Olives, which is before Jerusalem on the east, and the mount of Olives shall cleave in the midst thereof toward the east and toward the west, and there shall be a very great valley; and half of the mountain shall remove toward the north, and half of it toward the south." -- Zechariah 14:4

### The Human Need (Humanistic)
- David, rejected by his own people, his own son Absalom rising against him, went up over the Mount of Olives weeping. A king without a throne. A father betrayed by his own household.
- Eight hundred years later, Jesus sat on that same mountain and wept over Jerusalem: "O Jerusalem, Jerusalem, thou that killest the prophets, and stonest them which are sent unto thee, how often would I have gathered thy children together, even as a hen gathereth her chickens under her wings, and ye would not!" (Matthew 23:37). A King rejected by His own.
- On the Mount of Olives, Jesus taught the Beatitudes and the great Olivet discourse -- the sermon that looks all the way to the end of time.
- From this mountain He ascended. To this mountain He shall return.

### Divine Revelation
**The Word says:**
> "Which also said, Ye men of Galilee, why stand ye gazing up into heaven? this same Jesus, which is taken up from you into heaven, shall so come in like manner as ye have seen him go into heaven." -- Acts 1:11

**The type unfolds:**

David on the Mount of Olives is one of the most striking types of Christ in all the Old Testament. A rejected king, despised by his own household, weeping over the city he loved. Brother Branham drew this out powerfully. And yet David returned to his throne. The rejection was not the end of the story -- it was the middle. Jesus, the Son of David, was rejected on the same mountain, but Zechariah 14:4 says His feet shall stand there again. The Mount of Olives is the mountain of rejection AND restoration. That is the very heart of Jubilee: what was lost shall be returned; what was rejected shall be received; the King is coming back.

**From the Message:**
> "The Son of David sat on the same mountain, when David was rejected as king and went up over the hill (Mount of Olives), looking back, weeping as a rejected king... How perfect of the Son of God eight hundred years later. A rejected King among his own people sat on the mount and wept over Jerusalem, as a rejected King."
> *-- 1960-12-04 -- Church Ages, part 1: Revelation Chapter 1 -- p42*

**Sub-points:**
- David weeping on Olivet = Christ weeping over Jerusalem (a type of rejection)
- Jesus teaching on the Mount of Olives = the Word going forth before judgment
- Jesus ascending from Olivet = the King departing
- Jesus returning to Olivet (Zechariah 14:4) = the restoration of all things -- the ultimate Jubilee
- Indictment - Message

### THUS SAITH THE LORD -- The Vision

The Mount of Olives is the mountain of the in-between. It is where the King was rejected and from where He shall return. We are living in that in-between right now. The world has rejected the Message. The denominations have rejected the Word. But hear me tonight: the same feet that ascended from Olivet are coming back to Olivet. And when they do, this earth shall split open, and the New Jerusalem shall rise, and every man shall return to his possession. That is the final Jubilee. But Bride, you do not have to wait for that day to walk in freedom. The spiritual Jubilee is now. The trumpet has sounded. The Message has gone forth. Come out of Babylon. Come out of the systems. Come back to the Word. Come back to your possession. This is Jubilee.

## POINT 4 -- THE TRUMPET THAT DEMANDS A DECISION

**Key Scripture:** "But if the servant shall plainly say, I love my master, my wife, and my children; I will not go out free: Then his master shall bring him unto the judges; he shall also bring him to the door, or unto the door post; and his master shall bore his ear through with an aul; and he shall serve him for ever." -- Exodus 21:5-6

### The Human Need (Humanistic)
- Freedom is a gift, but it must be received. The most terrifying truth in all of scripture is this: you can hear the trumpet and refuse to go free.
- Some slaves loved their bondage. They had grown accustomed to the master's house. They preferred the security of slavery to the risk of freedom. And so they chose to stay.
- There are people tonight who have heard the Message, who have seen the vindication, who know in their hearts this is the truth -- and yet they will not leave. They will not leave their denomination. They will not leave their tradition. They will not leave their comfortable bondage. And the consequence is devastating.

### Divine Revelation
**The Word says:**
> "For it is impossible for those who were once enlightened, and have tasted of the heavenly gift, and were made partakers of the Holy Ghost, And have tasted the good word of God, and the powers of the world to come, If they shall fall away, to renew them again unto repentance; seeing they crucify to themselves the Son of God afresh, and put him to an open shame." -- Hebrews 6:4-6

**The type unfolds:**

When the trumpet sounded and a slave refused to go free, he was taken to the doorpost of the temple, and his ear was bored through with an awl. This was a mark -- a permanent mark -- that said, "This man heard the trumpet and refused." He could never hear the Jubilee again. No matter how many times the trumpet sounded in the future, he was deaf to it. His ear had been marked. That awl through the ear is a type of the mark of the beast -- the rejection of the Holy Ghost. When a man hears the truth, sees it vindicated, and turns away, God marks that ear. He will never hear the Gospel trumpet again. He will be religious, he will go to church, but he will never be free.

**From the Message:**
> "When God calls you to receive the Holy Ghost, and you refuse it, then God marks your ear, and you'll never hear it again. You're a slave to the world the rest of your days."
> *-- 1960-08-02 -- El Shaddai -- p86*

> "If you refuse to hear the acceptable year... you were bored in the ear with an awl. That means to say, that you have crossed the line between grace and judgment and you will never hear the gospel again!"
> *-- 1965-02-19 -- This day this Scripture is fulfilled -- p15*

**Sub-points:**
- The awl through the ear = the mark of the one who heard and refused
- The ear is the organ of faith ("faith cometh by hearing") -- when the ear is marked, faith can no longer enter
- This is not about sinning in weakness; this is about deliberately choosing bondage over freedom when the trumpet is clearly sounding
- The Jubilee trumpet does not sound forever -- there is a window of grace

### THUS SAITH THE LORD -- The Vision

Hear me, friends. The trumpet is not going to sound forever. There is an acceptable year -- and that year has a beginning and an end. We are living in the sounding of the last trumpet to the Gentile Bride. The Message has gone forth. The mysteries have been revealed. The Pillar of Fire is here vindicating the Word. If you can hear that trumpet tonight, then act on it. Drop the hoe. Leave the field. Go home to your Father's house. Do not let another day pass where you live as a slave when the trumpet has already declared you free. For if you hear and refuse, there comes a marking -- a deafness that is not physical but spiritual -- and you will serve that old master for the rest of your days. This is not a threat. It is a warning from a loving God who is saying, "Come now. This is Jubilee. Do not let it pass you by."

## POINT 5 -- BEAUTY FOR ASHES: THE RESTORATION OF ALL THINGS

**Key Scripture:** "To appoint unto them that mourn in Zion, to give unto them beauty for ashes, the oil of joy for mourning, the garment of praise for the spirit of heaviness; that they might be called trees of righteousness, the planting of the LORD, that he might be glorified." -- Isaiah 61:3

### The Human Need (Humanistic)
- Ashes are what remain after the fire. They speak of destruction, loss, waste -- things that were once beautiful, now reduced to dust.
- Every person in this room tonight has ashes of some kind. Broken relationships. Wasted years. Failures that seem irredeemable. Opportunities that burned up.
- The human heart asks: "Can anything be done with what is left of my life? Can God make something out of these ashes?"

### Divine Revelation
**The Word says:**
> "And I will restore to you the years that the locust hath eaten, the cankerworm, and the caterpiller, and the palmerworm, my great army which I sent among you." -- Joel 2:25

**The type unfolds:**

The Jubilee was not merely about freeing slaves. It was about restoration to original possession. "Ye shall return every man unto his possession" (Leviticus 25:13). Whatever had been lost, sold, or taken was returned. The land went back to its original owner. The family was reunited. The debts were wiped clean. Isaiah 61:3 takes this further: God does not merely restore -- He exchanges. Ashes become beauty. Mourning becomes joy. Heaviness becomes praise. The Jubilee is not a repair; it is a transformation. And the end result? "That they might be called trees of righteousness, the planting of the LORD." You are not a patched-up slave. You are a planting of the Lord -- rooted, established, fruitful, glorifying God.

**From the Message:**
> "Everything went back to its original beginning again. All slaves, they went back to the original beginning. If a man was hoeing in the field and the trumpet sounded, and he heard it, he'd throw down the hoe and say, 'I'm no more a slave, I go back home to my family.' Everything was given back."
> *-- 1961-06-18 -- Revelation, Chapter 5, part 2 -- p84*

**Sub-points:**
- Ashes = what sin, failure, and the world have done to us
- Beauty = what Christ gives in exchange -- character, holiness, the nature of Christ formed in the Bride
- "The planting of the LORD" = not something man produced, but something God planted -- predestination, election, the seed of God
- The Jubilee restores everything to its original state -- and the original state of the Bride is fellowship with God, dominion, and the fullness of the Spirit

### THUS SAITH THE LORD -- The Vision

Bride of Christ, listen to me. You are not defined by your ashes. You are defined by what God has planted in you before the foundation of the world. The Jubilee says: "Return to your possession." And your possession is not a piece of land in Palestine. Your possession is the Word of God, the power of the Holy Ghost, the fellowship with the Father, and the image of Jesus Christ. Everything that Adam lost -- the dominion, the glory, the communion -- is being restored in this hour. Not tomorrow. Now. The garment of praise is yours. The oil of joy is yours. The beauty that replaces the ashes is nothing less than the character of Jesus Christ being formed in the Bride. You are trees of righteousness. You are the planting of the LORD. Stand tall. This is your Jubilee.

## CONCLUSION

### The Epiphany -- The Question Answered

> If the trumpet of Jubilee is sounding right now in this hour, why are so many of God's people still living as slaves?
>
> Because they have not yet heard with the ear of faith what the trumpet is saying: You are free. Return to your possession. Everything that was lost has been restored through the atonement of Jesus Christ, and the Message of this hour is the final blast of the Jubilee trumpet -- calling the Bride out of every system of bondage and back to the original Word of God. The only thing keeping you a slave is your refusal to drop the hoe and go home.

### Summary

- **Point 1 (Moriah):** The Jubilee is rooted in the atonement -- without the sacrifice on the mountain, there is no trumpet to sound.
- **Point 2 (Bethesda):** The Jubilee is personal -- Jesus does not address the multitudes; He walks to the one the Father has chosen and asks, "Wilt thou be made whole?"
- **Point 3 (Mount of Olives):** The Jubilee spans from rejection to restoration -- the King who was rejected on Olivet is returning to Olivet, and every man shall return to his possession.
- **Point 4 (The Awl):** The Jubilee demands a decision -- hear and go free, or refuse and be marked as a slave forever.
- **Point 5 (Beauty for Ashes):** The Jubilee does not merely free; it transforms -- ashes become beauty, mourning becomes joy, and the Bride is revealed as the planting of the Lord.

> **The heart of this message:** This is Jubilee -- the trumpet is sounding, the atonement has been made, and every predestinated seed of God has the right to drop the hoe of bondage and return to their original possession in Christ.

### A Closing Hymn

**Suggested hymn:** "The Year of Jubilee Has Come"
**Why:** This hymn carries the exact theme of the sermon -- the proclamation of freedom, the sounding of the trumpet, and the invitation to return. It allows the congregation to respond in worship to the very truth that has been preached, declaring with their own lips that Jubilee has come.

*Additional quotes, scriptures, illustrations, or typological threads found during research that did not fit the main outline but may be drawn upon by the Spirit during the actual preaching:*

- **The moon as a type of the church (Isaiah 24:23):** "The moon represents the church. Could develop this as a sub-point under the Mount of Olives section if the Spirit leads.

It was also the end of the fiftieth year." -- *1956-07-15 -- The mark of the beast -- p21.* Ties Pentecost and Jubilee together -- the same number, the same Spirit, the same liberty.

- **The seven Sabbaths and the church ages:** "Seven church ages, being seven Sabbaths, exactly seven Sabbaths from the Pentecostal jubilee trumpet -- a Pentecostal jubilee feast -- until the waving of the sheaf, and then the Pentecostal jubilee." -- *1964-07-19 -- The Feast of the Trumpets -- p92.* Powerful connection between the seven church ages and the seven Sabbaths of years that precede the Jubilee.

- **The story of old Mose the slave set free:** "He said, 'Boss, I'm free this morning.' ... 'I'm free from the law of sin and death. Christ set me free last night at a little old meeting.' ... - **Jesus stopped in the middle of the Scripture (Isaiah 61:2):** "Remember Jesus stopped in the middle of a Scripture, because part of it was fulfilled then, the rest for His second coming? 'To preach the acceptable year of the Lord, bind up the brokenhearted,' and stopped; not 'bring judgment to the Gentile' until His second coming." -- *1965-07-25 -- What is the attraction on the mountain? -- p50.* Jesus read only the Jubilee portion and closed the book. The judgment portion belongs to His second coming. We are still in the Jubilee portion -- but not for long.

- **Walled cities vs. unwalled cities (Leviticus 25:29-31):** "The ones that was walled in never got to go free. They were bond slaves the rest of their time." -- *1962-11-22 -- Return and Jubilee -- p28.* Those in denominations (walled cities) cannot hear the Jubilee. Only those in the open field (outside organizational walls) can go free.

- **Exodus 21:5-6 (the willing slave):** Full text for reference -- "And if the servant shall plainly say, I love my master, my wife, and my children; I will not go out free: Then his master shall bring him unto the judges; he shall also bring him to the door, or unto the door post; and his master shall bore his ear through with an aul; and he shall serve him for ever."

- **Romans 10:17 connection:** "Faith cometh by hearing, hearing by the Word of God. He knowed as certain as that sound of the jubilee came, that he was a free man." -- *1955-03-11 -- The Seal of Antichrist -- p37.* The link between hearing and faith is central to the Jubilee -- the slave was freed not by his own effort but by hearing the sound.
