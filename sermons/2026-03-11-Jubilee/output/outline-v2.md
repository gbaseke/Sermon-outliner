## THIS HOUR -- PROPHETIC ANCHOR

The Jubilee trumpet is not a future event for the Bride of Christ -- it is sounding right now. Seven church ages have run their course, like seven Sabbaths of years, and we stand at the threshold of the fiftieth -- the Jubilee. The Seventh Seal has been opened, the mysteries have been revealed, and the Message of the hour is the Gospel trumpet calling every predestinated seed back to their original possession. But this trumpet does two things: it liberates and it indicts. The same sound that sets the captive free becomes evidence against those who refuse to let the captives go. The Sanhedrin of Jesus' day crucified the Word made flesh. The organized religious world of this day is crucifying the Word made manifest. Brother Branham stood as an attorney for God and indicted this generation for that crime. The Jubilee is both a courtroom and a homecoming -- and which one it is for you depends on what you do when you hear the trumpet.

## INTRODUCTION -- SETTING THE TYPE

**Tone:** Warm and pastoral, but with an undertone of urgency -- a courtroom is in session.

### The Human Scene (Humanistic)

Imagine two scenes. The first: an Israelite slave, toiling in a field. He has been sold into bondage -- perhaps by his own poor choices, perhaps by circumstances beyond his control. He has forgotten the taste of freedom. His children were born in the master's house. Every morning he picks up the hoe, and every evening he sets it down. Then one day a sound breaks through the hot air of the field -- the clear, unmistakable blast of a silver trumpet. The Jubilee priest is riding through the land. That slave has a decision to make.

Now the second scene: a courtroom. A prosecutor stands before the bench with evidence in his hand. He is not indicting a criminal from the streets. He is indicting the religious establishment -- the very ones who were supposed to be shepherding God's people. The charge? They heard the trumpet and refused to release the captives. They kept God's people in bondage to creeds, traditions, and man-made systems while the trumpet of the living Word was sounding in the land.

Those two scenes are not separate. They are the same hour. The Jubilee is both a liberation and a trial. Tonight we will see both.

### The Type Established
- **Shadow (OT):** The Year of Jubilee (Leviticus 25) -- every fiftieth year, all slaves went free, all debts were cancelled, every man returned to his original possession. But it was proclaimed on the Day of Atonement -- the blood came first, then the trumpet.
- **Substance (NT/End-time):** Jesus Christ IS the Jubilee. He announced it in the synagogue at Nazareth (Luke 4:18-19). But the same generation that heard the trumpet crucified the One who blew it. And this generation has done the same thing -- rejecting the vindicated Word, calling it fanaticism, banding it out of their churches. The Jubilee is sounding, but the Sanhedrin is in session again.

## POINT 1 -- THE MOUNTAIN WHERE GOD PROVIDES (MORIAH): THE ATONEMENT THAT AUTHORIZES THE TRUMPET

**Key Scripture:** "And Abraham called the name of that place Jehovahjireh: as it is said to this day, In the mount of the LORD it shall be seen." -- Genesis 22:14

### The Human Need (Humanistic)
- Every person carries something they cannot pay for -- a debt too large, a bondage too deep, a need too great for human resources.
- Abraham loved his son. That was not the problem. God asked him to place the dearest thing in his life on the altar, and Abraham did not know how God would work it out.
- But here is the thing that must grip us tonight: the Jubilee trumpet could not sound until the Day of Atonement. No blood, no trumpet. No sacrifice, no freedom. The entire system of liberation hangs upon what happened on a mountain.

### Divine Revelation
**The Word says:**
> "And he said, Take now thy son, thine only son Isaac, whom thou lovest, and get thee into the land of Moriah; and offer him there for a burnt offering upon one of the mountains which I will tell thee of." -- Genesis 22:2

**The type unfolds:**

Mount Moriah is one of the most sacred pieces of ground on the earth, because it is where God's provision has been displayed across the ages. On Moriah, Abraham offered Isaac, and God provided the ram -- a type of Christ, the Lamb of God. On that same ridge, Solomon built the Temple -- the dwelling place of God among men. And on Moriah's extension -- Calvary -- Jesus Christ, God's only Son, was offered as the final atonement. Without the atonement there is no Jubilee. The trumpet has authority to sound only because the blood has been shed. And it was shed right there -- on that mountain.

But notice something else about Calvary. The same mountain that authorized the trumpet of liberation also became the scene of the greatest crime in human history. "There they crucified him." The very act that purchased your freedom was carried out by wicked hands. The Sanhedrin rejected the Lamb while standing on the mountain of the Lamb.

**From the Message:**
> "Behind it now, we see the picture He was painting: God giving His only Son."
> *-- 1954-04-04 -- God has a Provided Way -- p35*

**Sub-points:**
- Abraham on Moriah: God provides the sacrifice (the ram -- a type of Christ)
- Solomon on Moriah: God provides the dwelling place (the Temple -- a type of the Body of Christ)
- Christ on Moriah (Calvary): God provides the atonement -- and wicked hands crucify the provision
- The Jubilee trumpet sounded on the Day of Atonement -- it is rooted in what God did on this mountain
- The mountain of provision is also the mountain of indictment: those who reject the sacrifice are guilty of the blood

### THUS SAITH THE LORD -- The Vision

Beloved, the God who provided on Moriah is the same God who has provided for you tonight. Every shadow that was cast on that mountain pointed to one thing: the Lamb of God that taketh away the sin of the world. And if the atonement has been made -- and it has -- then the trumpet has a right to sound, and you have a right to go free. But hear this also: those who stand on the mountain of Calvary and reject the Lamb are not innocent bystanders. They are guilty of the blood. The religious world of this hour has the vindicated Word before it, the same way the Sanhedrin had the vindicated Messiah before it. And what have they done? The same thing. The mountain of provision has become the mountain of indictment.

## POINT 2 -- THE HOUSE OF MERCY BECOMES A COURTROOM (BETHESDA)

**Key Scripture:** "When Jesus saw him lie, and knew that he had been now a long time in that case, he saith unto him, Wilt thou be made whole?" -- John 5:6

### The Human Need (Humanistic)
- The name "Bethesda" means "House of Mercy" or "House of Grace." Think of it -- a building dedicated to mercy, and yet it was full of suffering. Five porches, representing the five books of Moses (the Law), and under those porches lay multitudes who could not be healed by the Law.
- These were not casual visitors. They were desperate. Blind, halt, withered -- waiting. Some had been there for years. They were close to the water but could not get in.
- There are people sitting in churches tonight in the same condition: close to something, waiting for something, but unable to lay hold of it. And who is responsible? The system that keeps them there -- religious enough to have a form of godliness, but denying the power that could set them free.

### Divine Revelation
**The Word says:**
> "Having a form of godliness, but denying the power thereof: from such turn away." -- 2 Timothy 3:5

**The type unfolds:**

Five porches -- the five books of the Law. The Law could show you your condition but could not heal you. You could lie under its shadow all your life and never be made whole. The denominations are modern-day Bethesdas -- houses that are named after mercy but have no power to deliver. The people lie there, waiting for the moving of the water, waiting for some system to save them, and the system cannot do it. It has a form of godliness but denies the power thereof.

Then Jesus walked in. He did not heal the multitudes. He bypassed them all and went straight to one man -- the one the Father showed Him. The Jubilee trumpet does not save the world; it calls out those who will hear. And notice: by healing that one man, Jesus indicted the entire system. He proved that what the pool could not do in thirty-eight years, the Word could do in a moment. The system stands condemned -- not by argument, but by demonstration.

**Sub-points:**
- Bethesda (House of Mercy) with five porches = the Law, which diagnosed but could not heal
- The multitudes waited for the moving of the water = religious form without power -- a form of godliness
- Jesus walked past the multitudes to one man = sovereignty, election, and the indictment of the system by demonstration
- The denominations are modern Bethesdas: named after mercy, full of suffering, denying the power that could set people free
- "Wilt thou be made whole?" = the personal call of Jubilee -- but also the question that exposes every system that has kept you bound

### THUS SAITH THE LORD -- The Vision

Church, we are not waiting for the moving of the water. The Word Himself has walked into the house. The pool of religion has no power. Thirty-eight years that man lay there, and no system could touch him. But one Word from the living God and he rose up and walked. That is the indictment against organized religion in this hour: God has proven by His vindicated Word that what the denominations cannot do, the Message of the hour can do. ## POINT 3 -- THE MOUNT OF OLIVES: THE MOUNTAIN OF REJECTION AND THE COURTROOM OF HISTORY

**Key Scripture:** "O Jerusalem, Jerusalem, thou that killest the prophets, and stonest them which are sent unto thee, how often would I have gathered thy children together, even as a hen gathereth her chickens under her wings, and ye would not!" -- Matthew 23:37

### The Human Need (Humanistic)
- David, rejected by his own people, his own son Absalom rising against him, went up over the Mount of Olives weeping. A king without a throne. A father betrayed by his own household.
- Eight hundred years later, Jesus sat on that same mountain and wept over Jerusalem. He did not weep in anger. He wept because He had offered them everything -- the Jubilee, the liberty, the restoration -- and they would not have it. "How often would I have gathered you ... and ye would not."
- There is something in those words that should shake every religious leader to his foundation. The indictment is not that they could not. It is that they would not.

### Divine Revelation
**The Word says:**
> "And his feet shall stand in that day upon the mount of Olives, which is before Jerusalem on the east, and the mount of Olives shall cleave in the midst thereof toward the east and toward the west, and there shall be a very great valley; and half of the mountain shall remove toward the north, and half of it toward the south." -- Zechariah 14:4

**The type unfolds:**

David on the Mount of Olives is one of the most striking types of Christ in all the Old Testament. A rejected king, despised by his own household, weeping over the city he loved. And yet David returned to his throne. Jesus, the Son of David, was rejected on the same mountain. But here is where the courtroom opens: Jesus did not merely weep over Jerusalem -- He pronounced a verdict. "Ye would not." That is the language of indictment. He had come with the Jubilee trumpet in His mouth (Luke 4:18), and the religious establishment refused the sound. They rejected the Prophet. They crucified the Word. And in rejecting Him, they sealed their own judgment.

The same spirit that moved the Sanhedrin to reject the Messiah on Olivet is the same spirit that has moved the denominations to reject the Message of this hour. Brother Branham drew this parallel with prophetic clarity.

The denominations have done to the vindicated Word exactly what the Sanhedrin did to the vindicated Christ. They have rejected it. They have called it fanaticism. They have banded it out of their pulpits. And they stand guilty -- indicted by the very Word they refused. But Bride, hear me: the same feet that ascended from Olivet are coming back to Olivet. The King who was rejected is returning. And every man shall return to his possession. The Jubilee is not cancelled by the rejection -- it is confirmed by it. Come out of Babylon. Come out of the systems. Come back to the Word. This is Jubilee.

- But there is something worse than being guilty, and that is being guilty and not knowing it. The Sanhedrin believed they were serving God when they crucified Christ. The denominations believe they are serving God when they reject the vindicated Word. The most dangerous criminal is the one in a clerical robe who believes his own creed is God's truth.

## POINT 5 -- THE TRUMPET THAT DEMANDS A DECISION: THE AWL OR THE OPEN ROAD

**Key Scripture:** "But if the servant shall plainly say, I love my master, my wife, and my children; I will not go out free: Then his master shall bring him unto the judges; he shall also bring him to the door, or unto the door post; and his master shall bore his ear through with an aul; and he shall serve him for ever." -- Exodus 21:5-6

### The Human Need (Humanistic)
- Freedom is a gift, but it must be received. The most terrifying truth in all of scripture is this: you can hear the trumpet and refuse to go free.
- Some slaves loved their bondage. They had grown accustomed to the master's house. They preferred the security of slavery to the risk of freedom. And so they chose to stay.
- There are people tonight who have heard the Message, who have seen the vindication, who know in their hearts this is the truth -- and yet they will not leave their denomination, their tradition, their comfortable bondage. They love the master's house more than the Father's house.

## POINT 6 -- BEAUTY FOR ASHES: THE VERDICT FOR THE BRIDE

**Key Scripture:** "To appoint unto them that mourn in Zion, to give unto them beauty for ashes, the oil of joy for mourning, the garment of praise for the spirit of heaviness; that they might be called trees of righteousness, the planting of the LORD, that he might be glorified." -- Isaiah 61:3

### The Human Need (Humanistic)
- Ashes are what remain after the fire. They speak of destruction, loss, waste -- things that were once beautiful, now reduced to dust.
- Every person in this room tonight has ashes of some kind. Broken relationships. Wasted years. Failures that seem irredeemable. Opportunities that burned up. And some of those ashes were caused by the very religious systems that were supposed to help you. Years spent in dead religion. Years under creeds that had no power. Years waiting by a pool that could never heal you.
- The human heart asks: "Can anything be done with what is left of my life after the systems have failed me?"

### Divine Revelation
**The Word says:**
> "And I will restore to you the years that the locust hath eaten, the cankerworm, and the caterpiller, and the palmerworm, my great army which I sent among you." -- Joel 2:25

**The type unfolds:**

The Jubilee was not merely about freeing slaves. It was about restoration to original possession. "Ye shall return every man unto his possession" (Leviticus 25:13). Whatever had been lost, sold, or taken was returned. Isaiah 61 takes this further: God does not merely restore -- He exchanges. Ashes become beauty. Mourning becomes joy. Heaviness becomes praise.

But notice the context of Isaiah 61. Verse 1 is the Jubilee proclamation -- liberty to the captives. Verse 2 is the day of vengeance -- the indictment. And verse 3 is what God does for those who responded to the trumpet. The beauty for ashes is not for the world. It is for "them that mourn in Zion." It is the Bride's portion. The courtroom has two verdicts: guilty for those who refused the trumpet, and restoration for those who heard it. The indictment is not the end of the sermon. The invitation is.

**From the Message:**
> "Everything went back to its original beginning again. All slaves, they went back to the original beginning. If a man was hoeing in the field and the trumpet sounded, and he heard it, he'd throw down the hoe and say, 'I'm no more a slave, I go back home to my family.' Everything was given back."
> *-- 1961-06-18 -- Revelation, Chapter 5, part 2 -- p84*

> "God pulled his elected church out of them denominations fifty years ago. God pulled that remnant out. This is jubilee. He's calling again. Return! Jubilee!"
> *-- 1962-11-22 -- Return and Jubilee -- p79*

**Sub-points:**
- Ashes = what sin, religion, and the world have done to us -- especially years wasted in dead systems
- Beauty = what Christ gives in exchange -- character, holiness, the nature of Christ formed in the Bride
- Isaiah 61:1-3 contains all three movements: liberation (v1), indictment (v2 -- day of vengeance), and restoration (v3 -- beauty for ashes)
- "The planting of the LORD" = not something man produced, but something God planted -- predestination, election, the seed of God
- The courtroom has two verdicts: condemnation for the refusing and beauty for the believing

### THUS SAITH THE LORD -- The Vision

Bride of Christ, listen to me. The courtroom is not for you. The indictment is not against you. You heard the trumpet and you responded. You came out. You left the walled city. You dropped the hoe. And now the God of the Jubilee speaks a different word over you -- not "Guilty," but "Beautiful." Beauty for ashes. Oil of joy for mourning. The garment of praise for the spirit of heaviness. Everything the locust ate, everything the denomination stole, everything that dead religion wasted -- God is restoring it right now. You are not defined by your ashes. You are defined by what God has planted in you before the foundation of the world. You are trees of righteousness, the planting of the LORD. The indictment falls on the system. The beauty falls on the Bride. Stand tall. This is your Jubilee.

## CONCLUSION

### The Epiphany -- The Question Answered

> If the trumpet of Jubilee is sounding right now in this hour, why are so many of God's people still living as slaves -- and who is guilty for keeping them there?
>
> Because the organized religious world -- the modern Sanhedrin, the federation of churches, the denominational system -- has crucified the Word afresh and kept God's people in bondage to creeds without power. They are guilty. God has indicted them through His vindicated Word and His prophet. But the trumpet is still sounding. The same blast that indicts the system liberates the individual. If you can hear it tonight, you are free. Drop the hoe. Leave the walled city. Return to your possession. The atonement has been made on Moriah, the Word has walked through the house of Bethesda, the King is returning to Olivet, and the Bride is receiving beauty for ashes. This is Jubilee -- and it is also a courtroom. Choose which verdict will be yours.

### Summary

- **Point 1 (Moriah):** The Jubilee is rooted in the atonement -- without the sacrifice on the mountain, there is no trumpet to sound; but the mountain of provision is also the mountain of indictment, for they crucified the Lamb on the very ground where God provided Him.
- **Point 2 (Bethesda):** The Jubilee exposes powerless religion -- Jesus bypassed the whole system and healed one man by the Word, indicting every form of godliness that keeps people bound without the power to deliver.
- **Point 3 (Mount of Olives):** The Jubilee trumpet was rejected by the religious establishment of Jesus' day, and the same spirit of rejection has indicted this generation -- "They crucified Him then, and they crucify Him now."
- **Point 4 (The Indictment):** This generation stands guilty of crucifying the Word of God afresh -- the same vindicated signs, the same wicked refusal, the same verdict.
- **Point 5 (The Awl):** The Jubilee demands a decision -- hear and go free, or refuse and be marked; the denominations have submitted to the awl, and those in walled cities will never go free.
- **Point 6 (Beauty for Ashes):** The courtroom has two verdicts -- condemnation for the system that refused, and beauty for the Bride who responded; the Jubilee ends not with indictment but with restoration.

> **The heart of this message:** The Jubilee is both a courtroom and a homecoming. The trumpet that indicts the religious world for crucifying the Word is the same trumpet that sets the Bride free and restores her to everything Adam lost. Hear it, and go home.

### A Closing Hymn

**Suggested hymn:** "The Year of Jubilee Has Come"
**Why:** After a message that moves from liberation through indictment, the congregation needs to land on the invitation -- not the verdict. This hymn allows every heart to respond to the trumpet that has been sounding all evening, declaring with their own lips that the Jubilee has come and they are going free. The courtroom is closed. The homecoming has begun.

## PREACHER'S RESERVE

*Additional quotes, scriptures, illustrations, or typological threads found during research that did not fit the main outline but may be drawn upon by the Spirit during the actual preaching:*

- **Branham as attorney for God:** "I take myself as an attorney to---on this indictment. That the Word of God being my witness, I indict this generation for the crucifixion." -- *1963-07-07 -- The Indictment -- p28.* Could be used to frame the entire message as a courtroom scene if the Spirit leads strongly in that direction.

- **The moon as a type of the church (Isaiah 24:23):** "The moon represents the church. It shows light on the earth in the absence of the sun." -- *1964-01-19 -- Shalom -- p17.* The moon (church) reflects the Son. When the Son returns, the moon is no longer needed. Could develop under Olives if needed.

- **Pentecost means fifty -- the Jubilee number:** "The word Pentecost means 'fifty.' It was the outgoing of the Holy Spirit at the end of the fifty days." -- *1956-07-15 -- The mark of the beast -- p21.* Ties Pentecost and Jubilee together -- the same number, the same Spirit, the same liberty.

- **The seven Sabbaths and the church ages:** "Seven church ages, being seven Sabbaths, exactly seven Sabbaths from the Pentecostal jubilee trumpet." -- *1964-07-19 -- The Feast of the Trumpets -- p92.* Powerful connection between the seven church ages and the seven Sabbaths of years that precede the Jubilee.

- **The story of old Mose the slave set free:** "He said, 'Boss, I'm free this morning.' ... 'I'm free from the law of sin and death. Christ set me free last night at a little old meeting.'" -- *1958-05-17 -- Zacchaeus -- p9.* A beautiful illustration if a personal story is needed to bring the invitation home.

- **Jesus stopped in the middle of Isaiah 61:2:** "Remember Jesus stopped in the middle of a Scripture, because part of it was fulfilled then, the rest for His second coming? 'To preach the acceptable year of the Lord, bind up the brokenhearted,' and stopped; not 'bring judgment to the Gentile' until His second coming." -- *1965-07-25 -- What is the attraction on the mountain? -- p50.* Jesus read only the Jubilee portion and closed the book. The judgment portion (the indictment) belongs to this second-coming hour. We are living in both -- the last of the Jubilee and the beginning of the indictment.

- **Walled cities vs. unwalled cities (Leviticus 25:29-31):** "The ones that was walled in never got to go free. They were bond slaves the rest of their time." -- *1962-11-22 -- Return and Jubilee -- p28.* / "Remember those in the walled cities could not come out in the jubilee. No, sir. They were in bondage." -- *1964-07-19 -- The Feast of the Trumpets -- p44.* This is a devastating type if fully developed: the denomination is the walled city, and those inside cannot hear the trumpet.

- **Romans 10:17 connection:** "Faith cometh by hearing, hearing by the Word of God. He knowed as certain as that sound of the jubilee came, that he was a free man." -- *1955-03-11 -- The Seal of Antichrist -- p37.* The link between hearing and faith is central -- the awl destroys the very organ through which faith enters.

- **Nicodemus -- "We know":** "Rabbi, we, the Pharisees, the preachers, the teachers, we know You're a teacher sent from God, for no man could do the things that You do unless God was with him." -- *1963-07-07 -- The Indictment -- p52.* They knew. They admitted it privately. But publicly they crucified Him. The denominations today know the Message is true -- and refuse it anyway. This deepens the indictment.

- **The tares bound in bundles:** "The apostate organization churches is bound tighter than they ever was bound. They are bundled together in a federation of churches, ready for the fires of God's judgment." -- *1962-03-18 -- The Spoken Word is the original seed -- p363.* A strong quote if the prophetic urgency needs to be pressed further.

- **Federation of churches and communism:** "Because they have rejected the gospel that separates them and makes them a different people." -- *1963-07-07 -- The Indictment -- p132.* The reason for the federation is the rejection of the separating Gospel -- the Jubilee trumpet that calls out.

- **"Come out of her, my people":** "Come out of Babylon, confusion! Come out of your creeds and your self-styled things. I will receive you," saith the Lord." -- *1960-12-25 -- God's Wrapped Gift -- p87.* The invitation after the indictment -- the final altar call of the Jubilee.
