# When the Fire Dies Down
## Preacher's Notes — Full Pastoral Manuscript

*Date: 2026-04-22*
*Core Texts: 1 Kings 19; Matthew 11:28-30; Isaiah 40:28-31*
*Tone: Pastoral, tender, encouraging — this sermon is medicine, not rebuke*

---

## Opening Prayer

*Heavenly Father, we come before Thee tonight just as we are — some strong, some stumbling, some quietly at the end of ourselves. Thou knowest every juniper tree in this room, every heart that has run farther than it can stand. Lord, we do not come for rebuke; we come for bread. We come for shade. We come for the still small voice. Speak tenderly tonight to Thy weary ones, and let every burdened soul lay something down at the foot of the cross before we leave. In Jesus' name, Amen.*

---

## Slide 1 — When the Fire Dies Down

Beloved, tonight I want to speak gently to the hearts that are tired. Not every sermon is meant to shake you; some are meant to shelter you. The Word of God says in 1 Kings 19:4, *"It is enough."* Elijah said it, and God did not strike him down for saying it — God fed him. If that is where you are tonight, you are exactly where this message is aimed. You are seen. You are loved. And God is near.

## Slide 2 — Something the Bible Understands

The word "burnout" is a modern word, but it is not a modern experience. It is as old as Eden. Every great servant of God in Scripture walked through seasons where the light inside them flickered low. And not once — not one single time — did God shame them for it. He met them. He fed them. He whispered to them. If you are burned out tonight, you are not outside of Scripture. You are inside it.

## Slide 3 — You Are Not the First

Moses said, *"It is too heavy for me"* (Num 11:14). Elijah asked God to take his life. David wept until his couch swam with tears (Ps 6:6). Jeremiah tried to quit preaching but the Word burned like fire in his bones. Paul said he was *"pressed out of measure, above strength"* (2 Cor 1:8). Even our Lord Jesus, being wearied with His journey, sat by a well (John 4:6). If these men bent beneath the weight, it is no shame for you to feel it too.

## Slide 4 — Elijah After the Victory

Notice the timing: Elijah had just called down fire from heaven. The prophets of Baal were defeated. Heaven answered. And then — a day's journey into the wilderness, and a juniper tree, and a prayer to die. Great victory often ushers in great weariness. Satan cannot stop the fire, so he comes for the man afterward. If you are low after a high, you are not falling from grace — you are walking a well-worn path.

## Slide 5 — God's First Response: Food and Sleep

Read 1 Kings 19:5-7 slowly. There is no sermon. No correction. No demand for Elijah to get back up and be strong. Just an angel, and a cake baked on coals, and a cruse of water, and the words, *"Arise and eat."* God let him sleep. Then fed him. Then let him sleep again. Sometimes the holiest thing you can do tonight is simply rest. God is not in a hurry with you.

## Slide 6 — The Juniper Tree Is a Gift

Brother Branham said, *"I'm glad that there is a juniper tree. All God's people needs to go to that juniper tree."* (*What hearest thou, Elijah?* 1959-06-09). That tree did not grow by accident. God planted it there for His servant. If you find yourself under one tonight, do not despise it. It may not be a detour — it may be an appointment. Some of the deepest work God ever does in a man, He does in the shade of a juniper tree.

## Slide 7 — The Prophet Was There Too

In *What doest thou here?* (1959-03-01), Brother Branham confessed, *"Frankly, I'm right under it now."* The same prophet who called down healing power had days when he put a pistol against his head and laid his hand toward a high-voltage wire. And God held him back. If that man wept in the shadow, beloved, you are allowed to weep too. Ministry does not exempt you from the valley — it often leads you through it.

## Slide 8 — The Root: Carrying What God Never Gave

Jethro looked at Moses and said, *"Thou wilt surely wear away... this thing is too heavy for thee; thou art not able to perform it thyself alone"* (Ex 18:18). That was not rebuke — that was love. Burnout is often the fruit of carrying what God never asked you to carry. The seventy elders were not a demotion for Moses; they were a rescue. If help is being offered to you tonight, it is not weakness to receive it. It is wisdom.

## Slide 9 — The Root: Strength That Is Not His

Isaiah 40:30 says, *"Even the youths shall faint and be weary, and the young men shall utterly fall."* Self-strength always runs dry. But the next verse is the promise: *"They that wait upon the LORD shall renew their strength."* The eagle does not flap harder — he mounts up on a current that is not his own. Rest is not the opposite of strength; rest is the soil where strength grows back.

## Slide 10 — A Quiet Day with Jesus

Mark 6:31 — our Lord turned to His disciples, who had no time so much as to eat, and said, *"Come ye yourselves apart into a desert place, and rest awhile."* This was prescribed by the Great Physician Himself. It was not laziness. It was not a lack of zeal. It was holy recovery. If Jesus pulled His own disciples away from the crowds, He is not ashamed to pull you away tonight.

## Slide 11 — The Still Small Voice

When God finally came to Elijah at the cave, He did not come in the wind, or the earthquake, or the fire. He came in a still small voice. And His first word was not, *"How dare you run?"* It was, *"What doest thou here?"* — a gentle question from a loving Father. God is not shouting at you tonight, beloved. He is whispering. Lean in and listen.

## Slide 12 — A Rock in a Weary Land

Isaiah 32:2 — *"A man shall be... as the shadow of a great rock in a weary land."* Brother Branham preached it this way in *Without money or without price* (1959-08-02): *"If you are weary, speak to the rock! Don't have to pay Him; speak to Him!"* You do not need eloquence. You do not need strength. You need only to lift your voice, even a whisper, toward the Rock that is higher than you.

## Slide 13 — Come Unto Me

*"Come unto me, all ye that labour and are heavy laden, and I will give you rest"* (Matt 11:28). Brother Branham said in *Hebrews, Chapter 4* (1957-09-01): *"That is the rest: the Holy Ghost."* The rest Jesus offers is not a vacation — it is a Person. And then He adds, *"Take my yoke upon you... my burden is light."* A yoke is built for two. He is not handing you a load; He is stepping in beside you to carry it.

## Slide 14 — Cast Your Care

*"Casting all your care upon him; for he careth for you"* (1 Peter 5:7). Not some of your care. All of it. The big ones, the small ones, the ones you are ashamed to name. Psalm 55:22 says, *"Cast thy burden upon the LORD, and he shall sustain thee."* You do not hold Him up, friend. He holds you. Roll it off tonight and leave it at the altar.

## Slide 15 — Weary in Well Doing

Galatians 6:9 — *"And let us not be weary in well doing: for in due season we shall reap, if we faint not."* Hebrews 12:3 adds, *"Consider him... lest ye be wearied and faint in your minds."* Whatever you have poured out in His name has not been poured into the sand. The harvest is coming. The reaping is real. Hold the plow just a little longer — the field is closer to gold than you know.

## Slide 16 — Arise and Eat

The angel said to Elijah, *"Arise and eat; because the journey is too great for thee"* (1 Kings 19:7). God did not deny the difficulty — He acknowledged it, and then He provided for it. He has bread for your table tonight. He has a juniper for your shade. He has a whisper for your heart. The fire is not out, beloved — it is only banked. *"They that wait upon the LORD shall renew their strength; they shall mount up with wings as eagles."* Come and eat.

---

## Closing Prayer

*Lord Jesus, to every weary saint in this room tonight, we ask for the angel's touch and the angel's bread. We ask for the shade of the juniper tree. We ask for the still small voice that says, "I am here, and I am not angry with thee." Lift the loads that never belonged to them. Set down the yokes that were never from Thy hand. And put upon them instead Thy yoke, which is easy, and Thy burden, which is light. Send them home fed, rested, and quietly sure that Thou hast not forsaken them. In the name of our Lord Jesus Christ, Amen.*

## Benediction

*"Now the God of peace... make you perfect in every good work to do his will, working in you that which is wellpleasing in his sight, through Jesus Christ; to whom be glory for ever and ever. Amen."* — Hebrews 13:20-21
