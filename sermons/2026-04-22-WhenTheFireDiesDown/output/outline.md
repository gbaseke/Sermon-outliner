# When the Fire Dies Down
## A Pastoral Message on Burnout — 16 Slide Outline

*Date: 2026-04-22*
*Tone: Pastoral, encouraging, gentle — never rebuking the weary*
*Core Texts: 1 Kings 19; Matthew 11:28-30; Isaiah 40:28-31*

---

### Slide 1 — When the Fire Dies Down
- A word for the weary saint
- Based on 1 Kings 19 and Matthew 11
- Scripture: *"It is enough"* — 1 Kings 19:4
- You are seen. You are loved.

### Slide 2 — Something the Bible Understands
- The word "burnout" is modern
- The experience is as old as Eden
- God's greatest servants knew this valley
- He never once rebuked them for it
- Tonight we find ourselves in good company

### Slide 3 — You Are Not the First
- Moses cried, "It is too heavy" (Num 11:14)
- Elijah asked God to take his life
- David wept until his bed swam
- Jeremiah tried to quit preaching
- Paul "despaired even of life" (2 Cor 1:8)
- Even Jesus grew weary (John 4:6)

### Slide 4 — Elijah After the Victory
- He had just called down fire
- He ran a day into the wilderness
- He sat beneath a juniper tree
- "Take away my life" — 1 Kings 19:4
- Great victory often precedes great weariness

### Slide 5 — God's First Response: Food and Sleep
- No sermon. No correction. No demand.
- "Arise and eat" — 1 Kings 19:5
- An angel baked him bread
- He slept. He ate. He slept again.
- Sometimes the holiest act is rest

### Slide 6 — The Juniper Tree Is a Gift
- *"I'm glad that there is a juniper tree"*
- — Bro. Branham, *What hearest thou, Elijah?*
- God planted it there on purpose
- A place to get quiet before Him
- Your breakdown may be an appointment

### Slide 7 — The Prophet Was There Too
- *"Frankly, I'm right under it now"*
- — Bro. Branham, *What doest thou here?*
- He testified of his darkest hour
- God held back his hand from harm
- If the prophet wept, you may too

### Slide 8 — The Root: Carrying What God Never Gave
- Jethro to Moses: *"Thou wilt surely wear away"*
- Exodus 18:18 — "too heavy for thee"
- You were never meant to carry alone
- God sent seventy elders to help
- Help is not weakness; it is wisdom

### Slide 9 — The Root: Strength That Is Not His
- *"Even the youths shall faint"* — Isaiah 40:30
- Self-strength always runs dry
- Waiting on the Lord renews us
- Eagles mount up; they do not strain
- Rest is the soil of new strength

### Slide 10 — A Quiet Day with Jesus
- *"Come ye apart... and rest awhile"* — Mark 6:31
- The disciples had no time to eat
- Jesus prescribed a desert place
- Not laziness — holy recovery
- Even the Master needed the mountain

### Slide 11 — The Still Small Voice
- Not in the wind, earthquake, or fire
- A quiet whisper after the storm
- God meets us in gentleness
- He asks, *"What doest thou here?"*
- He is not shouting at you tonight

### Slide 12 — A Rock in a Weary Land
- Isaiah 32:2 — shelter in the storm
- *"If you are weary, speak to the rock"*
- — Bro. Branham, *Without money or without price*
- You don't have to pay Him
- Just speak. He hears the weakest voice.

### Slide 13 — Come Unto Me
- *"All ye that labour and are heavy laden"*
- Matthew 11:28 — His open invitation
- The rest Jesus gives is the Holy Ghost
- *"Take my yoke... my burden is light"*
- He carries the heavy end of every yoke

### Slide 14 — Cast Your Care
- 1 Peter 5:7 — cast it on Him
- *"For he careth for you"*
- Not some of it — all of it
- Psalm 55:22 — He will sustain thee
- You do not carry Him; He carries you

### Slide 15 — Weary in Well Doing
- Galatians 6:9 — "be not weary"
- *"In due season we shall reap"*
- Hebrews 12:3 — consider Him who endured
- Your labor is not in vain
- The harvest is nearer than you think

### Slide 16 — Arise and Eat
- The journey is too great for thee (1 Kings 19:7)
- But He has bread for your table
- He has a juniper for your shade
- He has a whisper for your heart
- Beloved, the fire is not out — rest first
- *"They that wait upon the LORD shall renew their strength"*
