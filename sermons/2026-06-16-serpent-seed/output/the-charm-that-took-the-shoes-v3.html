The Charm That Took the Shoes

Alternate titles: Flattery or Enchantment? · One Word Off, and the Gate Stood Open
Scriptures: Genesis 3:13–15; Daniel 11:21,32; Revelation 6:1–2; Proverbs 30:18–20 Theme: Deception is Satan’s master-weapon — he does not break the gate, he charms the heart off one Word Prepared: 2026-06-16

How did the liar get past me — was it flattery or enchantment that he used — and where do I fortify myself so that I am never beguiled again?

This Hour — Prophetic Anchor

  • Same subtle spirit whispers in this last hour.
  • Jesus warned it would deceive the very elect.
  • Not crude sin — a polished, religious lie.
  • Daniel saw it: vile person obtains by flatteries.
  • The Bride is separated by holding every Word.
  • This message hedges the flock before the deceiver.

Introduction — Setting the Type

Tone: warm and pastoral — open the heart before opening the Word.

The Human Scene

  • A man promised birds if I gave my shoes.
  • I gave my shoes; he never came back.
  • The shame hurt worse than the bare feet.
  • How did I not see him coming?
  • Every person has a story like that.
  • Smooth voice, friendly smile, too-good offer.
  • The small voice inside knew — but we reasoned it away.
  • Before my shoes, Eve walked away losing everything.
  • Solomon called it: “the way of a serpent upon a rock” — no tracks.

Shadow (OT): Eve in the garden — beguiled, not broken; charmed and talked out of the Word she already had.

Substance (NT / End-time): The church through the ages, and the elect Bride in this last hour, facing the same antichrist spirit dressed in religion and flattery.

Person Profile — Eve

Visible need
Nothing was lacking — garden, husband, God's presence, spoken Word.
Invisible need
A craving for wisdom beyond what God had said.
Motive
Wanted more light — appetite itself planted by the deceiver.
Intention
Believed she was adding to God's Word, not subtracting.
What God saw
A daughter charmed off the Word — and a Redeemer already in His heart.
Point 1

He Does Not Break In — He Beguiles

“Now the serpent was more subtil than any beast of the field which the LORD God had made.”

Genesis 3:1

The Human Need

  • We watch the front gate for monsters.
  • He slips in the back door as a friend.
  • Got caught not because we are stupid.
  • We expected an enemy but met a charmer.
  • Con man picks the lock of trust, not doors.

Divine Revelation

“And the LORD God said unto the woman, What is this that thou hast done? And the woman said, The serpent beguiled me, and I did eat.”

Genesis 3:13
  • Eve never said the serpent forced her.
  • "Beguiled" — charmed, won over, defiled through agreement.
  • Her own hand reached out; he made her want it.
  • Deceiver never makes you victim — makes you accomplice.
  • He did not put the fruit in her mouth.

“I am told that a snake can catch the eye of a bird and charm that bird with its enchantments, and the stare of its eyes, until such a way, if that bird will give the snake its attention for just a few minutes, that bird will flutter and completely become paralyzed. And the snake will take the bird. And I can believe that, because I know of another serpent, the devil, that if he could ever catch the eye with his enchantments, with your modern rock-n-roll, and all your fandangles of the world, if he can ever catch you, young lady or young man, just long enough to get his charm in you, you’ll flutter, but you can’t get loose. He’ll hold you till he swallows you up in sin.”

— 1958-10-03 · Looking at the unseen · ¶32
  • "Beguile" means "defiled" — leaves a mark inside.
  • Serpent worked through agreement, not force.
  • Most subtil — reasonable, pleasant, warm voice.
  • Charm holds only while you give it attention.

Thus Saith the Lord — The Vision

  • Enemy comes refined, religious, reasonable, and warm.
  • In this hour the lie wears a clerical collar.
  • Guard where you give attention and agreement.
  • He beguiled God's daughter — never broke God's gate.
  • Only ones safe: refuse to give him their eye.
Point 2

The Weapon Was Flattery — “He Shall Come In Peaceably”

“And in his estate shall stand up a vile person, to whom they shall not give the honour of the kingdom: but he shall come in peaceably, and obtain the kingdom by flatteries.”

Daniel 11:21

The Human Need

  • Bible names the weapon by name: flattery.
  • Flattery is a lie that feels like love.
  • "You have beautiful eyes" bypasses reason entirely.
  • The man who took my shoes befriended me first.
  • He came in peaceably — never with a sword.

Divine Revelation

“And the serpent said unto the woman, Ye shall not surely die… ye shall be as gods, knowing good and evil.”

Genesis 3:4–5
  • Serpent did not insult Eve — he elevated her.
  • "Ye shall be as gods" — pure flattery.
  • Told her she was destined for more light.
  • Daniel saw same spirit standing up centuries later.
  • Vile person obtains the kingdom — not by war, by flatteries.
  • Honeyed voice that told Eve crowned a man over the church.

“Incarnate, he become a man and then they crowned him. Read, notice... or read rather, how Daniel said ‘he will take over the church kingdoms.’ Would you like to read it? We’ve got time to do that, haven’t we? All right. Let’s go back to Daniel just a moment. Turn back to the book of Daniel and we’ll read just a moment. And we won’t be, maybe another fifteen or twenty minutes, or thirty, or something. All right. Let’s get Daniel the 11th chapter, and let’s take the 21st verse. Here’s Daniel. Daniel is speaking now how this fellow is going to take over. And in his estate shall stand up a vile person ^^ [Rome talking] , ^^ to whom they shall not give the honour of the kingdom ^^ [now, watch] ^^: but he shall come in peaceable, and obtain the kingdom by flatteries.”

— 1963-03-18 · Seals 3: The First Seal · ¶137
  • Flattery is the antichrist's diplomacy — charm over conquest.
  • "Come in peaceably" is the wolf's disguise.
  • Every offer is a promotion off your Word-place.

Thus Saith the Lord — The Vision

  • Deceiver flatters his way through, not kicks his way in.
  • Gathering the church world with compliments and unity now.
  • Bride is not flattered off her foundation.
  • When smooth voice says "you deserve more," she answers: "It is written."
Point 3

One Word Off, and the Fortress Stood Open

“Yea, hath God said, Ye shall not eat of every tree of the garden?”

Genesis 3:1

The Human Need

  • Underneath flattery was one question: "Are you sure?"
  • He loosened one Word, not all of them.
  • "Yea, hath God said?" — the whole fortress opened.
  • We do not fall to a thousand lies.
  • We fall to one word added or doubted.

Divine Revelation

“And the serpent said unto the woman, Ye shall not surely die.”

Genesis 3:4
  • God said "surely die"; serpent added one word: "not."
  • Did not throw out the Bible — just revised one syllable.
  • Dressed it as more light, deeper understanding.
  • Eve's fall started before she ate the fruit.
  • Began when she let two ideas stand together.
  • Science of deception: reasoning added alongside the Word.

“That’s the thing that broke the fellowship in Eden, is because that Eve failed to keep the focus of her looking on God’s word, and listened to the reasonings of the enemy, see. See? If she’d just stayed right with, ‘God said so, that settles it. God said so.’ But she didn’t want to do that, see. She changed the focus of her glass and began to see instead of being just one way, there become two ways, see. She saw, ‘Well now, maybe this man could be right. Maybe he is right. Now we know God is right; and maybe he’s right. This is just more light on what God said.’ You see there? There’s the same thing happens today, exactly right back to the same spot, see. You see two ideas; there’s only one. There’s not no creed, it’s the Word. It’s God’s word, God’s purpose, God’s plan. All other plans are no good. That ... and Eve did this evil thing.”

— 1963-04-21 · Victory Day · ¶33
  • Weapon was God's Word with one word moved.
  • "More light on what God said" — deceiver's favourite disguise.
  • Fall was a change of focus, not faith.
  • From "God said so, that settles it" to "maybe."

Thus Saith the Lord — The Vision

  • Deceiver does not need to overturn your whole faith.
  • He only needs you to surrender one Word.
  • It is down to a razor's edge in this hour.
  • Antichrist message ninety-nine percent right — one Word off.
  • Bride takes no "more light" that moves a single Word.
  • "God said so, that settles it" — no debate entertained.
Point 4

The Same Serpent Rode Into the Church on a White Horse

“And I saw, and behold a white horse: and he that sat on him had a bow; and a crown was given unto him: and he went forth conquering, and to conquer.”

Revelation 6:2

The Human Need

  • "Am I the only one foolish enough to be fooled?"
  • No — the whole church world was beguiled.
  • Most dangerous deceptions look white, orderly, respectable.
  • We trust a crowd, a council, a credential.
  • That is the exact door the deceiver walked through.

Divine Revelation

“So hast thou also them that hold the doctrine of the Nicolaitanes, which thing I hate.”

Revelation 2:15
  • First seal: white horse — teachers said Holy Spirit conquering.
  • Branham by revelation: that rider was the antichrist spirit.
  • Same serpent from the garden, now grown into doctrine.
  • Nicolaitanes: conquer the laity, organise what God had scattered.
  • "We just want fellowship" — and ended with a crowned man.
  • Same flattery that defiled Eve defiled the early church.

“Now, first stage---white horse. He was just a teacher, but an antichrist spirit in its doctrine and its quality. It was innocent. It couldn’t hurt nothing, seemed like. Just going on... That’s the way Satan comes in. Oh, he’s a slick bird. He told Eve, ‘Now, you know you’re looking for wisdom. You don’t know what’s right and wrong.’ Said, ‘Now, if your eyes were opened, you would know what...’ And said, ‘The fruit is very pleasant. It’s good. It is pleasant to the eye. You should take it. Now you don’t know whether it is or not, do you?’ ‘No, I don’t, but God said not to do it.’ ‘But, oh!’ ‘Well, I know, but God said He would...’ ‘Surely He won’t to do that.’ Just as sweet as it could be. Look what it did. Watch this antichrist spirit rise up amongst the early church, a doctrine of Nicolaitanes. ‘Nico,’ conquer the laity, make a holy man. ‘Why, we just want fellowship. Why, you’re scattered out here. Nobody knows where nobody’s at. I think we all ought to have an organization and just be different. We go right ... put ourselves together. We ought to make a lodge out of it.’”

— 1963-03-20 · Seals 5: The Third Seal · ¶104
  • White horse rider: antichrist spirit in a white cloak.
  • "Surely He won't do that" — same voice, organized the church.
  • Rode in as a teacher; was handed a crown.
  • Beguiled church from Nicaea to church-unions of our day.

Thus Saith the Lord — The Vision

  • Same rider still in the saddle right now.
  • Gathering the beguiled into one fold — calling it unity.
  • Spirit of Christ in the Bride calls her out.
  • Elect Bride knows the rider by his fruit — not his robe.
  • She stays with the Lamb and His Book.
Point 5

Where the Bride Fortifies: “The People That Know Their God”

“And such as do wickedly against the covenant shall he corrupt by flatteries: but the people that do know their God shall be strong, and do exploits.”

Daniel 11:32

The Human Need

  • Your deepest question is exactly the right one.
  • "Where do I fortify so I am never caught again?"
  • Cannot out-argue the serpent — Eve tried and lost.
  • Safety is not entering the debate at all.
  • Fortress is not cleverness, research, or spiritual experiences.
  • Fortress is a settled heart that knows God.

Divine Revelation

“And a stranger will they not follow, but will flee from him: for they know not the voice of strangers… My sheep hear my voice, and I know them, and they follow me.”

John 10:5, 27
  • Flatteries corrupt the wicked; cannot move those who know God.
  • Not the clever who stand — those who know Him.
  • Eve had the Word but lost her focus.
  • She quoted it: "God said not to do it."
  • Her defense failed when she entertained the debate.
  • Shield was willingness to say: "God said so, that settles it."
  • Bride measures every sign by the Word, not the crowd.
  • Anything contrary to the Word — leave it alone.

“Now, can’t you see that you can’t put your trust in healing campaigns? You can’t put your trust in any kind of a sign like that. The only thing you can put your trust in is thus saith the Lord from the Bible. Now, church, that is exactly where I have tried to keep you, my children. And if something happens to me and God takes me out of this earth, don’t you never fail. Remember this with all your heart: stay with that Word! Don’t you leave that Word! Anything contrary to it, leave it alone, no matter what it is. Then you know it’s right, see. A healing campaign now! Sinful men that even shock their own managers with their vulgar, and dirt, and filth; and people throw away their crutches and get healed by looking at these boys. It’s so filthy, and dirty, and antichrist! You see, it’s Satan on a mock campaign, see. He does anything that the Christ can do, but he can’t confirm the Word, see. He’ll take part of it here and part of it here, but he can’t take it altogether, see. He can’t get it together. So you see, no wonder the Bible said that would almost fool the very elected if it was possible---the antichrist spirit.”

— 1964-08-23 · Questions and Answers, Part 2 · ¶5
  • Refuse "Yea, hath God said?" with "Thus saith the Lord."
  • Antichrist imitates everything Christ does — except confirm every Word.
  • Bird breaks the charm by lifting its head and looking up.
  • Bride breaks every enchantment by fixing eyes on the Word.
  • Elect know His voice — not by intellect, by predestination.

Thus Saith the Lord — The Vision

  • Build your wall around the Word, not your reasoning.
  • Flatteries that corrupt the wicked cannot corrupt those who know God.
  • Antichrist cannot get the whole Word together — Bride holds it all.
  • You will not be beguiled again — not by cleverness.
  • Too settled in the Word to debate the serpent.
  • Stay with that Word. Anything contrary — leave it alone.

Conclusion

How did the liar get past me — was it flattery or enchantment that he used — and where do I fortify myself so that I am never beguiled again?

  • He got past you the same way he got past Eve.
  • Not by force — by flattery, coming in peaceably.
  • He moved your focus off "God said so" onto a sweet lie.
  • It was both: flattery that warmed your pride, enchantment that held your eye.
  • Word that names the weapon also names the fortress.
  • "The people that do know their God shall be strong."
  • Fortify behind every Word — refuse the debate entirely.
  • God put enmity between your seed and the serpent's.
  • A Redeemer already crushed the head of the one who beguiled you.

The Sermon in Miniature

  • Point 1: He beguiles the heart — defiling through your own agreement, never by force.
  • Point 2: The weapon was flattery — “obtain the kingdom by flatteries” — a lie that feels like love.
  • Point 3: One word off opened the fortress — a single reasoning entertained beside the Word.
  • Point 4: Same serpent on a white horse — antichrist spirit in righteousness, from Nicaea to today.
  • Point 5: Bride fortifies as people who know their God — refusing the debate, measuring every sign by Scripture.

The serpent beguiles by flattery and one word off, but the Bride is kept by every Word of God — and the heel he bruised is the heel that crushed his head.

A Closing Hymn

Suggested hymn: “How Firm a Foundation”

Why: The whole sermon drives to one fortress — “Thus saith the Lord from the Bible” — and this hymn declares that the believer’s whole confidence rests on the unmoved foundation of God’s Word against every flattery and reasoning of the enemy.
