## THIS HOUR — PROPHETIC ANCHOR

The same subtle spirit that whispered to Eve in the garden is riding in this last hour, and Jesus warned it would be so near the truth that "if it were possible, they shall deceive the very elect" (Matthew 24:24). This is not the hour of crude, obvious sin — it is the hour of the polished lie, the religious counterfeit that keeps just enough of the Word to look right. The Bride is being separated from the church not by works, signs, or sincerity, but by one thing: whether she will hold to *every* Word of God, or be reasoned off of it by one word. This message is a shepherd putting a hedge of the Word around his flock before the deceiver comes again.

---

## INTRODUCTION — SETTING THE TYPE

**Tone:** Warm and pastoral — open the heart before opening the Word.

### The Human Scene (Humanistic)
Every one of us has been scammed at least once — and the sting is not just the loss, it is the shame. We lie awake asking, "Am I that gullible? How did I not see him coming?" We replay it: the smooth voice, the friendly smile, the offer that sounded almost too good — and the part of us that *knew* something was off but talked itself out of it. Beloved, that is exactly the oldest wound in the human story. Before there was a thief in the night or a con on the street, there was a serpent at a tree, and a woman who walked away from that conversation having lost everything — and saying, almost in a daze, "I have done no wickedness." Solomon said it was a thing too wonderful for him: "the way of a serpent upon a rock... and the way of a man with a maid" (Proverbs 30:19). How does the deceiver leave no tracks? That is what we want God to show us tonight.

### The Type Established
- **Shadow (OT):** Eve in the garden — beguiled, not broken; talked out of the Word she already had
- **Substance (NT/End-time):** The church through the ages, and the elect Bride in this last hour, facing the same antichrist spirit dressed in religion

### Person Profile — Eve
- **Visible need:** Nothing was lacking — she had a garden, a husband, the presence of God, and the spoken Word for her protection
- **Invisible need:** A craving for wisdom and experience beyond what God had said — "ye shall be as gods, knowing good and evil" (Genesis 3:5)
- **Motive:** She wanted more light, a deeper understanding — not realizing the appetite itself had been planted in her by the deceiver
- **Intention:** She intended no rebellion; she believed she was *adding* to what God said, not subtracting from it
- **What God saw:** A daughter who had taken her eyes off His Word and put them on a reasoning — and a love already moving in His heart that would put enmity between her seed and the serpent's, and send a Redeemer

## POINT 1 — HE DOES NOT BREAK IN; HE BEGUILES

**Key Scripture:** "Now the serpent was more subtil than any beast of the field which the LORD God had made." — Genesis 3:1

### The Human Need (Humanistic)
- We picture the devil coming as a monster, so we watch the front gate for something hideous — and he comes to the back door as a friend
- The reason we got caught is not that we are stupid; it is that we expected an enemy and met a charmer
- A con man never announces himself; he agrees with you, flatters you, makes you feel understood — and the lock he picks is not your door, it is your trust

### Divine Revelation
**The Word says:**
> "And the LORD God said unto the woman, What is this that thou hast done? And the woman said, The serpent beguiled me, and I did eat." — Genesis 3:13

**The type unfolds:**
Notice Eve does not say, "The serpent forced me," or "The serpent overpowered me." She says, "The serpent *beguiled* me" — he charmed me, he won me over, he defiled me through agreement. The most subtle beast of the field did not break the commandment over her; he talked her into breaking it herself, so that her own hand reached out and took. That is the signature of the deceiver in every age: he never makes you his victim, he makes you his accomplice. He did not steal the fruit and put it in her mouth — he made her *want* it, and then she called it her own choice.

**From the Message:**
> "And there he was. And when he did, God said, begin to call for Eve and Adam. And he said, \"I was naked.\" And He said, \"Who told you you was naked?\" Then they begin to--in army fashion, passing the buck. Said, \"Well, the woman You gave me done it. She was the one who persuaded me.\" And she said, \"The serpent give me an apple.\" All right, preacher, get next to yourself. She said, \"The serpent beguiled me.\" Do you know what \"beguile\" means? Means \"defiled.\" The... The devil never gave her an apple. \"The serpent has beguiled me.\" And then the curse came."
> *— 1958-09-28 · The Serpent's Seed · ¶71*

**Sub-points:**
- "Beguile" means "defiled" — the lie is not merely a trick, it is a defilement; it leaves a mark on the inside
- The serpent worked through agreement, not force — which is why she felt no alarm until it was done
- He came as the most "subtil" — the most reasonable, the most pleasant voice in the garden

### THUS SAITH THE LORD — The Vision
Bride of Christ, hear your Shepherd: the enemy of your soul is not going to ride up to you wearing horns. He comes refined, religious, reasonable, and warm. THUS SAITH THE LORD — in this hour the lie wears a clerical collar and quotes a Bible. So stop watching only for the obvious monster and start guarding the place where you give your *agreement*. The serpent never broke God's gate; he beguiled God's daughter — and the only ones he cannot beguile are the ones who refuse to reason away a single Word.

## POINT 2 — THE WEAPON WAS ONE WORD OFF

**Key Scripture:** "Yea, hath God said, Ye shall not eat of every tree of the garden?" — Genesis 3:1

### The Human Need (Humanistic)
- You ask, "What did he use against me — was it flattery or enchantment?" Both — but underneath both was a *question*: "Are you really sure?"
- He did not deny the Word outright; he loosened it just enough — "Yea, hath God said?" — and once a single Word was up for debate, the whole fortress was open
- We do not fall to a thousand lies; we fall to one — one word added, one word doubted, one reasoning entertained

### Divine Revelation
**The Word says:**
> "And the serpent said unto the woman, Ye shall not surely die." — Genesis 3:4

**The type unfolds:**
God had said, "Thou shalt surely die." The serpent moved exactly *one word*: "Ye shall *not* surely die." He did not throw out the Bible; he revised it by a single syllable, and then he dressed it as more light — "God doth know that in the day ye eat thereof... ye shall be as gods" (Genesis 3:5). Eve's fall did not begin when she ate; it began the moment she took her eyes off the plain Word of God and let a reasoning stand beside it. She let there be *two* ideas where God had given only one. That is the whole science of deception: not to deny the Word, but to add a reasoning to it until you can no longer tell God's voice from the stranger's.

**From the Message:**
> "That's the thing that broke the fellowship in Eden, is because that Eve failed to keep the focus of her looking on God's word, and listened to the reasonings of the enemy, see. See? If she'd just stayed right with, \"God said so, that settles it. God said so.\" But she didn't want to do that, see. She changed the focus of her glass and began to see instead of being just one way, there become two ways, see. She saw, \"Well now, maybe this man could be right. Maybe he is right. Now we know God is right; and maybe he's right. This is just more light on what God said.\" You see there? There's the same thing happens today, exactly right back to the same spot, see. You see two ideas; there's only one. There's not no creed, it's the Word. It's God's word, God's purpose, God's plan. All other plans are no good. That ... and Eve did this evil thing."
> *— 1963-04-21 · Victory Day · ¶33*

**Sub-points:**
- The weapon was not a new religion — it was God's own Word with one word moved
- "More light on what God said" is the deceiver's favorite disguise — addition feels like growth
- The fall was a change of *focus*: from "God said so, that settles it" to "maybe there's another way to see it"

### THUS SAITH THE LORD — The Vision
Church, this is why you were beguiled, and this is the warning for the days ahead: the deceiver does not need to overturn your whole faith — he only needs you to surrender *one Word*. THUS SAITH THE LORD, it is down in this hour to a honed razor's edge — not Methodist against Catholic, but every Word against almost every Word. The antichrist anointing of this age would deceive the very elect with a message that is ninety-nine percent right. So the Bride takes no "more light" that moves a single Word; she keeps her focus on "God said so, that settles it," and a stranger's reasoning she will not follow.

## POINT 3 — THE SAME SERPENT RODE INTO THE CHURCH

**Key Scripture:** "And I saw, and behold a white horse: and he that sat on him had a bow; and a crown was given unto him: and he went forth conquering, and to conquer." — Revelation 6:2

### The Human Need (Humanistic)
- "Am I the only one foolish enough to be fooled?" No — the whole church world was beguiled, and most never felt it happen
- The most dangerous deceptions don't look dark; they look *white* — orderly, unified, respectable, religious
- We trust a crowd, a council, a credential — and that is precisely the door the deceiver walked through to take the early church

### Divine Revelation
**The Word says:**
> "So hast thou also them that hold the doctrine of the Nicolaitanes, which thing I hate." — Revelation 2:15

**The type unfolds:**
When the Lamb opened the first seal, out rode a *white* horse — and the teachers said that surely meant the Holy Spirit conquering the early age. But Brother Branham, by revelation, saw the truth: this rider was the antichrist spirit, riding out under a cover of white righteousness. It is the very same serpent from the garden, only now grown into a doctrine — the Nicolaitanes, "Nico," to conquer the laity, to organize what God had scattered, to build a lodge out of the fellowship of the saints. It began so innocently, so reasonably — "we just want fellowship" — and it ended at the Council of Nicaea with a man crowned on the throne of a beguiled church. The same one-word-off charm that defiled Eve defiled the bride of the first age.

**Sub-points:**
- The white horse rider was not the Holy Spirit — it was the antichrist spirit in a white cloak of false righteousness
- The same serpent voice — "surely He won't do that" — that beguiled Eve organized the early church
- It rode in as a teacher with no crown, and was handed a crown — a throne built on a beguiled people; what beguiled the early church through the councils still beguiles through the great church-unions of our day

The same rider is still in the saddle, and his last great work is to gather all the beguiled into one fold under one head — and to call it unity, love, and righteousness. ## POINT 4 — WHERE THE BRIDE FORTIFIES: KEEP THE FOCUS ON THE WORD

**Key Scripture:** "And a stranger will they not follow, but will flee from him: for they know not the voice of strangers." — John 10:5

### The Human Need (Humanistic)
- Your deepest question is the right one: "Where should I fortify myself so I am never caught again?"
- You cannot out-argue the serpent — Eve tried to reason with him and lost; the only safety is not to enter the debate at all
- The fortress is not more cleverness, more research, or more spiritual experiences — it is a settled heart anchored to "God said so"

### Divine Revelation
**The Word says:**
> "My sheep hear my voice, and I know them, and they follow me." — John 10:27

**The type unfolds:**
Eve's defense did not fail because she lacked the Word — she *quoted* it: "God said not to do it." Her defense failed because she let the focus of her looking move off that Word and onto a reasoning. The shield was never her ability to debate; it was her willingness to refuse the debate and say, "God said so, that settles it." The end-time Bride fortifies in exactly the opposite place from where Eve fell: she does not measure the Word by signs, wonders, oil, blood, or the size of the crowd that believes it — she measures every sign by the Word. When the deceiver brings "more light," she stays with the Bible; anything contrary to it, she leaves alone, no matter how sweet, how reasonable, or how religious it sounds.

**From the Message:**
> "Now, can't you see that you can't put your trust in healing campaigns? You can't put your trust in any kind of a sign like that. The only thing you can put your trust in is thus saith the Lord from the Bible. Now, church, that is exactly where I have tried to keep you, my children. And if something happens to me and God takes me out of this earth, don't you never fail. Remember this with all your heart: stay with that Word! Don't you leave that Word! Anything contrary to it, leave it alone, no matter what it is. Then you know it's right, see. A healing campaign now! It's so filthy, and dirty, and antichrist! You see, it's Satan on a mock campaign, see. He does anything that the Christ can do, but he can't confirm the Word, see. He'll take part of it here and part of it here, but he can't take it altogether, see. He can't get it together. So you see, no wonder the Bible said that would almost fool the very elected if it was possible---the antichrist spirit."
> *— 1964-08-23 · Questions and Answers, Part 2 · ¶5*

**Sub-points:**
- Do not enter the serpent's debate — refuse the question "Yea, hath God said?" with "Thus saith the Lord, that settles it"
- The test is not the sign but the Word: the antichrist can imitate everything Christ does, but he cannot confirm *every* Word together
- The elect know His voice and flee a stranger — not by intellect, but because their names were written in the Lamb's book before the foundation of the world

### THUS SAITH THE LORD — The Vision
This is where you build your wall, child of God: not around your reasoning, but around the Word. THUS SAITH THE LORD — the antichrist spirit can almost fool the very elect, but it cannot get the *whole* Word together, and the Bride is the woman clothed with that whole Word. You will not be beguiled again, not because you have grown too smart for the serpent, but because you have grown too settled in the Word to debate him. Stay with that Word. Anything contrary to it, leave it alone, no matter what it is. That is your fortress, and the gates of hell shall not prevail against it.

## CONCLUSION

### The Epiphany — The Question Answered

> How did the liar get past me — what did he really use against me — and where do I fortify myself so that I am never beguiled again?
>
> "He got past you the same way he got past Eve — not by force but by charm, beguiling you into surrendering one Word, moving your focus off 'God said so' onto a reasonable-sounding lie. What he used against you was never a monster; it was a question — 'Yea, hath God said?' And you fortify yourself in the one place he can never follow: behind every Word of God, refusing his debate, saying 'God said so, that settles it' — for the same God who put enmity between your seed and the serpent's already sent a Redeemer to crush the head of the one who beguiled you."

### Summary
- **Point 1:** He does not break the gate — he beguiles the heart, defiling you through your own agreement, never by force.
- **Point 2:** The weapon was one word off — "Yea, hath God said?" — a single Word surrendered, a reasoning let in beside the Word.
- **Point 3:** The same serpent rode into the church on a white horse — the antichrist spirit cloaked in righteousness, organizing the beguiled from Nicaea to the church-unions of today.
- **Point 4:** The Bride fortifies behind the whole Word — refusing the debate, measuring every sign by Scripture, knowing His voice and fleeing the stranger.

> **The heart of this message:** The serpent beguiles by one word off, but the Bride is kept by every Word of God — and the heel he bruised is the heel that crushed his head.

### A Closing Hymn

**Suggested hymn:** "How Firm a Foundation"
**Why:** The whole sermon drives to one fortress — "Thus saith the Lord from the Bible" — and this hymn declares that the believer's whole confidence rests on the unmoved foundation of God's Word against every reasoning of the enemy.
