The Charm That Took the Shoes

Alternate titles: Flattery or Enchantment? · One Word Off, and the Gate Stood Open
Scriptures: Genesis 3:13–15; Daniel 11:21,32; Revelation 6:1–2; Proverbs 30:18–20 Theme: Deception is Satan’s master-weapon — he does not break the gate, he charms the heart off one Word Prepared: 2026-06-16

The Opening Question

How did the liar get past me — was it flattery or enchantment that he used — and where do I fortify myself so that I am never beguiled again?

This Hour — Prophetic Anchor

The same subtle spirit that whispered to Eve at the tree is riding in this last hour, and Jesus warned it would come so near the truth that “if it were possible, they shall deceive the very elect” (Matthew 24:24). This is not the hour of crude, obvious sin — it is the hour of the polished lie, the flattering voice, the religious counterfeit that keeps just enough of the Word to look right. Daniel saw it centuries away: the vile person who would “obtain the kingdom by flatteries” and “corrupt by flatteries.” The Bride is being separated from the church not by works, signs, or sincerity, but by one thing — whether she will hold to every Word of God or be charmed off of it by one word. This message is a shepherd putting a hedge of the Word around his flock before the deceiver comes again.

Introduction — Setting the Type

Tone: warm and pastoral — open the heart before opening the Word.

The Human Scene

I was six years old when a man promised me a cage full of birds — if I would just take off my shoes and hand them over while he went to fetch them. I gave him my shoes. I never saw the man, the birds, or my shoes again. And here is the strange thing: it was not the bare feet that hurt the most, it was the shame. I lay awake replaying it — how did I not see him coming? Am I that gullible? Beloved, every one of us has a story like that. The smooth voice, the friendly smile, the offer that sounded almost too good — and the small voice inside that knew something was off, but that we talked ourselves out of. That is the oldest wound in the human story. Before there was a man stealing a child’s shoes with a promise of birds, there was a serpent at a tree promising wisdom, and a woman who walked away from that conversation having lost everything — saying, almost in a daze, “I have done no wickedness.” Solomon called it a thing too wonderful for him: “the way of a serpent upon a rock… and the way of a man with a maid” (Proverbs 30:19). How does the deceiver leave no tracks? That is what we want God to show us.

Shadow (OT): Eve in the garden — beguiled, not broken; charmed and talked out of the Word she already had.

Substance (NT / End-time): The church through the ages, and the elect Bride in this last hour, facing the same antichrist spirit dressed in religion and flattery.

Person Profile — Eve

Visible need
Nothing was lacking — she had a garden, a husband, the presence of God, and the spoken Word for her protection.
Invisible need
A craving for wisdom and experience beyond what God had said — “ye shall be as gods, knowing good and evil” (Genesis 3:5).
Motive
She wanted more light, a deeper understanding — not realizing the appetite itself had been planted by the deceiver.
Intention
She intended no rebellion; she believed she was adding to what God said, not subtracting from it.
What God saw
A daughter who had taken her eyes off His Word onto a reasoning — and a love already moving in His heart that would put enmity between her seed and the serpent’s, and send a Redeemer.
Point 1

He Does Not Break In — He Beguiles

“Now the serpent was more subtil than any beast of the field which the LORD God had made.”

Genesis 3:1

The Human Need

  • We picture the devil coming as a monster, so we watch the front gate for something hideous — and he comes to the back door as a friend.
  • The reason we got caught is not that we are stupid; it is that we expected an enemy and met a charmer.
  • A con man never announces himself. He agrees with you, flatters you, makes you feel understood — and the lock he picks is not your door, it is your trust.

Divine Revelation

“And the LORD God said unto the woman, What is this that thou hast done? And the woman said, The serpent beguiled me, and I did eat.”

Genesis 3:13

Notice Eve does not say, “The serpent forced me,” or “The serpent overpowered me.” She says, “The serpent beguiled me” — he charmed me, he won me over, he defiled me through agreement. The most subtle beast of the field did not break the commandment over her; he talked her into breaking it herself, so that her own hand reached out and took. That is the signature of the deceiver in every age: he never makes you his victim, he makes you his accomplice. He did not steal the fruit and put it in her mouth — he made her want it, and then she called it her own choice.

“I am told that a snake can catch the eye of a bird and charm that bird with its enchantments, and the stare of its eyes, until such a way, if that bird will give the snake its attention for just a few minutes, that bird will flutter and completely become paralyzed. And the snake will take the bird. And I can believe that, because I know of another serpent, the devil, that if he could ever catch the eye with his enchantments, with your modern rock-n-roll, and all your fandangles of the world, if he can ever catch you, young lady or young man, just long enough to get his charm in you, you’ll flutter, but you can’t get loose. He’ll hold you till he swallows you up in sin.”

— 1958-10-03 · Looking at the unseen · ¶32
  • “Beguile” means “defiled” — the lie is not merely a trick, it is a defilement; it leaves a mark on the inside.
  • The serpent worked through agreement, not force — which is why she felt no alarm until it was done.
  • He came as the most “subtil” — the most reasonable, the most pleasant voice in the garden; the charm holds you only as long as you give it your attention.

Thus Saith the Lord — The Vision

Bride of Christ, hear your Shepherd: the enemy of your soul is not going to ride up to you wearing horns. He comes refined, religious, reasonable, and warm. Thus saith the Lord — in this hour the lie wears a clerical collar and quotes a Bible. So stop watching only for the obvious monster, and start guarding the place where you give your attention and your agreement. The serpent never broke God’s gate; he beguiled God’s daughter — and the only ones he cannot beguile are the ones who will not give him their eye for even a moment.

Point 2

The Weapon Was Flattery — “He Shall Come In Peaceably”

“And in his estate shall stand up a vile person, to whom they shall not give the honour of the kingdom: but he shall come in peaceably, and obtain the kingdom by flatteries.”

Daniel 11:21

The Human Need

  • You asked, “What did he use against me — was it flattery or enchantment?” Beloved, the Bible names the weapon by name, and it is flattery.
  • Flattery is a lie that feels like love. “You have the most beautiful eyes in the world” works where an argument never could — because it bypasses your reason and warms your pride.
  • The man who took my shoes did not threaten me; he befriended me. He came in peaceably. That is exactly how the deceiver always comes — not with a sword, but with a compliment and a promise.

Divine Revelation

“And the serpent said unto the woman, Ye shall not surely die… ye shall be as gods, knowing good and evil.”

Genesis 3:4–5

Watch the serpent’s method — it is pure flattery. He does not insult Eve; he elevates her: “ye shall be as gods.” He tells her she is destined for more, that her eyes will be opened, that she is too wise to be kept in the dark. Daniel, looking down the long corridor of the church ages, saw that very same spirit stand up in a vile person who would not be given the kingdom but would obtain it — how? “By flatteries.” He comes in peaceably. He does not conquer the church with an army; he flatters his way onto her throne. The same honeyed voice that told Eve she was god-like told the early church she was destined to rule and reign — and crowned a man over a beguiled people. Brother Branham read that very verse over the first seal:

“Incarnate, he become a man and then they crowned him. Read, notice... or read rather, how Daniel said ‘he will take over the church kingdoms.’ Would you like to read it? We’ve got time to do that, haven’t we? All right. Let’s go back to Daniel just a moment. Turn back to the book of Daniel and we’ll read just a moment. And we won’t be, maybe another fifteen or twenty minutes, or thirty, or something. All right. Let’s get Daniel the 11th chapter, and let’s take the 21st verse. Here’s Daniel. Daniel is speaking now how this fellow is going to take over. And in his estate shall stand up a vile person ^^ [Rome talking] , ^^ to whom they shall not give the honour of the kingdom ^^ [now, watch] ^^: but he shall come in peaceable, and obtain the kingdom by flatteries.”

— 1963-03-18 · Seals 3: The First Seal · ¶137
  • Flattery is the antichrist’s diplomacy — he obtains by charm what he could never seize by force.
  • “Come in peaceably” is the disguise: the wolf wrapped in a lamb’s gentleness, contrary to the Word underneath.
  • What flattery offers is always a promotion off of your place in the Word — “ye shall be as gods,” “you deserve more light,” “surely you’re too wise to be held back.”

Thus Saith the Lord — The Vision

Church, this is why you were taken, and this is the warning for the days ahead: the deceiver does not kick down your door — he flatters his way through it. Thus saith the Lord, the spirit that is gathering the church world in this hour comes in peaceably, with compliments and unity and warm words, and it is obtaining the kingdom by flatteries even now. The Bride is not flattered off her foundation. When the smooth voice tells her she deserves more than the plain Word, she answers as her Lord did in the wilderness: “It is written.”

Point 3

One Word Off, and the Fortress Stood Open

“Yea, hath God said, Ye shall not eat of every tree of the garden?”

Genesis 3:1

The Human Need

  • Underneath the flattery was a question: “Are you really sure?” Once a single Word was up for debate, the whole fortress was open.
  • He did not deny the Word outright; he loosened it just enough — “Yea, hath God said?”
  • We do not fall to a thousand lies; we fall to one — one word added, one word doubted, one reasoning entertained.

Divine Revelation

“And the serpent said unto the woman, Ye shall not surely die.”

Genesis 3:4

God had said, “Thou shalt surely die.” The serpent moved exactly one word: “Ye shall not surely die.” He did not throw out the Bible; he revised it by a single syllable, and dressed it as more light — “God doth know that in the day ye eat thereof… ye shall be as gods.” Eve’s fall did not begin when she ate; it began the moment she took her eyes off the plain Word and let a reasoning stand beside it. She let there be two ideas where God had given only one. That is the whole science of deception: not to deny the Word, but to add a reasoning to it until you can no longer tell God’s voice from the stranger’s.

“That’s the thing that broke the fellowship in Eden, is because that Eve failed to keep the focus of her looking on God’s word, and listened to the reasonings of the enemy, see. See? If she’d just stayed right with, ‘God said so, that settles it. God said so.’ But she didn’t want to do that, see. She changed the focus of her glass and began to see instead of being just one way, there become two ways, see. She saw, ‘Well now, maybe this man could be right. Maybe he is right. Now we know God is right; and maybe he’s right. This is just more light on what God said.’ You see there? There’s the same thing happens today, exactly right back to the same spot, see. You see two ideas; there’s only one. There’s not no creed, it’s the Word. It’s God’s word, God’s purpose, God’s plan. All other plans are no good. That ... and Eve did this evil thing.”

— 1963-04-21 · Victory Day · ¶33
  • The weapon was not a new religion — it was God’s own Word with one word moved.
  • “More light on what God said” is the deceiver’s favorite disguise — addition feels like growth.
  • The fall was a change of focus: from “God said so, that settles it” to “maybe there’s another way to see it.”

Thus Saith the Lord — The Vision

Church, this is the warning for the days ahead: the deceiver does not need to overturn your whole faith — he only needs you to surrender one Word. Thus saith the Lord, it is down in this hour to a honed razor’s edge — not Methodist against Catholic, but every Word against almost every Word. The antichrist anointing of this age would deceive the very elect with a message that is ninety-nine percent right. So the Bride takes no “more light” that moves a single Word; she keeps her focus on “God said so, that settles it,” and a stranger’s reasoning she will not follow.

Point 4

The Same Serpent Rode Into the Church on a White Horse

“And I saw, and behold a white horse: and he that sat on him had a bow; and a crown was given unto him: and he went forth conquering, and to conquer.”

Revelation 6:2

The Human Need

  • “Am I the only one foolish enough to be fooled?” No — the whole church world was beguiled, and most never felt it happen.
  • The most dangerous deceptions don’t look dark; they look white — orderly, unified, respectable, religious.
  • We trust a crowd, a council, a credential — and that is precisely the door the deceiver walked through to take the early church.

Divine Revelation

“So hast thou also them that hold the doctrine of the Nicolaitanes, which thing I hate.”

Revelation 2:15

When the Lamb opened the first seal, out rode a white horse — and the teachers said that surely meant the Holy Spirit conquering the early age. But Brother Branham, by revelation, saw the truth: this rider was the antichrist spirit, riding out under a cover of white righteousness. It is the very same serpent from the garden, only now grown into a doctrine — the Nicolaitanes, “Nico,” to conquer the laity, to organize what God had scattered, to build a lodge out of the fellowship of the saints. It began so innocently, so reasonably — “we just want fellowship” — and it ended at the Council of Nicaea with a man crowned over a beguiled church. The same flattering, one-word-off charm that defiled Eve defiled the bride of the first age.

“Now, first stage---white horse. He was just a teacher, but an antichrist spirit in its doctrine and its quality. It was innocent. It couldn’t hurt nothing, seemed like. Just going on... That’s the way Satan comes in. Oh, he’s a slick bird. He told Eve, ‘Now, you know you’re looking for wisdom. You don’t know what’s right and wrong.’ Said, ‘Now, if your eyes were opened, you would know what...’ And said, ‘The fruit is very pleasant. It’s good. It is pleasant to the eye. You should take it. Now you don’t know whether it is or not, do you?’ ‘No, I don’t, but God said not to do it.’ ‘But, oh!’ ‘Well, I know, but God said He would...’ ‘Surely He won’t to do that.’ Just as sweet as it could be. Look what it did. Watch this antichrist spirit rise up amongst the early church, a doctrine of Nicolaitanes. ‘Nico,’ conquer the laity, make a holy man. ‘Why, we just want fellowship. Why, you’re scattered out here. Nobody knows where nobody’s at. I think we all ought to have an organization and just be different. We go right ... put ourselves together. We ought to make a lodge out of it.’”

— 1963-03-20 · Seals 5: The Third Seal · ¶104
  • The white horse rider was not the Holy Spirit — it was the antichrist spirit in a white cloak of false righteousness.
  • The same serpent voice — “surely He won’t do that” — that beguiled Eve organized the early church.
  • It rode in as a teacher with no crown, and was handed a crown — a throne built on a beguiled people. What beguiled the early church through the councils still beguiles through the great church-unions of our day.

Thus Saith the Lord — The Vision

Beloved, lift your eyes and see what is moving in the earth right now. The same rider is still in the saddle, and his last great work is to gather all the beguiled into one fold under one head — and to call it unity, love, and righteousness. Thus saith the Lord, the white horse rider is riding to his confederation in this very hour, and the Spirit of Christ in the Bride is calling His own out of it. You were not the only one beguiled — an entire church world has been flattered onto that white horse. But the elect Bride knows the rider is not the Holy Spirit, no matter how white his robe, because she stays with the Lamb and His Book.

Point 5

Where the Bride Fortifies: “The People That Know Their God”

“And such as do wickedly against the covenant shall he corrupt by flatteries: but the people that do know their God shall be strong, and do exploits.”

Daniel 11:32

The Human Need

  • Your deepest question is the right one: “Where should I fortify myself so I am never caught again?”
  • You cannot out-argue the serpent — Eve tried to reason with him and lost; the only safety is not to enter the debate at all.
  • The fortress is not more cleverness, more research, or more spiritual experiences — it is a settled heart that knows God by His Word.

Divine Revelation

“And a stranger will they not follow, but will flee from him: for they know not the voice of strangers… My sheep hear my voice, and I know them, and they follow me.”

John 10:5, 27

Daniel draws the line in one verse: the same flatteries that corrupt those who do wickedly cannot move “the people that do know their God.” Mark the difference — it is not the clever who stand, it is those who know Him. Eve’s defense did not fail because she lacked the Word; she quoted it: “God said not to do it.” Her defense failed because she let the focus of her looking move off that Word onto a reasoning. The shield was never her ability to debate; it was her willingness to refuse the debate and say, “God said so, that settles it.” The end-time Bride fortifies in exactly the opposite place from where Eve fell: she does not measure the Word by signs, wonders, oil, blood, or the size of the crowd that believes it — she measures every sign by the Word. When the flatterer brings “more light,” she stays with the Bible; anything contrary to it, she leaves alone, no matter how sweet, how reasonable, or how religious it sounds.

“Now, can’t you see that you can’t put your trust in healing campaigns? You can’t put your trust in any kind of a sign like that. The only thing you can put your trust in is thus saith the Lord from the Bible. Now, church, that is exactly where I have tried to keep you, my children. And if something happens to me and God takes me out of this earth, don’t you never fail. Remember this with all your heart: stay with that Word! Don’t you leave that Word! Anything contrary to it, leave it alone, no matter what it is. Then you know it’s right, see. A healing campaign now! Sinful men that even shock their own managers with their vulgar, and dirt, and filth; and people throw away their crutches and get healed by looking at these boys. It’s so filthy, and dirty, and antichrist! You see, it’s Satan on a mock campaign, see. He does anything that the Christ can do, but he can’t confirm the Word, see. He’ll take part of it here and part of it here, but he can’t take it altogether, see. He can’t get it together. So you see, no wonder the Bible said that would almost fool the very elected if it was possible---the antichrist spirit.”

— 1964-08-23 · Questions and Answers, Part 2 · ¶5
  • Do not enter the serpent’s debate — refuse the question “Yea, hath God said?” with “Thus saith the Lord, that settles it.”
  • The test is not the sign but the Word: the antichrist can imitate everything Christ does, but he cannot confirm every Word together.
  • The bird breaks the snake’s charm only by lifting its head and looking up — so the Bride breaks every enchantment by taking her eyes off the reasoning and fixing them on the Word and the One who spoke it.
  • The elect know His voice and flee a stranger — not by intellect, but because their names were written in the Lamb’s book before the foundation of the world.

Thus Saith the Lord — The Vision

This is where you build your wall, child of God: not around your reasoning, but around the Word. Thus saith the Lord — the flatteries that corrupt the wicked cannot corrupt the people that know their God; the antichrist spirit can almost fool the very elect, but it cannot get the whole Word together, and the Bride is the woman clothed with that whole Word. You will not be beguiled again, not because you have grown too smart for the serpent, but because you have grown too settled in the Word to debate him. Stay with that Word. Anything contrary to it, leave it alone, no matter what it is. That is your fortress, and the gates of hell shall not prevail against it.

Conclusion

How did the liar get past me — was it flattery or enchantment that he used — and where do I fortify myself so that I am never beguiled again?

He got past you the same way he got past Eve — not by force but by flattery, coming in peaceably, charming you into surrendering one Word and moving your focus off “God said so” onto a sweet-sounding lie. It was both flattery and enchantment: the compliment that warmed your pride and the stare that held your eye. But the Word that names the weapon also names the fortress — “the people that do know their God shall be strong.” You fortify yourself in the one place he can never follow: behind every Word of God, refusing his debate, knowing His voice, lifting your head off the charmer and onto the Lamb — for the same God who put enmity between your seed and the serpent’s already sent a Redeemer to crush the head of the one who beguiled you.

The Sermon in Miniature

  • Point 1: He does not break the gate — he beguiles the heart, defiling you through your own agreement, never by force.
  • Point 2: The weapon was flattery — “he shall come in peaceably, and obtain the kingdom by flatteries” — a lie that feels like love.
  • Point 3: One word off opened the fortress — “Yea, hath God said?” — a single Word surrendered, a reasoning let in beside the Word.
  • Point 4: The same serpent rode into the church on a white horse — the antichrist spirit cloaked in righteousness, organizing the beguiled from Nicaea to the church-unions of today.
  • Point 5: The Bride fortifies as “the people that know their God” — refusing the debate, measuring every sign by Scripture, knowing His voice and fleeing the stranger.

The serpent beguiles by flattery and one word off, but the Bride is kept by every Word of God — and the heel he bruised is the heel that crushed his head.

Why: The whole sermon drives to one fortress — “Thus saith the Lord from the Bible” — and this hymn declares that the believer’s whole confidence rests on the unmoved foundation of God’s Word against every flattery and reasoning of the enemy.
