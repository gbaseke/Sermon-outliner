## INTRODUCTION --- SETTING THE TYPE

**Tone:** Warm and pastoral --- open the heart before opening the Word.

### The Human Scene (Humanistic)
There is a day we seldom preach about. We love to preach Good Friday --- the sacrifice, the blood, the finished work. We love to preach Easter Sunday --- the empty tomb, the risen Lord, the angels in white. But between Friday and Sunday there is a day we almost skip over entirely: Saturday. Dark Saturday. The day when the body of Jesus lay cold in a borrowed tomb. The day when Roman soldiers stood guard over a sealed stone. The day when the disciples hid behind locked doors, weeping, confused, and bitterly disappointed. Everything they had believed in, everything they had given their lives for, seemed to be finished. The Teacher was dead. The miracles were over. The Kingdom had not come. It was the darkest day in human history --- and God said nothing. Have you ever had a Saturday like that? A day between the promise and the fulfillment, where heaven is silent and the stone is sealed? That is where many of God's people find themselves right now.

### The Type Established
- **Shadow (OT):** Job on the ash heap --- everything stripped away, wife saying "Curse God and die," friends accusing, God seemingly silent
- **Substance (NT/End-time):** The Bride in the squeeze --- pressed on every side, the world against her, denominations sealed shut, but the purpose of God undefeatable

**Branham on this type:**
> "There was too much of you in you, till God had to squeeze it out through trials. And while that squeezing was coming on, it's hard. But after awhile the skies clear back, and you see the purpose of God, and you cry, 'I know my Redeemer liveth, and at the last days He'll stand on the earth. Though the skin worms destroys this body, yet in my flesh shall I see God.' These little trials and things are just for a moment, and they fade and pass away; but they're only done for your good --- let us remember that --- that God would get glory."
> *--- 1960-04-17 - I Know - p47*

### Person Profile: The Disciples on Saturday
- **Visible need:** Safety, direction, a leader --- their Master was dead and buried
- **Invisible need:** A revelation that what looked like defeat was the very mechanism of God's victory
- **Motive:** Fear and grief drove them into hiding; they were protecting themselves from the same fate
- **Intention:** To survive the fallout of following a Man the world had killed
- **What God saw:** Chosen vessels, already sealed in His purpose, who only needed one more sunrise to become the most powerful witnesses the world has ever known

## POINT 1 --- DARK SATURDAY: WHEN THE STONE IS SEALED

**Key Scripture:** "So they went, and made the sepulchre sure, sealing the stone, and setting a watch." --- Matthew 27:66

### The Human Need (Humanistic)
- Sometimes life gives you a Friday you did not expect --- a diagnosis, a death, a divorce, a betrayal, a collapse of everything you trusted in
- But the hardest part is not the blow itself; it is the Saturday that follows --- the silence, the waiting, the not knowing
- Friday has drama; Sunday has triumph. But Saturday has nothing but a sealed stone and unanswered questions
- Many believers are living in a Saturday right now --- they have had their Friday, and Sunday has not come yet

### Divine Revelation
**The Word says:**
> "Command therefore that the sepulchre be made sure until the third day, lest his disciples come by night, and steal him away." --- Matthew 27:64

**The type unfolds:**
Notice what the Pharisees did: they sealed the stone with the Roman seal. They posted armed guards around the tomb. They did everything in their power to make sure that the purpose of God could not come to pass. They were trying to keep dead what God had already purposed to raise. And that is exactly what the enemy tries to do in your life. When disappointment comes, the devil seals it. He puts a guard around your heart and says, "Stay in that tomb. It is over. This is the end." But beloved, no Roman seal has ever held back the purpose of God.

**From the Message:**
> "Satan could not take them. He could not drown Noah in the flood until the purpose of God had been finished. He could not burn up the Hebrew children until the purpose of God had been finished. He could not kill Job with boils and troubles until the purpose of God had been finished. Neither could the lions eat Daniel until God's purpose had been finished. Neither could death and old-age take Abraham until the purpose of God had been finished. And neither can it take you or can it take me until the purpose of God of our life is finished. So we gather from there a consolation."
> *--- 1960-04-17 - I Know - p22*

**Sub-points:**
- The seal was never meant to keep Jesus in; it was meant to prove, beyond all doubt, that only God could have brought Him out
- Your sealed stone is not evidence that God has failed --- it is the very setup for the greatest demonstration of His power
- The darker the Saturday, the more glorious the Sunday

### THUS SAITH THE LORD --- The Vision
Church, the world has sealed the stone over this Message. They have said, "That old prophet's dead. That old-time religion is finished." The denominations have set their guard. But I want to declare to you: no Roman seal, no World Council of Churches, no government edict can hold back what God has purposed to raise. The Bride of Christ will come forth. The Third Pull will operate. Sunday is on its way.

## POINT 2 --- WHEN DISAPPOINTMENT DRIVES YOU BACKWARD: PETER GOES FISHING

**Key Scripture:** "Simon Peter saith unto them, I go a fishing. They say unto him, We also go with thee. They went forth, and entered into a ship immediately; and that night they caught nothing." --- John 21:3

### The Human Need (Humanistic)
- After the crucifixion, Peter did what so many of us do when we are disappointed: he went back to what he knew before he ever met Jesus
- He picked up the old nets. He went back to the old trade. He said, in essence, "It was a beautiful dream, but it is over now. I will go back to being a fisherman."
- And notice --- the others followed him. Disappointment is contagious. When a leader goes backward, others go with him
- Have you ever been there? After a season of disappointment, have you quietly gone back to an old habit, an old way of thinking, an old way of living --- because you lost hope?

### Divine Revelation
**The Word says:**
> "And that night they caught nothing." --- John 21:3b

**The type unfolds:**
Peter was the finest fisherman in Galilee, and he fished all night and caught nothing. Why? Because God will not bless a backward step. You cannot go back to the old nets once you have been called to be a fisher of men. The empty net was God's mercy --- He would not let Peter succeed at going backward. When your old life stops working, it is not punishment; it is the love of God refusing to let you settle for less than His purpose.

**From the Message:**
> "How they were so disappointed! They had went away to their fishing nets again. Peter said, 'I go fishing.' The disciples said, 'I'll just go with you.' They were altogether tore up, disappointed. There they took His body down, wrapped it in some linen, and laid it in the grave. And said, 'That's the finish of that fanatic.'"
> *--- 1953-04-05 - Go Tell My Disciples - p35*

**Person Profile: Simon Peter**
- **Visible need:** Direction, provision --- he had no income, no teacher, no plan
- **Invisible need:** A fresh encounter with the risen Christ that would burn away his shame and reignite his calling
- **Motive:** Self-preservation and grief; he defaulted to the only identity he had before Jesus called him
- **Intention:** To make a living, to survive, to find normalcy in the wreckage
- **What God saw:** The rock upon which He would build His Church --- a man who only needed to hear one word from the shore to leap back into purpose

**Sub-points:**
- Disappointment always tries to push you backward, but God's purpose always moves forward
- An empty net is sometimes God's loudest sermon --- He is saying, "This is not where you belong"
- Peter fished all night in his own strength and caught nothing; one word from Jesus filled the net to breaking

### THUS SAITH THE LORD --- The Vision
Bride of Christ, if you have been going backward --- if disappointment has sent you back to the old nets, back to the old ways, back to the old thinking --- hear the Word of the Lord: there is nothing for you back there. The nets are empty. But the One standing on the shore of your life has a word for you, and when you obey it, the nets will break with the fullness of what He has prepared.

## POINT 3 --- THE STRANGER ON THE SHORE: WHEN GOD COMES AFTER YOU

**Key Scripture:** "But when the morning was now come, Jesus stood on the shore: but the disciples knew not that it was Jesus." --- John 21:4

### The Human Need (Humanistic)
- In their darkest hour, the disciples did not go looking for Jesus --- Jesus came looking for them
- He stood on the shore in the early morning, and they did not even recognize Him. That is what disappointment does: it blinds you to the very presence of the One who has come to deliver you
- How many of us have had God standing right in front of us and we could not see Him because our eyes were full of tears and our hearts were full of grief?

### Divine Revelation
**The Word says:**
> "Jesus saith unto them, Children, have ye any meat? They answered him, No. And he said unto them, Cast the net on the right side of the ship, and ye shall find." --- John 21:5-6

**The type unfolds:**
Jesus did not rebuke them. He did not say, "Why did you leave your post?" He simply asked a question: "Have you caught anything?" And when they said no, He gave them a direction. That is how God deals with disappointed people --- not with condemnation, but with a new instruction. He said, "Cast the net on the other side." You have been fishing on the wrong side of the boat, beloved. Your disappointment has had you looking in the wrong direction. God is saying to you tonight: throw your net to the other side.

**From the Message:**
> "He thought he was a great man, chopped the high priest's son's ears off like that. Well, he thought he was a great fellow, you know, he had learned something. But he knowed nothing. He had to forget all about it. And there he was out there, said, 'Well, I know one thing, I'm a fisherman. I can still make a living, fishing.' He cast all night and didn't get nothing. Oh, what a discourage! Every time he pulled it up, an empty net. And he was so discouraged!"
> *--- 1961-11-19 - Perfect Strength by Perfect Weakness - p134*

**Sub-points:**
- God does not wait for you to get yourself together before He comes to you --- He meets you at your lowest
- Sometimes the greatest revelation comes disguised as a stranger's question
- Obedience to God's direction, even when it makes no natural sense, is the way out of disappointment
- When Peter obeyed, the net was so full it nearly broke --- God always over-answers the obedient heart

### THUS SAITH THE LORD --- The Vision
In this hour, the Bride may feel she has been fishing all night in darkness with nothing to show for it. But the Morning Star is standing on the shore. He is not far off. He is right here, right now. And His instruction to you is the same: cast your net on the other side. Stop looking at what you have lost. Start looking at what He is about to give. Obey the voice of the Spirit, and the net will not be able to hold what is coming.

## POINT 4 --- JOB'S WIFE AND THE VOICE OF SURRENDER: WHEN THOSE CLOSEST TO YOU TELL YOU TO QUIT

**Key Scripture:** "Then said his wife unto him, Dost thou still retain thine integrity? curse God, and die." --- Job 2:9

### The Human Need (Humanistic)
- One of the cruelest dimensions of disappointment is when the people closest to you --- the ones you love and trust --- tell you to give up
- Job had lost his children, his livestock, his health, and his standing. And then the one person who should have stood by him looked at him and said, "Just curse God and die. It is not worth it."
- Sometimes it is not the enemy outside that nearly breaks you; it is the voice inside your own house

### Divine Revelation
**The Word says:**
> "But he said unto her, Thou speakest as one of the foolish women speaketh. What? shall we receive good at the hand of God, and shall we not receive evil? In all this did not Job sin with his lips." --- Job 2:10

**The type unfolds:**
Job is the oldest book in the Bible, and it gives us the oldest answer to the question of suffering. Job's wife represents the voice of the flesh --- the voice that says, "If God were real, He would not let this happen. Give up." But Job refused. He did not call her a foolish woman; he said she spoke like one. He kept his integrity. And in the end, it was Job --- not his wife, not his friends, not his accusers --- who received the revelation: "I know that my Redeemer liveth."

**From the Message:**
> "Even his wife turned against him sitting out on this ash heap scraping himself with the ... of the boils that was on him. His children was all killed, and his sheep gone. Everything looked like had happened to him, but still Job stayed right there. And his wife come out, said, 'Job, why don't you curse God and die?' He said, 'Thou speakest like a foolish woman.' Now, he never said she was foolish; said she spoke like one. Said, 'You speak like a foolish woman. The Lord gave, the Lord taken away. Blessed be the name of the Lord.'"
> *--- 1955-06-05 - Fellowship Through the Reconciliation of the Blood - p41*

**Person Profile: Job**
- **Visible need:** Health, family, restoration --- everything a man could need had been stripped away
- **Invisible need:** A vision of the coming Redeemer that would anchor his soul beyond the ash heap
- **Motive:** Integrity --- he refused to abandon what he knew to be true
- **Intention:** To hold fast to God even if it cost him everything, including the support of his wife
- **What God saw:** A prophet, a seed --- a man in whom the Word could be revealed, who would stand when every voice around him said "Fall"

**Sub-points:**
- Not every voice that speaks to you in your dark hour is a voice from God --- test every word against the Word
- The voice of surrender often comes from someone who loves you but does not have your revelation
- Holding your integrity in the darkness is the very thing that positions you for the vision

### THUS SAITH THE LORD --- The Vision
Bride, there are voices in this hour telling you to compromise. Voices saying, "Just go along with the system. Just join the Council. Just give up on the old-time way." But like Job, you speak back to that voice: "The Lord gave, and the Lord taken away; blessed be the name of the Lord." Your integrity in this dark hour is not stubbornness --- it is the evidence that a predestinated seed will not deny the Word, no matter what it costs.

## POINT 5 --- THE PROPHET'S DARK SATURDAY: WILLIAM BRANHAM AND THE FURNACE OF AFFLICTION

**Key Scripture:** "For I reckon that the sufferings of this present time are not worthy to be compared with the glory which shall be revealed in us." --- Romans 8:18

### The Human Need (Humanistic)
- If you think God only uses people who have never been disappointed, consider the prophet of this age
- William Branham lost his wife Hope and his baby Sharon Rose within days of each other, right after the 1937 Ohio River flood. He was a young man, barely started in the ministry, and everything was taken from him
- He walked up to that graveyard and wept over their graves, questioning everything

### Divine Revelation
**The Word says:**
> "And we know that all things work together for good to them that love God, to them who are the called according to his purpose." --- Romans 8:28

**The type unfolds:**
God was after William Branham. Not to destroy him --- to shape him. The furnace of affliction was not punishment for disobedience; it was preparation for a ministry that would shake the world. The same man who wept at that graveside became the man through whom the Pillar of Fire would be photographed, the Seven Seals would be opened, and the Bride would be called out. His Dark Saturday became the foundation for the greatest prophetic ministry since the apostles.

**From the Message:**
> "I was sick one time when I lost my wife; I lost my baby; lost my father, and lost my brother, and lost my sister-in-law. And Billy was laying, dying, and I was just about gone. I was going up the road crying, going to her grave (her and the baby, and the baby on her arm) --- going to the grave."
> *--- 1962-12-30 - The Absolute - p125*

> "When I lost my baby, my little Sharon, that was one thing that stumbled me. I said, 'How can that be for the good? How can it be for the good?' And months later, when I seen her standing there in all the beauty of a young girl, speaking to me... I knowed if she had lived she might have turned out wrong. God had to take her while she was tender and sweet. I know I'll see her again."
> *--- 1960-04-17 - I Know - p46*

**Person Profile: William Branham**
- **Visible need:** A wife, a family, health, stability --- the basic necessities of a young man trying to serve God
- **Invisible need:** A breaking that would make him completely dependent on the supernatural, a vessel emptied of self
- **Motive:** He loved God and wanted to serve Him, but was torn between obedience and the counsel of others
- **Intention:** To obey the call of God on his life, even when everything around him fell apart
- **What God saw:** The seventh angel messenger --- a man whose brokenness would become the very channel through which the mysteries of God would flow to the end-time Bride

**Sub-points:**
- God does not waste a single tear. Every loss in Branham's life was used to forge a deeper compassion and a sharper anointing
- The man who lost his baby girl could never turn away a cross-eyed child in a prayer line --- his pain became his ministry
- Your Dark Saturday is not the end of your story; it is the preparation for a purpose you cannot yet see

### THUS SAITH THE LORD --- The Vision
If God could take the ashes of William Branham's grief and raise up the greatest healing and prophetic ministry of the twentieth century, what do you think He can do with your disappointment? Do not despise the furnace, Bride. It is the place where God removes the dross and reveals the gold. Your Dark Saturday is simply evidence that God has something so great ahead of you that the enemy is trying everything he can to stop you before you get there.

## POINT 6 --- THE ANGEL IN THE PRISON: DELIVERANCE THROUGH THE MESSAGE OF YOUR DAY

**Key Scripture:** "And, behold, the angel of the Lord came upon him, and a light shined in the prison: and he smote Peter on the side, and raised him up, saying, Arise up quickly. And his chains fell off from his hands." --- Acts 12:7

### The Human Need (Humanistic)
- Peter was in prison, chained between two soldiers, awaiting execution. Herod had already killed James. Peter was next. It looked hopeless
- But notice: the church was praying. They did not know how God would answer, but they prayed without ceasing
- And God sent an angel --- a light shined in the prison, and the chains fell off

### Divine Revelation
**The Word says:**
> "And his chains fell off from his hands." --- Acts 12:7b

**The type unfolds:**
An angel is a messenger. The word "angel" means "messenger." God delivers His people through a message. In every age, when God's people were in bondage, He sent a messenger with a message, and the chains fell off. In the days of Moses, the message was "Let my people go." In the days of Luther, the message was "The just shall live by faith." And in this last day, God sent a prophet-messenger with the fullness of the Word, and that Message is the angel that walks into your prison cell tonight. The light that shone in Peter's prison is the same light that shines through the Message of the hour.

**From the Message:**
> "The word angel means the 'messenger.' And just got through, in the 1st book here, 'God, in sundry times and divers manner, spoke to the fathers by the prophets.' That was God's messengers. And they were God's angels. A messenger is an angel; or an angel is the messenger, rather."
> *--- 1957-08-25 - Hebrews Chapter 2 Part 1 - p20*

> "The reason people in Noah's day did not go into the ark --- because they never recognized the message nor the messenger. That's the only reason they perished, is because they didn't recognize the hour that they were living."
> *--- 1964-07-26 - Recognizing Your Day and Its Message - p41*

**Sub-points:**
- Every age has had its prison and its angel. The prison of this age is denominational creeds, worldly systems, and unbelief. The angel of this age is the Message of the hour
- Peter thought he was dreaming when the angel came --- sometimes deliverance is so supernatural you can hardly believe it is real
- The church prayed without ceasing, and God answered beyond what they expected. Keep praying, Bride

### THUS SAITH THE LORD --- The Vision
You are delivered through the Message of your day. When you recognize the angel --- when you recognize the messenger God sent to this age and receive the Word he brought --- the chains fall off. The prison doors open. The guards cannot hold you. You walk out free. That is what the Message does: it shines a light in the darkest prison cell of disappointment and says, "Arise up quickly."

## POINT 7 --- THE SQUEEZE IS COMING, BUT SO IS SUNDAY: END-TIME APPLICATION

**Key Scripture:** "For I reckon that the sufferings of this present time are not worthy to be compared with the glory which shall be revealed in us." --- Romans 8:18

### The Human Need (Humanistic)
- We are living in the squeeze. The pressure on the Bride is increasing --- economically, spiritually, socially
- The stone has been sealed. The guard has been set. The world says it is finished
- And it feels like Saturday --- that long, silent, hopeless Saturday between Friday's suffering and Sunday's resurrection

### Divine Revelation
**The Word says:**
> "In the end of the sabbath, as it began to dawn toward the first day of the week, came Mary Magdalene and the other Mary to see the sepulchre. And, behold, there was a great earthquake: for the angel of the Lord descended from heaven, and came and rolled back the stone from the door, and sat upon it." --- Matthew 28:1-2

**The type unfolds:**
The Roman seal was the most powerful legal instrument in the ancient world. To break it meant death. And yet, on that first Easter morning, an angel descended from heaven and broke it as though it were nothing. He did not even move the stone to let Jesus out --- Jesus was already gone. He moved the stone to let the witnesses in, to see that the tomb was empty. The enemy's greatest effort was God's smallest obstacle.

**From the Message:**
> "Fire cannot defeat the purpose of God's plan. And, oh, how I love to say this this morning. All the hydrogen bombs in the world can never defeat the resurrection of His church."
> *--- 1958-04-06 - I Know My Redeemer Liveth - p17*

> "Sin can't stop the purpose of God! God will not be defeated!"
> *--- 1958-06-19 - Ye Must Be Born Again - p44*

**Sub-points:**
- The squeeze is real, but it is Saturday --- and Sunday always follows Saturday
- The Third Pull is God's answer to the squeeze: when the pressure becomes the greatest, the power will become the most evident
- The angel who broke the Roman seal is the same Holy Spirit that will break every chain on the Bride and catch her away

### THUS SAITH THE LORD --- The Vision
Bride of Christ, hear me: the squeeze is your Dark Saturday. But just as surely as Friday led to Sunday, the squeeze will lead to the resurrection --- the catching away of the Bride. Do not fear the sealed stone. Do not fear the guard. The angel is already descending. The earthquake is already rumbling. And when that stone rolls away, it will not be to let you out --- you will already be free --- it will be to show the world that the tomb is empty and the Bride is gone. Hold fast. Sunday is coming.

## CONCLUSION

### The Epiphany --- The Question Answered

> How do I remain hopeful when I am disappointed --- when I have done all I know to do, and the heavens are silent, and the stone is sealed, and it feels like God has forgotten me?
>
> "You remain hopeful because no disappointment --- not a sealed stone, not an empty net, not a prison cell, not the loss of everything you love --- has ever stopped the purpose of God. Saturday is not the end of the story. It is the necessary space between the sacrifice and the resurrection. And if God is silent on your Saturday, it is only because He is already preparing your Sunday."

### Summary

- **Point 1:** The sealed stone is not evidence of defeat --- it is the setup for the greatest demonstration of God's power.
- **Point 2:** When disappointment drives you backward to the old nets, God will make them come up empty to show you that your future is with Him.
- **Point 3:** Jesus does not wait for you to find Him --- He comes to the shore of your darkest hour and gives you a new direction.
- **Point 4:** The voice that tells you to quit is not the voice of God --- hold your integrity like Job, and the Redeemer will reveal Himself.
- **Point 5:** God turned the prophet's deepest grief into the foundation of the greatest ministry of this age --- He will do the same with your pain.
- **Point 6:** You are delivered through the Message of your day --- when you recognize the angel, the chains fall off.
- **Point 7:** The squeeze is your Dark Saturday, but Sunday is coming --- the angel is descending, the stone is rolling, and the Bride is going home.

> **The heart of this message:** Saturday always feels like the end, but in the economy of God, it is simply the eve of resurrection. Hold on, Bride --- Sunday is coming.

  **"Predestinated Unto Adoption" — What It Means (Ephesians 1:5)**  
  Brother Branham teaches two distinct but connected things in this phrase:                                             
  ---
  1. Predestinated — Written Before the World Began

  ▎ "Before the world ever began... before the foundation of the world we were predestined unto the adoption of sons...
  an anchor to the soul that's not tossed about here and there but anchored in Christ."
  ▎ — 1961-06-11 Revelation, Chapter 5, part 1 ¶41

  The predestination part means God chose His children before creation — not because of anything they would do, but by
  His foreknowledge and sovereign will.

  ---
  2. Adoption — NOT What We Think It Means in English

  This is the key revelation. In English, "adoption" means taking in a child from outside the family. But in the Bible,
  the Greek word is huiothesia — the placing of a son. It is a Roman legal custom applied to a child already born in the
   family:

  ▎ "A father took his son out into a public place, placed a robe upon him — a royal robe — and they had a ceremony. It
  was the ceremony of adoption. The child that had been born into the family, become placed by adoption in the family.
  And after that, that boy's name was just as good on a check as his daddy's was."
  ▎ — 1958-01-26 Hear Ye Him ¶24

  ▎ "He called witnesses out into the street, and he took this boy and put a special robe on him, a beautiful robe, and
  set him out in the street, and had a ceremony of adoption. And the father, the very father of this boy, adopted the
  boy into his own family... from that day on, that boy had a legal right to write his..." (checks on his father's
  account)
  ▎ — 1955-11-11 Where I Think Pentecost Failed ¶57

  ---
  So the Process Has Two Steps:

  
  |       Step       |                      Event                      |         Parallel in Christianity          |
  |------------------|-------------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------|
  | Birth            | Born into the family                            | New birth / receiving the Holy Ghost      |
  | Adoption / Placing | Publicly positioned in the family with full authority | Manifestation as a mature son — the third  pull |
  
  

  ▎ "We are predestinated unto the adoption of children... We're born in the Spirit of God, sure, receive the Holy
  Ghost, and crying, 'Abba, Father!'... That's right, we're children, but we can't get nowhere. We can't whip the
  devil..."
  ▎ — 1960-05-18 Adoption, part 2: Manifested Sons of God ¶107

  Being born again gives you life in the family. Adoption gives you authority in the family.

  ---
  God Did This to His Own Son First

  ▎ "God done the same thing to His Son: took Him apart and put Him up on the mount, transfigured Him before Peter,
  James, and John... And God adopted His Own Son before three witnesses — 'This is my beloved Son, hear ye Him.'"
  ▎ — 1956-02-24 Jehovah-Jireh ¶34

  The transfiguration was Jesus' public adoption ceremony. The Father put the robe of glory on Him before witnesses and
  declared His position.

  ---
  The Condition — Not Every Son Gets Placed

  ▎ "If the child was reckless, and never paid any attention, and wasn't so interested in the Father's work, that child
  never became adopted... But if he was a correct child, a good child, and loved the work of his father... then the
  tutor brought that word to the father, and there was what's called the placing of the son."
  ▎ — 1955-10-06 A Hidden Life ¶22

  ---
  The End-Time Application

  ▎ "Positionally placing his son, getting him into order... He's just as much authority — his word's just as good as an
   archangel's — better. This son was adopted, put on a high place, set out there, changed his robes, changed his
  colors. The Father had a ceremony."
  ▎ — 1960-05-22 Adoption, part 3: Position in Christ ¶53

  Brother Branham taught that the manifestation of the sons of God (Romans 8:19 — "the earnest expectation of the
  creature waiteth for the manifestation of the sons of God") is the end-time fulfillment of this adoption — when God
  publicly places His Bride in position with full authority before the rapture.

  ---
  In summary: Predestinated unto adoption means God foreordained before the world began that His children would not only
   be born into His family through the new birth, but would also be publicly placed and positioned as fully-authorized
  sons — with the same authority as the Father Himself — through a process of growth, obedience, and the manifestation
  of His Spirit in their lives.
