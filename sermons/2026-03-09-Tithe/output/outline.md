## THIS HOUR -- PROPHETIC ANCHOR

In this late hour, the Bride of Christ is being separated from the systems of this world. Everything that is not rooted in the Word is being shaken. The tithe is not a denominational program or a fundraising method -- it is a divine ordinance that predates the Law and continues under grace. When the Bride walks in the full light of the Word, she walks in obedience in every area, including the stewardship of her increase. God is restoring all things back to their original order, and the order of giving is no exception. The same God who met Abraham after the battle is the same God who meets His Bride today -- and He is still worthy of the tenth.

---

## INTRODUCTION -- SETTING THE TYPE

**Tone:** Warm and pastoral -- open the heart before opening the Word.

### The Human Scene (Humanistic)

There is hardly a subject in the church that produces more discomfort than money. People shift in their seats. They think of their bills, their debts, the cost of living. Some wonder, "Does God really need my money?" Others carry guilt because they have not been faithful. And still others have been so wounded by religious money-grabbing that they cannot hear the word "tithe" without suspicion. We must come to this subject honestly, gently, and with the Word open -- not to pressure anyone, but to show them what God actually said and why He said it. This is not about money. This is about lordship. This is about who owns what.

### The Type Established

- **Shadow (OT):** Abraham paying tithes to Melchisedec -- a priest who had no father, no mother, no beginning of days or ending of life -- after the slaughter of the kings (Genesis 14)
- **Substance (NT/End-time):** The Bride of Christ bringing her worship and her substance to the eternal High Priest, Jesus Christ, who ever liveth -- not under compulsion of the Law, but out of revelation and gratitude after the victory
- **Branham on this type:**
  > "Now, this Melchisedec, the King of Salem, Priest of the Most High, met Abraham and blessed him. And gave Him this tithing, Abraham did. And He was the King of Salem. And He brought out bread and wine, the communion, and gave it to Abraham after the battle (after the man had been won over)."
  > *-- 1957-09-15 -- Hebrews, Chapter 7, part 1 -- p47*

### Person Profile: Abraham

- **Visible need:** He had just fought a war to rescue Lot. He was weary from battle, carrying the spoils of victory.
- **Invisible need:** A recognition that the victory was not his own -- that God had delivered his enemies into his hand.
- **Motive:** Not obligation. Not law. Not compulsion. There was no commandment yet. Abraham gave because he recognized who Melchisedec was.
- **Intention:** To honour the One who was greater than himself -- to acknowledge that all he had came from God.
- **What God saw:** A man of faith. A man who understood, by revelation, that the tenth belonged to the One who gave the whole. God saw in Abraham the father of all who walk by faith -- and that included paying tithes.

## POINT 1 -- WHAT IS THE TITHE? THE TENTH BELONGS TO GOD

**Key Scripture:** "And all the tithe of the land, whether of the seed of the land, or of the fruit of the tree, is the LORD'S: it is holy unto the LORD." -- Leviticus 27:30

### The Human Need (Humanistic)

- Many believers do not tithe because they do not understand what the tithe actually is. They think of it as a donation, a gift, or an optional contribution.
- The struggle is real -- paychecks are tight, bills pile up, and the idea of giving away ten percent feels like a sacrifice they cannot afford.
- But the question is not "Can I afford to give?" The question is "Do I understand what already belongs to God?"

### Divine Revelation

**The Word says:**
> "And all the tithe of the land, whether of the seed of the land, or of the fruit of the tree, is the LORD'S: it is holy unto the LORD." -- Leviticus 27:30

**The type unfolds:**
The tithe is not a gift. It is a return. When a farmer harvested ten bushels of grain, one of those bushels was never his to begin with -- it was the Lord's. When a shepherd ran his flock through the counting gate, the tenth animal -- whether fat or lean, large or small -- belonged to God. The tithe is not generosity; it is honesty. It is the acknowledgment that God is the source of all increase. To withhold the tithe is not to be frugal -- it is to keep what belongs to another.

**From the Message:**
> "When God was setting in order, He said, 'All you Levites, I've called you out and made you priests. And all your brethren, the twelve tribes ... the other eleven tribes will pay you a tithing.' 'When you get nine bushels of apples, pour one bushel to the Levites. When you run your sheep through the hall here, pick up the tenth sheep. I don't care if it's a little one, or a big one, fat one, or poor one; that belongs to the Levites.'"
> *-- 1959-12-16 -- What is the Holy Ghost? -- p63*

**Sub-points:**
- The tithe is a tenth -- not five percent, not whatever is left over, but one-tenth of your increase
- The tithe is "holy unto the LORD" -- it is set apart; it is sacred property
- The tithe is not negotiable -- "I don't care if it's a little one, or a big one, fat one, or poor one; that belongs to the Levites"
- The difference between tithe and offering: the tithe is owed; the offering is given freely above and beyond the tithe

### THUS SAITH THE LORD -- The Vision

The Bride in this hour is coming back to divine order. We are not under a system of religious obligation -- we are under revelation. And revelation shows us that the tenth has always belonged to God. It belonged to Him before the Law was given, it belonged to Him under the Law, and it belongs to Him now. When you understand that you are only a steward of what He has provided, the tithe is no longer a burden -- it is an act of worship. The Bride gives not because she must, but because she sees.

## POINT 2 -- BEFORE THE LAW: ABRAHAM AND MELCHISEDEC

**Key Scripture:** "And blessed be the most high God, which hath delivered thine enemies into thy hand. And he gave him tithes of all." -- Genesis 14:20

### The Human Need (Humanistic)

- The most common objection to tithing is this: "That's Old Testament law. We're under grace now." People use this as a reason to close their wallets and quiet their conscience.
- But what if the tithe did not begin with the Law? What if it existed four hundred years before Moses ever stood on Sinai?
- If we can establish that the tithe predates the Law, then we cannot dismiss it as merely a legal requirement that has been fulfilled.

### Divine Revelation

**The Word says:**
> "And Melchizedek king of Salem brought forth bread and wine: and he was the priest of the most high God. And he blessed him, and said, Blessed be Abram of the most high God, possessor of heaven and earth: And blessed be the most high God, which hath delivered thine enemies into thy hand. And he gave him tithes of all." -- Genesis 14:18-20

**The type unfolds:**
Abraham had no Law commanding him to tithe. Moses had not yet been born. The Levitical priesthood did not exist. And yet, after God gave him the victory over the kings, Abraham met Melchisedec -- a priest of the Most High God, without father, without mother, without beginning of days or ending of life -- and he gave Him tithes of all. This was not law. This was revelation. Abraham recognized in Melchisedec someone greater than himself, and he responded with a tenth of everything. The tithe was born in faith, not in commandment. It was a response of the heart, not a requirement of the statute.

**From the Message:**
> "Tithing is right. Every Christian is duty bound to pay tithe. That's right. Never has been changed!"
> *-- 1957-09-15 -- Hebrews, Chapter 7, part 1 -- p64*

> "Say it's an Old Testament ordinance? I'd like you to prove it to me."
> *-- 1961-03-16 -- The Church choosing Law for Grace -- p31*

**Sub-points:**
- Abraham tithed approximately 430 years before the Law of Moses was given
- The tithe was an act of faith and worship, not legal compliance
- Melchisedec is a type of Christ -- "without father, without mother, without descent, having neither beginning of days, nor end of life; but made like unto the Son of God; abideth a priest continually" (Hebrews 7:3)
- If Abraham, the father of faith, paid tithes, then all who walk by the faith of Abraham follow the same pattern
- Pre-tax or post-tax? Abraham gave "tithes of all" -- he did not deduct his expenses first

### THUS SAITH THE LORD -- The Vision

Church, the tithe did not begin with the Law and it did not end with the Law. It began in the heart of a man who had a revelation of who God is. And it continues today in the heart of every believer who has the same revelation. The Bride of Christ is not under bondage to the Law -- she is free. But her freedom does not cancel her recognition of lordship. She gives because she knows who gave first. She tithes because she walks in the faith of Abraham.

## POINT 3 -- WHERE DOES THE TITHE GO? THE STOREHOUSE PRINCIPLE

**Key Scripture:** "Bring ye all the tithes into the storehouse, that there may be meat in mine house, and prove me now herewith, saith the LORD of hosts, if I will not open you the windows of heaven, and pour you out a blessing, that there shall not be room enough to receive it." -- Malachi 3:10

### The Human Need (Humanistic)

- "Can I give my tithe to a charity?" "Can I split it between the church and a missionary?" "What about helping a widow with my tithe?" These are sincere questions, and they come from a good heart. But sincerity is not enough -- we must follow the pattern.
- People sometimes want to direct their tithe to where they can see the result, where it feels most satisfying. But God said to bring it to a specific place.

### Divine Revelation

**The Word says:**
> "Bring ye all the tithes into the storehouse, that there may be meat in mine house." -- Malachi 3:10

**The type unfolds:**
The storehouse is the place where you receive your spiritual food. In the Old Testament, the tithes were brought to the temple -- to the Levites, the priests who ministered in the things of God. The tithe supported the ministry. It was God's system for sustaining those who gave their lives to His service. The storehouse was not a charity, not a building fund, not a benevolence account -- it was the place of ministry, the place of feeding.

**From the Message:**
> "Really, where you owe tithings is where you get your food. 'Bring ye all your tithes into the storehouse.' And the storehouse, you surely know what that is, it's where you get your food. So, that's where you get your spiritual food, and your obligation is to your church."
> *-- 1959-11-29 -- Let us see God -- p12*

> "I know people take their tithes and give them to a widow woman. That's wrong. If you've got anything to give the widow woman, give her, but don't give her God's tithe."
> *-- 1961-10-15 -- Questions and Answers -- p85*

**Sub-points:**
- The storehouse is where you receive your spiritual food -- your local church, your pastor
- Tithes go to support the ministry: "Tithes pays the preacher. He's to live by the tithes of the people." (1961-03-16, The Church choosing Law for Grace, p31)
- Building funds, benevolence, and charity are separate -- paid from offerings, not from the tithe
- In the Old Testament, a separate box was set at the door for building repairs; the tithe went to the priest
- Helping the poor is a Christian duty -- but it comes from your offering, not from God's tithe
- When during the month? The principle is firstfruits -- when the increase comes in, the first portion belongs to God, not the last

### THUS SAITH THE LORD -- The Vision

God has placed pastors in the Body to feed the flock. That is His order. And the tithe is His provision for that order. When you bring your tithe to the storehouse -- to the place where you are fed the Word -- you are not just paying a bill. You are participating in God's divine economy. You are sustaining the ministry of the Word in your local assembly. And God says, "Prove Me." Test Me. See if I will not open the windows of heaven. That is not a promise made to a denomination -- that is a promise made to you, personally, in this hour.

## POINT 4 -- THE TITHE AND THE OFFERING: TWO DIFFERENT THINGS

**Key Scripture:** "Will a man rob God? Yet ye have robbed me. But ye say, Wherein have we robbed thee? In tithes and offerings." -- Malachi 3:8

### The Human Need (Humanistic)

- Many believers use the words "tithe" and "offering" interchangeably, as if they mean the same thing. They do not.
- Some give an offering and think they have tithed. Others tithe and think they have given an offering. But the scripture names both -- separately.
- Understanding the difference brings clarity and freedom.

### Divine Revelation

**The Word says:**
> "Will a man rob God? Yet ye have robbed me. But ye say, Wherein have we robbed thee? In tithes and offerings." -- Malachi 3:8

**The type unfolds:**
In the Levitical system, the tithe was the required tenth. It was non-negotiable. It was owed. But the offerings were something else entirely -- wave offerings, heave offerings, freewill offerings. The offerings were above and beyond the tithe. They were expressions of gratitude, worship, and love. A man could give lavishly in his offerings, but he still owed his tithe. And a man could pay his tithe faithfully, but if he brought no offering, he was still short of what God desired. God asks for both -- the tithe which is His by right, and the offering which is yours to give by love.

**From the Message:**
> "Now, Levites, when you get all this, then you tithe too to the Lord. You make the wave-offering, the heave-offering, the different offerings. You tithe to the Lord."
> *-- 1959-12-16 -- What is the Holy Ghost? -- p63*

**Sub-points:**
- The tithe is the tenth -- it is owed; it is God's property returned to Him
- The offering is above and beyond -- it is voluntary, given out of love and gratitude
- God says we rob Him in both "tithes AND offerings" -- not one or the other
- The Levites themselves tithed on what they received -- even the ministers gave a tenth to the Lord
- A freewill offering reflects the condition of the heart; the tithe reflects obedience to the Word

### THUS SAITH THE LORD -- The Vision

Bride of Christ, do not confuse the two. Your tithe is what you owe. Your offering is what you love. Both are worship. Both are required. And both reveal where your heart truly is. "For where your treasure is, there will your heart be also" (Matthew 6:21). In this hour, God is looking for a people who will walk in the full counsel of His Word -- not picking and choosing, but obeying in every area. The tithe and the offering together are the full expression of a surrendered life.

## POINT 5 -- LEVI PAID TITHES IN ABRAHAM: THE PRINCIPLE OF SPIRITUAL SEED

**Key Scripture:** "And as I may so say, Levi also, who receiveth tithes, payed tithes in Abraham. For he was yet in the loins of his father, when Melchisedec met him." -- Hebrews 7:9-10

### The Human Need (Humanistic)

- Some may say, "That was Abraham. That was his decision. What does it have to do with me?"
- But the scripture reveals a principle that goes far deeper than personal choice: what the father does, the seed does. What is in the loins is already counted.
- This is both a warning and a comfort. What we do in faith echoes in our children and in the generations that follow.

### Divine Revelation

**The Word says:**
> "And as I may so say, Levi also, who receiveth tithes, payed tithes in Abraham. For he was yet in the loins of his father, when Melchisedec met him." -- Hebrews 7:9-10

**The type unfolds:**
Levi was Abraham's great-grandson. He had not yet been born. He had not yet drawn a breath, made a choice, or lifted a hand. And yet the Bible says -- not that he "would" pay tithes, not that he "potentially" paid tithes -- but that he "payed tithes in Abraham." Four generations before Levi ever walked the earth, his act of tithing was already credited to him because he was in the loins of a man of faith. This is the principle of the seed. What the father does in faith, the children carry forward.

**From the Message:**
> "Now, the Bible didn't say 'potentially' he paid it, the Bible said he 'paid tithes' when he was in his great-grandfather's loins. Glory! That's my Lord. Oh, He knowed it even before the world was ever formed. He knowed everything. And in Abraham he paid tithes."
> *-- 1958-09-27 -- Why are we not a denomination? -- p56*

> "Four generations before he ever come to earth, he was paying tithes to Melchisedec. And blessed be the name of the Lord! Then you that can't believe in predestination, foreordination and here, four generations before Levi would ever come out of the loins of Abraham, was paying tithes to Melchisedec."
> *-- 1957-09-15 -- Hebrews, Chapter 7, part 1 -- p65*

**Sub-points:**
- Levi paid tithes in Abraham -- not potentially, but actually, by God's accounting
- This reveals God's foreknowledge and the principle of the seed
- Your faithfulness in tithing is a testimony that passes to your children and your children's children
- Conversely, unfaithfulness also marks the generations -- "how can you run out here ... and what do you expect another generation to be?" (1958-09-27, p56)
- We who are the seed of Abraham by faith walk in the same pattern of faith -- including the tithe

### THUS SAITH THE LORD -- The Vision

Fathers, mothers -- what you do in faith today, your children will carry tomorrow. Levi paid tithes four generations before he was born because he was in the loins of a faithful man. You are setting a pattern right now. Your obedience is not just for you -- it is for the seed that comes after you. The Bride in this hour is not a single generation; she is a spiritual lineage, rooted in the faith of Abraham, walking in the light of this present Message. Walk in it faithfully.

## POINT 6 -- GOD'S CHALLENGE: "PROVE ME"

**Key Scripture:** "Prove me now herewith, saith the LORD of hosts, if I will not open you the windows of heaven, and pour you out a blessing, that there shall not be room enough to receive it." -- Malachi 3:10

### The Human Need (Humanistic)

- Fear is the enemy of obedience. People fear that if they tithe, they will not have enough. "What about my bills? What about my groceries? What about the unexpected expenses?"
- It is the same fear that has plagued God's people in every age: "If I give, will there be enough left?"
- But God does not ask us to tithe and then leave us on our own. He makes a promise. And more than a promise -- He issues a challenge.

### Divine Revelation

**The Word says:**
> "Prove me now herewith, saith the LORD of hosts, if I will not open you the windows of heaven, and pour you out a blessing, that there shall not be room enough to receive it." -- Malachi 3:10

**The type unfolds:**
This is the only place in all of scripture where God says, "Test Me." In every other area, He commands faith without conditions. But here, on the matter of the tithe, He says, "Go ahead. Try it. Prove Me. See if I will not do what I said." The windows of heaven are not opened by our cleverness or our budgeting -- they are opened by obedience. God connects material faithfulness to spiritual blessing. Not because He needs money, but because He is testing the heart. Where your treasure is, there your heart is also.

**From the Message:**
> "That's a challenge to any individual. And if I only had the time, and would not get to this little scripture that I wish to get to in about the next ten minutes, I would like to give you a personal testimony of how that works."
> *-- 1961-07-23 -- Questions and Answers (God being misunderstood) -- p49*

> "I kept ten percent and give God ninety. And then, when the law told me I couldn't do that ... I pay my tithes from that. Yes, sir! I believe in paying tithes. It's one of God's blessings that's ..."
> *-- 1964-08-23 -- Questions and Answers, Part 2 -- p92*

**Sub-points:**
- This is the only place in scripture where God invites man to test Him
- The promise is not vague -- "a blessing that there shall not be room enough to receive it"
- Brother Branham's own testimony: he gave ninety percent and kept ten -- and God always provided
- The blessing is not always financial; God opens windows of revelation, health, peace, and provision
- The fear of tithing is really the fear of trusting God -- and God says, "Prove Me"

### THUS SAITH THE LORD -- The Vision

Church, God is not asking you to gamble. He is asking you to obey -- and then watch Him work. He has staked His own Name on this promise. "Prove Me," He says. That is not a suggestion from a distant deity. That is a challenge from a Father who wants to bless His children. In this hour, the Bride does not live by fear -- she lives by faith. And faith is proven in the practical, everyday decisions of life. If you cannot trust God with ten percent, how will you trust Him with the rapture? Start where He tells you to start. Bring the tithe. Watch the windows open.

## CONCLUSION

### The Epiphany -- The Question Answered

> If tithing belonged to the Law, and the Law is fulfilled in Christ, then why should I still bring a tenth? What is the tithe, and to whom does it truly belong?
>
> The tithe never belonged to the Law -- it belonged to God. Abraham paid it four hundred years before Moses, and the eternal Priest, of whom it is witnessed that He liveth, still receives it today. You tithe not because you are under law, but because you have a revelation of who owns all things -- and you are walking in the faith of your father Abraham.

### Summary

- **Point 1:** The tithe is not a gift but a return -- the tenth has always been the Lord's, and it is holy.
- **Point 2:** Abraham tithed to Melchisedec four centuries before the Law, proving that the tithe is rooted in faith, not in legal obligation.
- **Point 3:** The storehouse is where you receive your spiritual food -- bring the tithe to the place where the Word feeds you.
- **Point 4:** The tithe is what you owe; the offering is what you love -- God asks for both, and both reveal the heart.
- **Point 5:** Levi paid tithes in Abraham's loins -- your faithfulness in this hour marks the generations after you.
- **Point 6:** God issues a challenge found nowhere else in scripture: "Prove Me" -- and He promises to open the windows of heaven.

> **The heart of this message:** The tithe is not about money -- it is about lordship. It is the practical, tangible acknowledgment that everything you have came from God, and a tenth of it was never yours to keep.

### A Closing Hymn

**Suggested hymn:** "I Surrender All"
**Why:** This hymn carries the exact spirit of the message -- a full yielding of everything to the Lord, including our material substance. Tithing is surrender made visible, and this hymn seals that truth in the heart.
